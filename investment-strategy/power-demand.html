Our High-Level Strategy & Rationale

The core investment thesis is that the exponential growth in AI computation is creating a generational surge in electricity consumption that the world's aging grid infrastructure cannot meet. The AI industry's growth will ultimately be paced not by Moore's Law, but by the linear, capital-intensive process of generating and transmitting electricity. This dynamic elevates the entire energy infrastructure value chain into the most durable "picks and shovels" investment of the AI era, with a 5- to 10-year investment horizon.

  • Generational Demand Shock: AI-driven data center power demand is projected to more than double by 2030, a rate of growth not seen in decades.
  • Physical Infrastructure Bottleneck: The existing grid is ill-equipped for this new load, creating non-negotiable demand for modernization, new generation, and transmission assets.

Timelines & Execution

Deployment (2025-2027): Deploy capital into core grid modernization and on-site power solutions to capture the immediate build-out phase.

Maturation (2028-2032): Realize gains as the market matures, new utility generation comes online, and next-generation technologies like SMRs move toward commercialization.

The Three Pillars: A Tiered Approach to the Value Chain

Grid Modernization

Core Holding

The "picks and shovels" play. Investing in suppliers of critical hardware (transformers, switchgear) needed to upgrade an aging grid facing unprecedented strain.

Regulated Utilities

Tactical Holding

Targeting select utilities deploying billions in rate-based capital to build new generation and transmission to serve data center hubs.

On-Site Generation

Core / Venture

Focusing on technologies (fuel cells, SMRs) that allow data centers to bypass grid constraints and secure "speed-to-power."

Prime Investment Candidates

Grid Modernization

Eaton (NYSE:ETN)

A diversified power management leader providing the essential "grid-to-chip" hardware for the AI build-out.

Investment Thesis:

Eaton is a dual beneficiary, profiting from both data center construction and the broader grid upgrades required to support them. Its portfolio of switchgear, UPS systems, and power distribution is fundamental to the entire thematic. As a mature, profitable leader with a record backlog, it represents the lowest-risk, core exposure to the AI power demand trend.

On-Site Power (Core)

Bloom Energy (NYSE:BE)

A pure-play on rapidly deployable on-site power, solving the critical "speed-to-power" problem for data centers.

Investment Thesis:

Bloom directly addresses the primary bottleneck for developers: multi-year grid connection delays. Its fuel cells can be deployed in months, offering a crucial bridge solution. The $5B Brookfield partnership validates its role as a primary, bankable power source for AI factories. It offers higher growth potential by solving the most immediate pain point in the value chain.

Tactical Utility Play

Select Utilities (e.g. DUK)

Incumbents positioned for significant rate-base growth by building generation and transmission for data centers.

Investment Thesis:

Regulated utilities are direct beneficiaries of the demand surge, with approved capital plans to build assets and earn a protected return. This is not a broad "buy all utilities" thesis but a tactical play on specific companies in favorable regulatory jurisdictions with large, confirmed data center projects in their service territories, offering stable growth and dividend income.

On-Site Power (Venture)

NuScale Power (NYSE:SMR)

A venture-style bet on the long-term, holy grail solution for powering gigawatt-scale AI campuses.

Investment Thesis:

SMRs offer the potential for 24/7, carbon-free baseload power. NuScale has a first-mover advantage with the only NRC-certified design. While pre-commercial and high-risk, a small allocation provides exposure to the transformative, long-duration upside if the technology achieves commercial viability. It is a bet on the ultimate endgame for data center power.

The Bear Scenario: 15% Probability (The "Demand Mirage")

Assumptions:This scenario materializes if enterprise AI monetization fails to scale (current 95% failure rate persists) and/or efficiency gains in AI hardware dramatically reduce power needs. This leads to high rates of data center project cancellations, creating a "demand mirage." Utilities are left with stranded assets, grid modernization spending slows, and demand for on-site power stalls. This represents a broad-based de-rating of the entire thematic.

The Neutral Scenario: 55% Probability (The Base Case)

Assumptions:This scenario reflects the consensus view where AI adoption continues apace, driving a sustained 15% CAGR in data center power demand through 2030. Grid connection delays remain a key bottleneck, creating strong demand for both grid hardware and on-site solutions. Utilities execute on their announced capital plans, driving steady rate-base growth. This is the "muddle through" scenario where the physical constraints create durable, long-term growth for all segments.

The Bull Scenario: 30% Probability (Exponential Acceleration)

Assumptions:This scenario assumes AI demand grows exponentially, exceeding even the most optimistic forecasts. Severe grid constraints and transformer shortages become acute, forcing a massive, accelerated shift to on-site generation. Hyperscalers sign large, multi-GW contracts for fuel cells as a primary power source and make firm financial commitments to first-of-a-kind SMR deployments to secure their long-term power needs, dramatically de-risking the technology roadmap.

Navigating the Headwinds: Key Risks

Regulatory & Permitting Risk

The single greatest impediment. Multi-year delays for grid projects and emerging political backlash against data centers create significant uncertainty for utilities and developers, potentially stalling the entire thematic.

Execution & Supply Chain Risk

A global shortage of critical components like high-voltage transformers, with lead times of 2-4 years, physically gates the pace of the build-out. Skilled labor shortages further inflate costs and extend project timelines.

Market & Demand Risk

The risk of a "demand mirage" is real. If AI fails to be widely monetized by enterprises, projected power demand may not materialize, leading to overbuilding and a wave of stranded assets across the value chain.

Technology & Adoption Risk

Next-gen solutions carry significant hurdles. SMRs face immense first-of-a-kind construction and cost risks, while fuel cells' economics are currently tied to volatile natural gas prices and fossil fuel infrastructure.
