Strategy Overview

Positioning for the Industrial Upcycle

A diversified industrials strategy capturing structural tailwinds in reindustrialisation, infrastructure, defence & aerospace, automation & robotics, and transportation & supply chains via targeted ETFs.

Universe: US-listed industrial thematic ETFs
Instruments: XLI, ITA, IYT, BOTZ
Horizon: 1–3 years
Approach: Core + satellites (thematic tilts)

Core Investment Thesis

Multi-tailwind upcycle

The industrial sector is positioned for a broad-based upcycle supported by multiple structural drivers. The strategy targets a 1–3 year horizon, using near-term volatility (growth scares, rate-driven sell-offs) as an entry point while aiming to capture a mid-cycle acceleration in industrial activity.

High-Level Strategy & Rationale

  • Reindustrialisation & infrastructure: reshoring and public investment lift demand for industrial goods and services.
  • Defence & aerospace demand: modernisation and geopolitical dynamics support sustained order books.
  • Automation & robotics: labour constraints and productivity initiatives accelerate adoption of robotics and AI.
  • Transportation & supply chains: e-commerce and network reconfiguration support logistics and transport volumes.

Timelines & Execution

  • Deployment (Late 2025 – Early 2026): build positions gradually (DCA), leaning into pullbacks.
  • Acceleration (Mid 2026 – 2027): capture upside as activity lifts; robotics benefits from capex, transports from volume recovery, defence from budget upcycles. Illustrative value change range: ~+6% to +30% over a 12-month window (base-to-bull), with downside risk ~−15% to −25% in a bear regime.
  • Evaluation (2028+): monitor late-cycle signals/valuation; rebalance or trim if drivers fade or the cycle peaks. Illustrative incremental upside range: ~0% to +10% annualised if the upcycle persists, with drawdown risk increasing to ~−10% to −20% if conditions turn risk-off.

ETF Descriptions & Rationale

Sub-theme coverage

Each ETF is selected to express a distinct driver of the industrial upcycle.

XLI

Core

Broad-based US industrials exposure across machinery, conglomerates, aerospace and transportation.

Rationale

Anchor position to capture the general industrial trend with lower idiosyncratic risk.

ITA

Satellite

Exposure to aerospace and defence primes and suppliers.

Rationale

Targets sustained defence spending and potential commercial aerospace rebound.

IYT

Satellite

US transportation and logistics (rail, airlines, trucking, delivery).

Rationale

Expresses cyclical recovery in volumes and pricing power as supply chains normalise.

BOTZ

Satellite

Robotics and AI exposure tied to automation in manufacturing and logistics.

Rationale

Captures secular automation tailwind; higher sensitivity to capex cycles and risk sentiment.

Key Risks & Intended Use by a Model

Cyclical exposure

Key risks

Risk Channel
Macroeconomic downturn Recession/credit stress impacts cyclical industrial earnings; transports and machinery can draw down sharply.
Inflation & rates Persistently high rates raise financing costs, dampen capex, and compress equity multiples.
Geopolitical / policy shifts Changes to defence budgets or infrastructure programs reduce tailwinds; escalation can disrupt supply chains.
Technology / competitive risk For BOTZ, tech winners/losers can diverge; adoption timing and monetisation risk remain material.
Sector concentration All holdings can correlate during risk-off events; consider sizing and hedges within a broader portfolio.
