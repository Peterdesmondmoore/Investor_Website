The Quantum Imperative: Rationale for a Pure-Play Strategy

The quantum computing industry is at a critical inflection point, transitioning from theoretical research to demonstrable commercial value‚Äîa milestone known as "Quantum Advantage". This transition presents a rare opportunity for asymmetric, venture-scale returns that are largely uncorrelated with the broader technology market. Our thesis is that a focused investment in pure-play hardware pioneers is the optimal way to capture this upside.

  • Validation Trigger: Achieving "Quantum Advantage"‚Äîsolving a commercially relevant problem better than a classical supercomputer‚Äîwill act as a powerful de-risking signal. This is expected to trigger a substantial, non-linear re-rating of valuations as a new class of enterprise customers and institutional investors moves from observation to active participation.
  • Undiluted Exposure: While tech giants like Google and IBM are major players, their scale dilutes the financial impact of a quantum breakthrough. In contrast, pure-play companies offer direct, undiluted exposure to the quantum thesis, where success is tied directly to industry progress, offering the potential for multi-bagger returns.

This dynamic creates a high-probability scenario for significant value creation. Our strategy is designed to capitalize on this long-term trend by investing across the primary technological modalities, targeting a 7-10 year holding period.

Timelines & Execution

Deployment (2026): The full capital allocation will be deployed at the start of 2026. Current valuations do not fully price in the non-linear upside of a near-term "Quantum Advantage" event, which could trigger a rapid and significant market re-rating.

Drawdown (7-10 Years): The full value is expected to be realized once fault-tolerant systems generate significant, recurring revenue, projected for the early 2030s. The drawdown will be considered as the full thesis matures over the 7-10 year horizon.

Prime Investment Candidates

A closer look at the curated selection of pure-play hardware pioneers that form our portfolio.

The Trapped-Ion Leader

IonQ (NYSE: IONQ)

A direct bet on the preeminent pure-play pursuing what is arguably the most promising modality for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

Investment Thesis

Technical leadership in qubit quality, a formidable balance sheet, and strategic M&A combine to position IonQ as the most likely pure-play to survive and emerge as a long-term market leader.

The Commercial Annealing Pioneer

D-Wave Quantum (NYSE: QBTS)

The most de-risked pure-play from a commercialization standpoint, using specialized annealers to solve high-value optimization problems today.

Investment Thesis

Its "value today" proposition provides a clear path to near-term revenue, which can fund long-term R&D in universal gate-based systems with less reliance on dilutive financing.

The Superconducting Full-Stack Player

Rigetti Computing (NASDAQ: RGTI)

A leveraged bet on the eventual dominance of the superconducting modality, differentiated by its vertically integrated, in-house chip manufacturing.

Investment Thesis

Owning the full manufacturing stack could enable higher margins and position Rigetti as a strategic "picks-and-shovels" infrastructure supplier to the entire quantum ecosystem.

The High-Risk Photonic Innovator

Quantum Computing Inc. (NASDAQ: QUBT)

A high-risk, high-reward speculative allocation on a potentially disruptive technological path: scalable, room-temperature quantum computing.

Investment Thesis

A breakthrough would be a game-changer, dramatically lowering deployment barriers. This "moonshot" vision is underwritten by a massive capital raise, providing a multi-year window for execution.

Navigating the Headwinds: Key Risks to Monitor

Effective management requires a disciplined framework that focuses on fundamental shifts, rather than on short-term market sentiment.

Technical & Scientific Risk

A breakthrough in a non-portfolio modality (e.g., neutral atoms), stagnation in error correction, or discovery of fundamental scalability roadblocks could render a chosen technology obsolete.

Commercial & Market Risk

A significant delay (over 24 months) in demonstrating "Quantum Advantage" could trigger a "Quantum Winter," causing a sharp decline in investor sentiment and delayed enterprise adoption.

Financial & Execution Risk

High cash burn rates, the need for highly dilutive financing at depressed valuations, or repeated slips in roadmap timelines raise significant solvency risks for pre-revenue companies.

Geopolitical Risk

US-China export controls on critical technologies, shifts in government funding, or the nationalization of quantum assets could disrupt supply chains and R&D roadmaps for key players.
