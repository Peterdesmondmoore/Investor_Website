Our High-Level Strategy & Rationale

The core investment thesis is that a powerful, long-term, and durable investment theme is emerging from the foundational physical and digital infrastructure required for the AI revolution. These are the "picks and shovels" plays that are essential for the Bull Case to materialize but represent strong secular growth trends even in the more probable Neutral scenario. This dynamic creates a compelling entry point for long-term investors targeting a 5- to 10-year investment horizon.

  • Foundational Growth Theses: Long-term, high-conviction investments in the indispensable infrastructure of the AI revolution. The return hypothesis is for strong, secular capital appreciation over a multi-year horizon.
  • Tactical & Hedging Theses: Short-term, catalyst-driven opportunities designed to profit from or hedge against the significant tail risk of a sharp market correction.

Timelines & Execution

Deployment (2025-2026): Deploy capital into foundational growth assets, leveraging the current supply-side, infrastructure-driven phenomenon of the AI boom.

Rebalancing (2027-2029): As the market matures, rebalance towards defensive positions and consider tactical hedges if leading indicators suggest an imminent downturn.

Prime Investment Candidates

Foundational Growth

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSM)

As the world's largest and most advanced semiconductor foundry, TSMC is an indispensable partner, manufacturing the complex chips designed by nearly every major AI player.

Investment Thesis:

Provides broad exposure to the entire AI theme without the need to bet on a single winning AI model or application. It's a "picks and shovels" play that is essential for the Bull Case to materialize but represents a strong secular growth trend even in the Neutral scenario.

Foundational Growth

Advanced Micro Devices (AMD)

Represents the most credible challenger to Nvidia's dominance in the GPU market, with its MI300 series of accelerators gaining traction among hyperscalers.

Investment Thesis:

A tactical play on the diversification of the AI accelerator market. As hyperscalers seek to reduce their dependence on a single supplier, AMD is well-positioned to capture a significant share of the growing demand for AI chips.

Foundational Growth

Broadcom Inc. (AVGO)

A leader in high-speed networking hardware and the design of custom AI chips (ASICs) for tech giants like Google and Meta.

Investment Thesis:

Offers exposure to two critical and often overlooked bottlenecks in the AI infrastructure build-out: the need for high-speed connectivity between GPUs and the growing demand for custom-designed AI accelerators.

Tactical & Hedging

ProShares UltraPro Short QQQ (SQQQ)

An inverse ETF designed to profit from a decline in the NASDAQ-100 index, the epicenter of the AI boom.

Investment Thesis:

A tactical tool to hedge against a sharp market downturn as described in the Bear Case. This is not a long-term holding but a catalyst-driven trade to be deployed when leading indicators suggest a high probability of an imminent correction.

The Bear Scenario: 25-35% Probability (A Tail Risk)

Assumptions:This scenario is defined by a sharp, systemic correction driven by a failure to meet lofty profit expectations, technological disillusionment as limitations become apparent, or a macroeconomic shock that triggers a liquidity crisis. A collapse in the valuations of leading AI firms would create a contagion effect, dragging down the broader market.

Probability Rationale:The 25-35% probability reflects this scenario's status as a "tail risk." The combination of historically stretched valuations, questionable financing practices, and the market's entry into the "Trough of Disillusionment" creates a fragile environment. A sharp correction could easily be triggered by a macroeconomic shock, a significant geopolitical event, or a major technological disappointment that shatters the prevailing narrative.

The Neutral Scenario: 40-50% Probability (The Base Case)

Assumptions:This scenario represents a market correction rather than a catastrophic crash. This "soft landing" would involve a significant deflation of the most speculative segments of the AI market, a painful shakeout among unprofitable startups, and a fundamental re-orientation of capital towards companies with demonstrable profitability and sustainable business models.

Probability Rationale:The 40-50% probability is anchored in the powerful financial foundations of the market's leaders, which are highly profitable, cash-rich incumbents. This provides a buffer against a full-scale systemic collapse. However, the speculative froth in the broader AI startup ecosystem makes a correction and consolidation almost inevitable. This scenario represents a necessary rebalancing that will wash out the excess and set the stage for a more sustainable growth phase.

The Bull Scenario: 20-30% Probability (A Significant Possibility)

Assumptions:This scenario assumes a sustained, long-term growth cycle where current valuations are ultimately justified by a fundamental, AI-driven productivity supercycle. In this outcome, AI is correctly identified as a General-Purpose Technology on par with electricity or the internet, unlocking trillions of dollars in economic value.

Probability Rationale:The 20-30% probability is justified by the growing body of firm-level evidence demonstrating significant, real-world productivity gains. The probability of this scenario playing out in the medium term is lower, as the "J-curve" of adoption suggests that the widespread, aggregate productivity gains needed to validate current valuations will take more time to materialize. However, the probability of the Bull case increases significantly as the time horizon extends beyond three years.

Navigating the Headwinds: Key Risks

Systemic Market Correction

A sharp, systemic correction driven by a failure to meet lofty profit expectations, technological disillusionment, or a macroeconomic shock that triggers a liquidity crisis remains the most significant threat.

Geopolitical & Supply Chain Disruptions

The AI hardware supply chain is a key vulnerability. A geopolitical event, such as China implementing its proposed export controls on rare earth minerals, could act as a direct and immediate trigger for a market downturn.

Technological Disillusionment

The initial euphoria surrounding generative AI is giving way to a more sober assessment of its current limitations. Persistent problems like "hallucinations" and a plateau in model improvements could undermine the narrative of exponential progress that justifies current valuations.

Circular Financing Unwinding

A market downturn could brutally expose the fragility of the circular financing arrangements that have propped up valuations. Companies that had booked massive revenues from these interdependent deals would be forced to issue massive write-downs as the value of their investments in their "customers" collapsed.
