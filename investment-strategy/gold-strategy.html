Strategy Overview

Gold as a Late-Cycle Hedge in a High-Valuation Regime

A "Core + Tactical Overlay" strategy that treats gold primarily as a strategic hedge and volatility buffer, while explicitly managing valuation compression risk after a major price re-rating.

Universe: Spot gold, allocated bullion, gold ETFs, listed derivatives
Instruments: XAUUSD, physical, GLD/IAU (examples), futures/options (overlay)
Horizons: 12 / 24 / 36 months
Structure: Core + Tactical overlay sleeves
As of: 10 Jan 2026 (baseline Nov 2025)
Spot gold: ~$4,430/oz

Core Investment Thesis

Hedge + regime risk

The strategy is rebased to reflect a late-cycle, high-valuation gold regime. Gold is treated as a strategic hedge against monetary disorder, geopolitical risk and policy error, with explicit recognition that near-term expected returns are lower after the 2025 re-rating.

Unchanged assumptions

  • Portfolio role: strategic diversifier and convexity sleeve under stress regimes.
  • Supply inelasticity: mine supply growth remains modest; structural supply does not rapidly expand with price.
  • Official sector support: central banks remain structurally biased to net purchases.

Strengthened assumptions

  • Regime risk: higher frequency of tail-risk events supports persistent risk premium.
  • Official demand band: central bank net purchases are modelled at 750–1,100 t/year.
  • ETF flow capacity: ETF flows are positive but remain below prior-cycle peaks.

Weakened assumptions

  • Jewelry volume: at current prices, jewelry demand is modelled at -15% to -25% YoY volume.
  • Return convexity from valuation: late-cycle valuation state increases downside sensitivity to real-rate upside.

Current phase

Risk-Managed Hold. The strategy emphasises maintaining strategic exposure while tightening drawdown controls and treating upside as scenario-dependent rather than trend-dependent.

"Core + Tactical Overlay" Structure

No leverage assumed

The sleeve is split into a core strategic exposure and a smaller overlay sleeve designed to adjust exposure and risk in response to scenario shifts.

Sleeves & indicative bands

Sleeve Description Indicative band
Core Strategic Allocated bullion or liquid ETF exposure representing long-term hedge intent. Designed for continuity across regimes. 70–90%
Tactical Overlay Overlay mechanisms (e.g., futures or options) used to manage convexity, volatility and drawdown sensitivity without changing the core thesis. 10–30%

Bands are expressed within the gold sleeve; total sleeve size is managed at the total portfolio level.

Scenario Framework & Market Logic

Valuation-sensitive

Three scenarios are defined with explicit probabilities and 12-month price ranges rebased to the current spot anchor.

Bear scenario — 20% probability

Assumptions: growth re-accelerates; inflation remains sticky; policy stays restrictive; real yields and USD strengthen. Demand: ETF flows weaken; jewelry remains constrained. Price path (12M): $3,500–$4,000.

Base scenario — 50% probability

Assumptions: soft landing or mild slowdown; gradual easing; elevated but stable risk premium. Demand: central bank purchases remain in the 750–1,100 t/year band; ETFs net positive. Price path (12M): $4,650–$5,000.

Bull scenario — 30% probability

Assumptions: recession or systemic stress; accelerated easing; falling real yields; higher risk aversion. Demand: ETF inflows increase; official sector remains net positive. Price path (12M): $5,000–$5,500.

Key Risks & Model Use

Real-rate risk

Risks are framed for monitoring and model integration, aligned to the rebased late-cycle posture.

Risk emphasis (updated)

Risk Transmission channel
Valuation compression Gold reprices lower if risk premium compresses and investor flows reverse.
Real-rate upside Higher sustained real yields raise opportunity cost and pressure price.
Regime reversal Strong growth + sticky inflation reduces hedge demand and supports USD.
Liquidity shock path dependency Short-term forced selling can precede later hedging demand rebound.

Model instructions (explicit)

  • Anchor modelling to spot: $4,430/oz.
  • Use scenario probabilities: Bear 20% / Base 50% / Bull 30%.
  • Use 12M price ranges: $3,500–$4,000 (Bear), $4,650–$5,000 (Base), $5,000–$5,500 (Bull).
  • Set volatility input: min 22% unless overridden by market data.
  • Treat return ranges as stress-test inputs; do not optimise solely on expected return.

Invalidation signals (hard)

Signal Threshold
10Y real yields > 2.5% sustained for 2 consecutive quarters
