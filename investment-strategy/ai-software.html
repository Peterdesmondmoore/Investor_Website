Our High-Level Strategy & Rationale

The core investment thesis addresses the immense chasm between the promise of AI and the reality of enterprise deployment. A landmark MIT study found that a staggering 95% of generative AI pilot programs fail to generate any ROI. This "last mile" crisis creates a compelling opportunity for the "Digital Scaffolding"‚Äîthe essential software layer that makes massive investments in AI commercially viable. Our strategy targets a 7- to 10-year investment horizon to capture the full enterprise adoption wave.

  • Market Inflection Point: As AI models become commoditized, spending is shifting from the models themselves to the tooling required to securely and compliantly deploy them.
  • Non-Discretionary Demand: Emerging regulations (e.g., EU AI Act) and the escalating AI-driven threat landscape create durable, compliance-driven demand for security and governance platforms.

Timelines & Execution

Deployment (2025-2027): Deploy capital into leading platforms across MLOps and AI-native cybersecurity to build positions as the market shifts from hype to fundamentals.

Maturation (2032-2035): Seek to re-evaluate as the market matures, winners are established, and the technology becomes a standard, ubiquitous part of the enterprise IT stack.

The Three Pillars of the Digital Scaffolding

MLOps (The Factory Floor)

The discipline of automating and standardizing the entire AI model lifecycle, from development and testing to deployment and monitoring, enabling predictable and scalable AI engineering.

AI-Native Cybersecurity

A new class of security designed to protect AI systems from novel threats (e.g., data poisoning, prompt injection) and leverage AI to enhance traditional security operations at machine speed.

AI Governance & Compliance

The technology layer that ensures AI systems operate responsibly, ethically, and in compliance with emerging legal frameworks, providing model explainability, bias detection, and audit trails.

Prime Investment Candidates

MLOps Leader

GitLab (GTLB)

The CI/CD leader leveraging its dominant DevOps platform to capture the emerging MLOps market.

Investment Thesis:

GTLB's single, integrated platform creates extremely high switching costs. Its strategic pivot extends this advantage into MLOps, offering a unified workflow for both traditional and AI software development. This provides a powerful wedge to capture enterprise AI spending, reducing complexity and total cost of ownership for customers.

Cybersecurity Leader

CrowdStrike (CRWD)

The endpoint security leader defining AI-native defense with a powerful, data-driven competitive moat.

Investment Thesis:

CRWD's formidable moat is its Threat Graph, which processes trillions of security signals daily to train its AI. This creates a virtuous data-driven network effect: more customers lead to more data, which leads to smarter AI, attracting more customers. In an AI security arms race, the company with the best data wins.

Cybersecurity Challenger

SentinelOne (S)

The autonomous challenger in AI security, differentiated by its focus on edge-based AI agents.

Investment Thesis:

S is differentiated by its autonomous AI agents that operate directly on devices, independent of cloud connectivity. This offers a unique value proposition for use cases requiring minimal latency and offline protection. The company's recent achievement of non-GAAP profitability marks a significant de-risking event, suggesting an attractive risk/reward profile.

The Bear Scenario: "AI Winter" (25% Probability)

Assumptions: A macroeconomic shock and persistent failure to monetize AI (pilot success rate remains <10%) leads to a collapse in AI-related CapEx. Enterprise AI budgets are frozen. Discretionary spending on MLOps platforms is slashed, while spending on AI-native security proves more resilient due to the escalating threat landscape, though deal sizes shrink and sales cycles lengthen.

The Neutral Scenario: "Soft Landing & Rebalancing" (50% Probability)

Assumptions: The market pivots to fundamentals as speculative hype wanes. Enterprise focus shifts from experimentation to profitable growth, and AI pilot success rates slowly improve to the 15-25% range. Demand for the full stack grows at a measured pace, with AI Governance and Security tools seeing the strongest demand due to their non-discretionary, risk-mitigating nature.

The Bull Scenario: "Productivity Revolution" (25% Probability)

Assumptions: AI is successfully deployed at scale, leading to a measurable productivity supercycle as enterprise pilot success rates rise above 30-40%. Explosive, non-discretionary demand materializes for the entire Digital Scaffolding stack, making these platforms a standard, non-negotiable component of the enterprise IT stack, analogous to CRM or ERP systems today.

Navigating the Headwinds: Key Risks

Hyperscaler Commoditization

AWS, Google, and Microsoft could bundle "good enough" tools into their cloud platforms at little to no extra cost, squeezing the margins of pure-play vendors. This is the most significant competitive threat.

AI Performance Plateau

If fundamental limitations of AI (e.g., hallucinations, unreliability) are not overcome, its use in mission-critical applications could be capped, limiting the ultimate market size for the entire ecosystem.

High Total Cost of Ownership

The high cost, complexity, and steep learning curve of these platforms could deter adoption by the mainstream market, confining the tools to a niche of sophisticated tech-forward companies.

"AI Winter" Macro Scenario

A severe, prolonged downturn in AI investment could freeze all but the most essential security and compliance spending, stalling the growth of the broader MLOps and development tools market.
