Investment Strategy

Securing the Ex-China REE Supply Chain ⛓️

A detailed breakdown of our investment strategy, designed to capitalize on the state-sponsored industrial realignment of the global Rare Earth Element (REE) market.

Our High-Level Strategy & Rationale

The foundational thesis is that the strategic imperative for Western nations to build resilient, domestic supply chains for Rare Earth Elements (REEs) has created a durable, multi-decade investment opportunity. This is not a conventional commodity cycle; it is a state-sponsored industrial realignment underwritten by national security, energy independence, and economic competitiveness, requiring a 7- to 10-year investment horizon.

  • Geopolitical Imperative: The opportunity arises from inverting a profound geopolitical risk: the weaponization of the REE supply chain by the People's Republic of China (PRC), which controls ~90% of midstream processing.
  • Structural Demand Shock: Demand is structurally tied to the global energy transition (EVs, wind turbines) and advanced defense applications, creating a non-discretionary, exponential growth trajectory.

Timelines & Execution

Deployment (Years 1-4): Deploy capital into cornerstone assets during the current "build-out" phase, navigating project execution risk as companies establish downstream processing and magnet manufacturing capabilities.

Drawdown (Years 5-10): Seek to exit the investment as the ex-China supply chain achieves a state of mature, cash-generative operation, and the market narrative shifts from construction risk to operational performance and efficiency.

Its well-funded push into downstream magnet manufacturing positions it to capture the highest-value segments of the market. Core Holding

Lynas Rare Earths (ASX:LYC)

The established, de-risked, and profitable incumbent in the ex-China rare earths space.

Investment Thesis:

A decade of operational expertise in complex solvent extraction at scale creates a powerful technical and human capital moat. As the trusted supplier for key Japanese, European, and American customers, it has a powerful first-mover advantage. Its DoD-supported expansion into the U.S. cements its role as a cornerstone of the Western rare earth alliance.

On-The-Radar Pick

USA Rare Earth (Private)

A highly strategic, private US company with a unique heavy rare earth (HREE) resource.

Investment Thesis:

This is a high-growth, higher-risk opportunity to monitor for a future public listing. Its Round Top project in Texas is uniquely rich in HREEs (Dy, Tb), which are critical for high-performance magnets and where Chinese dominance is most absolute. A successful development would make it a strategically invaluable asset for the West.

Predicted Performance Scenarios

Select a market scenario to view the projected outcome and the underlying assumptions.

The Bear Scenario: "Stalled Diversification" (25% Probability)

Assumptions: The Western initiative falters due to technical failures and strategic market manipulation by China. Beijing floods the market with low-cost REEs, driving prices below the sustaining costs of Western producers for a prolonged period. Major downstream projects face multi-year delays, leading to a crisis of confidence. End-users revert to Chinese sources, and the West remains critically dependent.

The Neutral Scenario: "Gradual & Grinding On-shoring" (50% Probability)

Assumptions: The ex-China supply chain is successfully established, but the process is slower and more expensive than projected. Core companies bring new facilities online but with 12-24 month delays and moderate cost overruns. REE prices remain volatile, but a "security premium" and long-term offtake agreements provide a floor price that ensures profitability. Western market share of processed REEs grows from <10% to 25-30%.

The Bull Scenario: "Strategic Realignment & Tech Acceleration" (25% Probability)

Assumptions: The Western build-out proceeds faster than anticipated, with flawless project execution. China becomes more aggressive with export controls, creating a supply shock and a durable price premium for non-Chinese material (>30%). A recycling technology innovator achieves a commercial breakthrough, rapidly adding a new source of supply to the Western ecosystem and re-rating the entire sector.

Navigating the Headwinds: Key Risks

Chinese Market Manipulation

The primary threat is China flooding the market with low-cost supply to render Western projects uneconomical. A strong balance sheet and government backing are key mitigants.

Project Execution Risk

Building complex processing plants is capital-intensive. Capex overruns and project delays are significant risks that could impair returns. Focus on management teams with proven track records.

Technological Substitution

A breakthrough in non-REE high-performance magnets (e.g., iron-nitride) would be a systemic, long-term threat by eroding the primary demand driver from EVs and wind.

Environmental & Social Opposition

"NIMBYism" can block or delay new mining and chemical processing projects, even with government support. Prioritize companies with existing, fully permitted flagship assets.
