Subject: Weekly State of the Market - Grains & Oilseeds (2025-09-16)

Grains & Oilseeds

Executive Summary

The latest Commitments of Traders (COT) data reveals a decisively bearish sentiment dominating the Grains & Oilseeds sector. Speculators are aggressively increasing their short exposure across key grain markets, with Soybeans and SRW Wheat showing extreme bearish positioning. While Canola and Rough Rice maintain a more balanced stance, speculative conviction is waning, signaling potential for further price weakness.

Key Opportunities

  • Grains Short Squeeze Potential (Soybeans, SRW Wheat): Extreme speculative short positions (COT Index near 10) in Soybeans and SRW Wheat could trigger sharp counter-trend rallies if any positive fundamental catalyst emerges, forcing significant short covering. Commercials are notably net long, indicating underlying supply concerns.
  • Canola Long Re-entry: Speculative net-longs are unwinding significantly WoW, suggesting that the current selling pressure might offer an attractive re-entry point for long positions once the market finds a stable bottom.

Key Risks

  • Deepening Bearish Trend in Grains: Speculators are consistently adding to net-short positions in Soybeans, Soybean Meal, and SRW Wheat. This strong conviction points to potential for further downside pressure, supported by commercials strategically accumulating long positions to offset future production.
  • Fading Conviction in Rough Rice: Speculative net-longs are decreasing, indicating weakening bullish support. This erosion of conviction leaves Rough Rice vulnerable to further price declines if demand falters or supply outlooks improve.

Asset Class Summaries

SOYBEANS - CHICAGO BOARD OF TRADE

  • Speculators:Dominantly bearish with a significant net-short position (-133,174 contracts), increasing their short bets by 2,479 WoW. Positioning is extremely bearish (COT Index: 10.70, Z-score: -1.74).
  • Commercials:Heavily net-long (133,835 contracts), indicating hedging of future production at current prices.
  • Implication:Extreme speculative bearishness suggests high short-squeeze potential, while commercial net-longs signal an undervalued market from a supply perspective.

CANOLA - ICE FUTURES U.S.

  • Speculators:Marginally net-long (1,475 contracts), but significantly reduced their net-long exposure by 5,322 WoW, indicating waning bullish conviction. Positioning is neutral (COT Index: 44.59, Z-score: -0.06).
