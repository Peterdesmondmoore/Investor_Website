Subject: Weekly State of the Market - Industrial Metals (2025-09-16)

Industrial Metals

Executive Summary

The latest Commitments of Traders (COT) data reveals a generally cautious, if not outright bearish, sentiment across Industrial Metals. Speculative bullishness in Lithium Hydroxide is a clear outlier, approaching extreme levels. Conversely, Copper and Steel HRC saw significant increases in speculative short positions, signaling growing bearish conviction. Aluminium MWP traders, however, show signs of waning bearishness.

Key Opportunities

  • Lithium Hydroxide Upside: Managed Money net short positions are being significantly reduced, and relative positioning is very bullish (COT Index: 81.84, Z-score: 1.68), suggesting potential for continued upward price action.
  • Aluminium MWP Short-Covering: Managed Money traders notably reduced their net short exposure, indicating weakening bearish conviction that could spark a short-covering rally.

Key Risks

  • Copper Bearish Momentum: Speculators aggressively increased net short positions by 7,098 contracts WoW, signaling strong and accelerating bearish conviction with further downside potential.
  • Steel HRC Short Pressure: Managed Money added substantially to net short positions (+5,928 contracts WoW), reflecting high conviction in future price declines and sustained downward pressure.

Asset Class Summaries

Aluminium MWP

  • Speculators: Net short (-7,114 contracts), but actively reduced net short positions by 3,554 contracts WoW. Sentiment is bearish, but conviction is visibly weakening. COT Index: 27.39. Z-score: -0.23 (Normal).
  • Commercials: Remain significantly net short (-10,669 contracts), showing no significant week-over-week change. They consistently hedge against price increases.
  • Implication: Weakening speculative bearishness suggests potential for price stabilization or a short-covering rally in the near term.

Aluminium

  • Speculators: Slightly net long (+38 contracts), shifting from net short WoW by +78 contracts. However, the Managed Money Z-score (-2.13) indicates this small net long is extremely low historically, implying deep underlying bearishness.
  • Commercials: Slightly net long (+38 contracts), with no significant week-over-week change.
  • Implication: Despite a nominal net long, the extreme historical bearishness among speculators indicates strong underlying downside risk.

Steel-HRC
