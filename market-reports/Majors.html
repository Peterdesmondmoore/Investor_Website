Subject: Weekly State of the Market - Currencies (2025-09-23)

Currencies

Executive Summary

The latest Commitments of Traders (CoT) data for the week ending 2025-09-23 reveals a highly divergent sentiment across major currencies. Speculators are extremely bullish on high-yielding emerging market currencies (Mexican Peso, Brazilian Real) and strongly convinced of lower Euro short-term rates. Conversely, commodity-linked currencies (CAD, AUD, NZD) face extreme net-short positioning. This environment creates both momentum opportunities in bullish assets and significant risks of corrections from overextended positions, alongside short-squeeze potential in oversold assets.

This signals crowded trades vulnerable to sudden corrections. [source: COT Report, 2025-09-23]

This widespread bearishness warns of growing market concerns about economic slowdown. [source: COT Report, 2025-09-23]

This indicates overwhelming conviction in lower Eurozone rates. Divergence from central bank hawkishness or persistent inflation risks violent repricing. [source: COT Report, 2025-09-23]
