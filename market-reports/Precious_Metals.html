Weekly State of the Market - Precious Metals (2025-09-16)

Executive Summary

The latest Commitments of Traders (COT) data reveals a robust speculative bullish stance in Gold and Platinum, with Gold's positioning now at extreme levels. Silver maintains a strong bullish bias, while Palladium shows encouraging signs of short covering. Commercial traders are consistently increasing bearish hedges across most precious metals, indicating a strong counter-trend stance.

Key Opportunities

  • Gold's Persistent Upside: Strong and increasing speculative net-long positions signal continued upward price momentum, supported by sustained conviction.
  • Palladium Short Squeeze Potential: Speculators are actively reducing net-short exposure, setting the stage for a potential short squeeze that could drive prices higher.

Key Risks

  • Gold Overextension: Speculative net-longs in Gold are at extreme levels (Z-score > 2.0), increasing the risk of a sharp correction if profit-taking or a sentiment shift occurs. Commercials are aggressively hedging this bullish sentiment.
  • Micro Gold Weakness: A notable decrease in speculative net-long positions indicates waning bullish conviction, suggesting potential for further price depreciation.

Asset Class Summaries

Gold

  • Speculators: Dominantly bullish with a substantial net-long position of 158,971 contracts, increasing by 1,792 WoW. Positioning is at extreme bullish levels.
  • Commercials: Remain heavily net-short (-65,384 contracts), aggressively increasing bearish exposure by 2,830 contracts WoW. They are clearly hedging against rising prices.
  • Implication: Strong speculative buying fuels potential upside, but extreme positioning coupled with commercial hedging warns of high exhaustion risk and potential for a sharp reversal.

Silver

  • Speculators: Strongly bullish with a net-long position of 35,806 contracts. Net-longs slightly decreased by 49 contracts WoW, indicating minor profit-taking but sustained overall bullishness.
  • Commercials: Maintain a significant net-short position (-27,925 contracts), with a slight reduction in bearish bets (+19 contracts WoW).
  • Implication: Speculative bullish sentiment persists, though the marginal reduction in net-longs could hint at short-term consolidation or increased caution.

Platinum

  • Speculators: Bullish, increasing net-long positions by 1,272 contracts WoW to a total of 13,086 contracts. Conviction is building.
  • Commercials: Heavily net-short (-19,402 contracts), expanding their bearish hedges by 1,134 contracts WoW.
  • Implication: Rising speculative interest points to potential price appreciation, but aggressive commercial shorting suggests significant supply or strong hedging demand.

Palladium

  • Speculators: Net-short (-5,191 contracts), but reducing bearish bets significantly by 775 contracts WoW. This is indicative of short covering.
  • Commercials: Net-short (-2,077 contracts), slightly increasing bearish exposure by 373 contracts WoW.
  • Implication: Speculative short covering could provide a near-term floor or impetus for a rally, contrasting with continued, albeit modest, commercial bearishness.

Micro Gold

  • Speculators: Net-long (1,862 contracts), but significantly decreasing net-longs by 193 contracts WoW. Bullish conviction is clearly fading.
  • Commercials: Negligible net position (0 contracts). Commercial activity is not a material factor in this market.
  • Implication: Weakening speculative support suggests potential for further price declines as bullish sentiment dissipates.
