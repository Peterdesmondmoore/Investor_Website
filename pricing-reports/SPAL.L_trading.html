The Invesco Physical Palladium ETC is an exchange-traded commodity designed to provide investors with a straightforward and secure way to gain exposure to the price of palladium. It trades on the stock exchange like a regular stock, offering an alternative to purchasing and storing physical palladium bars or coins directly.

The investment seeks to replicate the performance of the spot price of palladium, less the ongoing fees. To achieve this, the ETC is physically-backed, meaning the issuer holds physical palladium bars in a secured vault (at J.P. Morgan Chase Bank in London). Each share of the ETC is a debt security that is entitled to a corresponding amount of the underlying metal.

Unlike financial products that use futures contracts or other derivatives, this ETC's value is directly tied to the palladium it holds. This structure aims to eliminate the potential tracking errors and market effects, such as contango and backwardation, that can be associated with futures-based commodity funds. It provides a direct investment in the performance of the palladium spot price.
