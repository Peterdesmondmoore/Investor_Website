The DB Gold Short ETN (DGZ) is an exchange-traded note designed for investors who want to profit from a decline in the price of gold. It is a tool for taking a short-term, bearish position on the precious metal.

The investment seeks to track the inverse (-1x) of the daily performance of the Deutsche Bank Liquid Commodity Index – Optimum Yield Gold Excess Return™. This means if the underlying gold index falls by 1% on a given day, DGZ is designed to rise by approximately 1%, and vice versa, before fees and expenses.

DGZ does not hold any physical gold. It is an Exchange Traded Note (ETN), which is an unsecured debt obligation of the issuer (Deutsche Bank AG). This means investors are exposed to the credit risk of the issuer, in addition to the market risk of the underlying gold index. The ETN achieves its inverse exposure through the use of futures contracts.
