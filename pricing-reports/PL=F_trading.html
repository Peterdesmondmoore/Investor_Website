Platinum Futures, traded under the ticker PL=F, are standardized, legally binding financial contracts in which a buyer agrees to purchase a specific quantity of platinum from a seller at a predetermined price on a future delivery date. These contracts are a primary tool for institutional investors, producers, and speculators to manage price risk or bet on the future direction of the platinum market.

The primary purpose of the futures market is hedging and price discovery. For example, a platinum mining company can sell futures contracts to lock in a selling price for its future production, while a manufacturer can buy futures to lock in a purchase price for platinum it will need. Speculators, on the other hand, use these contracts to profit from price movements without ever intending to take physical delivery.

Unlike an ETF like PPLT which represents ownership of physical platinum held in a vault, a futures contract is a derivative agreement. Most participants close out their positions before the contract expires, settling the profit or loss in cash. The price of PL=F reflects the market's collective expectation of platinum's value on the future delivery date, incorporating factors like storage costs, interest rates, and market sentiment.
