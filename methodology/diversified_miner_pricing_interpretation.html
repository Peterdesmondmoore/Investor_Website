1. Scope & intent

Translate pricing context into guidance for diversified, cash-flowing miners (multi-commodity exposure), distinguishing commodity-beta moves from equity-specific risk and valuation re-rating.

2. What the signal measures

Equity technical regime (trend, volatility, volume/liquidity, 52-week positioning) for diversified miners; interpreted as a combination of commodity-beta, equity risk premia, and company-specific rerating dynamics.

3. Primary drivers

  • Underlying commodity price baskets and related FX exposures
  • Equity risk sentiment and discount-rate changes
  • Earnings revision cycles and guidance surprises
  • Capital allocation events (dividends, buybacks, capex) and balance-sheet risk perception
  • Sector rotation and flow dynamics into/out of resources equities

4. Interpretation rules

Condition Economic meaning Investment implication
Uptrend with normal volatility and stable volumeConstructive rerating/earnings confidence with controlled riskMaintain/accumulate; treat pullbacks as adds if commodity backdrop is neutral-to-supportive.
Uptrend with rising volatility and deteriorating volume (thin participation)Late-cycle move; risk of reversal or false break increasesReduce incremental adds; require confirmation via volume or fundamental catalysts; tighten risk management.
Downtrend with high volatility and large drawdownRisk premia repricing; could be commodity shock, earnings downgrade cycle, or sector de-ratingDe-risk; wait for volatility compression and trend stabilisation before re-entry.
Breakout above 200d with elevated volume percentileRotation/flow-supported trend change; higher likelihood of persistenceUpgrade confidence; allow staged adds with defined risk limits.
Near 52-week highs while volatility risingExtended state with increasing path risk (valuation/earnings expectations may be saturated)Prefer hold/trim; avoid chasing unless confirmed by strong commodity move or earnings upgrades.

Implementation note: treat these as overrides/weights applied to the generic pillar states, not as a standalone trading system.

5. Regime bias logic

For diversified miners, trend signals are more reliable when supported by participation (volume) and moderate volatility. Volatility expansion often reflects equity risk premium shifts, so interpret it primarily as a sizing/risk control input.

6. Confirmation or conflict signals

Confirm with the relevant commodity basket trends, sector/market breadth, and earnings revision context. Conflicts (e.g., miner breakout while commodity basket weakens) should downgrade conviction.

7. Known limitations

Technical regimes may conflate commodity moves and equity risk repricing; corporate actions and index rebalances can distort volume; proxy OHLCV does not capture intraday gap risk around announcements.

8. Model usage notes

Use pricing context as a timing overlay on top of fundamental/commodity views. Weight volume-confirmed trend changes higher. Treat volatility spikes around earnings as event-risk rather than persistent regime shifts unless sustained.
