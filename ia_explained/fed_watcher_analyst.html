We map the Federal Reserve Watcher as an explicit analyst pipeline

The Federal Reserve Watcher is not a single report page. It starts from shared signal history, macro-theme generation, RSS monitoring, and Fed document extraction, then moves through macro, tone, Beige Book, and projections synthesis before the decision engine produces the live watcher output.

This Fed Watcher Analyst mirrors the current repo state. Red nodes highlight the confirmed implementation risks: the macro stage still reads April 1, 2026 legacy macro-theme HTML instead of the April 4, 2026 IA pages, the FOMC meeting label is manual, document caches are effectively write-once, audit artifacts are not reliable provenance, and the live podcast page still double-wraps its article fragment.

Model Architecture

How canonical state, macro themes, Fed documents, analytical modules, and the final decision engine are wired today to produce the live Federal Reserve Watcher. The diagram keeps the real flow visible, but it also makes the stale or fragile parts explicit.

1. Build canonical Fed context

Shared signal history, macro-theme generation, RSS snapshots, and processed Fed documents create the state that the watcher should consume.

2. Synthesize policy intent

Macro, tone, Beige Book, projections, and document validation flow into the Federal Reserve Watcher decision engine.

3. Publish outputs, expose fragility

The consolidated report, stored podcast payload, and live podcast page remain visible alongside the stale macro wiring, manual event label, cache limits, and wrapper bug.
