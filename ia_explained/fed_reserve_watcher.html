Fed Watcher Anatomy
Federal Reserve Tone Decoder
Systematically analyses Federal Reserve communications to quantify shifts in policy tone across inflation, labour, growth, risks, and committee dynamics.
  • Converts qualitative policy language into structured, time-series scores
  • Normalises tone to detect change, not just absolute stance
  • Preserves source excerpts to maintain interpretability and auditability
Fed Macro Signal Synthesiser
Aggregates 60+ macro, market, and policy signals into a small set of interpretable macro (detailed below) states that describe the prevailing Fed policy environment. Signal weighting adapts across cycle regimes to produce a consolidated policy backdrop for decision use.
  • US Growth & Business Cycle. Assesses the US business cycle and growth momentum.
  • US Inflation & Price Dynamics. Tracks underlying inflation pressures and price drivers.
  • US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions. Evaluates system liquidity and monetary tightness.
  • US Credit Conditions. Monitors credit availability, spreads, and stress.
  • US Labour Market. Analyses employment, wages, and labour tightness.
  • Global Growth & Economic Cycles. Identifies global growth momentum and cycle divergence.
  • Global Inflation & FX Dynamics. Examines inflation trends and currency dynamics.
  • Cross-Asset Positioning & Sentiment. Measures investor positioning and market sentiment.
  • Global Trade & Demand. Tracks trade flows and global demand signals.
Federal Reserve Beige Book Synthesiser
Systematically synthesises Federal Reserve Beige Book commentary to extract regional economic signals and assess underlying momentum, risks, and turning points.
  • Standardises qualitative regional commentary into structured economic signals
  • Identifies cross-district trends, divergences, and emerging pressures
  • Feeds bottom-up regional insight into the broader Fed policy assessment
Federal Reserve Watcher
Integrates decoded policy tone, macro signal synthesis, and rate decision forecasts into a single, decision-ready view of each Fed meeting.
  • Presents a clear policy bias with underlying drivers
  • Links scenarios to observable triggers and upcoming events
  • Enables direct comparison with market pricing and consensus
Future Improvements
Planned enhancements focus on refining policy calibration, improving confidence signalling, and strengthening comparison with market expectations.
  • Reaction-Function Calibration. Refine dynamic weights across longer-cycle regimes (pre-cut, mid-cycle, late-cycle) using documented Federal Reserve behaviour.
  • Confidence Scoring. Introduce an internal confidence score reflecting data completeness, signal alignment, and clarity of Fed communication.
  • Market Consensus Overlay. Incorporate sell-side, futures-implied, and survey-based expectations as a comparison layer, without influencing model probabilities.
