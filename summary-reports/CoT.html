State of the Market Summary

Based on Commitments of Traders Data as of September 23, 2025

1. Executive Summary

The latest Commitments of Traders (CoT) data as of September 23, 2025, reveals a profoundly divergent and complex market environment. Overall sentiment is characterized by simultaneous extreme speculative bullishness in many traditional "risk-on" assets and safe-havens, alongside deepening bearishness in cyclical indicators and certain rate-sensitive assets. There is no clear "risk-on" or "risk-off" consensus; instead, market participants are making highly specific, often contradictory, bets.

Key themes include rampant speculative long positioning in equities (S&P 500, Nasdaq), precious metals (Gold, Silver), and some agricultural commodities (Cocoa, Sugar, Live Cattle), signaling potential overextension and mean-reversion risk. Simultaneously, deep bearish conviction is evident in small-cap equities (Russell 2000), specific grains (Corn, Soybeans, SRW Wheat), several altcoins, and crucially, long-duration US Treasuries. A significant net-short position in VIX futures underscores widespread market complacency. This bifurcated positioning points to elevated volatility and increased potential for sharp reversals across multiple asset classes, demanding careful risk management.

  • VIX Futures: Non-commercial speculators hold a substantial net-short position of approximately -101,193 contracts (97,679 long vs. 198,872 short) with a low COT Index of 39.54 and negative Z-scores. [Volatility report] Another report notes a net-short of -90,000 contracts with a COT Index of 5 and Z-score of -2.5, explicitly flagging a `flag_squeeze_setup`. [Industrial Metals report]
