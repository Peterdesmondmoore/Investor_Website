M2 Money Supply Signal (m2sl)

This signal analyzes the M2 Money Supply (M2SL), a broad measure of money in circulation, which serves as a critical indicator of monetary policy and its potential impact on asset prices. An expanding money supply is typically indicative of a looser monetary policy, which can be a tailwind for various asset classes.

The signal is based on two primary calculations to capture both the long-term trend and short-term momentum:

  1. Year-over-Year (YoY) Percentage Change: This measures the annual growth rate of the M2 money supply.
  2. 3-Month Percentage Change: This serves as a momentum indicator, highlighting any recent acceleration or deceleration in money supply growth.

A signal is then generated based on the following criteria:

  • Bullish: High or accelerating M2 growth suggests a supportive environment for equities due to lower borrowing costs, and for precious metals as a hedge against potential currency debasement.
  • Bearish: Low or negative M2 growth indicates a tightening of monetary conditions, which can be a headwind for equities and precious metals.
  • Neutral: Moderate and stable M2 growth that does not meet the bullish or bearish thresholds.

Treasury Yield Curve Slope Signal (gs10, fedfunds)

The Treasury Yield Curve Slope Signal is a contrarian indicator that provides insight into economic expectations and their potential impact on various asset classes. It is based on the spread between the 10-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Rate (GS10) and the Federal Funds Effective Rate (FEDFUNDS).

The shape of the yield curve is a powerful predictor of economic activity:

  • A steepening curve (widening spread) often signals economic recovery and potential inflation, which can be bullish for equities and industrial metals.
  • An inverting or flat curve (narrowing or negative spread) typically precedes an economic slowdown, which is generally bearish for equities and industrial metals but can be bullish for precious metals as investors seek safe-haven assets.

The signal is generated based on both the level of the spread and its trend, calculated using a 3-month moving average:

  • Bullish: An inverted yield curve (≤ 0%) signals a potential economic downturn, which is bullish for precious metals.
  • Bearish: A wide spread (> 1.5%) or a steepening curve suggests economic expansion, which can be bearish for precious metals but positive for equities and industrial metals.
  • Neutral: A moderate and stable spread.

By monitoring the trend in real yields, this signal provides valuable insight into the underlying economic conditions that drive investment demand across different asset classes.
