Subject: Weekly State of the Market - China (2025-10-01)

China Manufacturing Index

Executive Summary

The latest Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) for China, reported on October 1, 2025, for September 2025, registered 49.8. This indicates a contraction in the manufacturing sector and marks the sixth consecutive month of PMI figures below the critical 50-point threshold, following two months of slight expansion in February and March 2025. Based on these indicators, the overall market sentiment for China's manufacturing sector appears moderately bearish.

For Industrial Metals, the consistent contraction in the 'new_order' index (e.g., 46.0 in September 2025) signifies significantly weakening forward demand, presenting a substantial downside risk. While the 'production' index has generally remained in expansionary territory, declining 'raw_material_inventory' levels reinforce expectations of reduced future demand, suggesting limited appetite for new metal orders.

Regarding China Equities, the sustained contraction in the overall PMI, combined with persistent weakness in both 'new_order' and 'employment' (e.g., employment at 45.0 in September 2025), points to a challenging operating environment for Chinese companies. This trend raises concerns about corporate profitability and broader economic growth, contributing to a moderately bearish outlook for China equities.
