Subject: Weekly State of the Market - China (2025-09-01)

China Manufacturing Index

Executive Summary

The latest data, primarily focusing on China's Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) for August 2025, indicates a moderately bearish overall market sentiment. The PMI registered 49.4, remaining below the 50-point threshold for expansion, signaling a continued contraction in the manufacturing sector. This follows a trend of sub-50 PMI readings for several recent months.

Key takeaways include:

  • China Equities: The persistent contraction in manufacturing, characterized by weak new orders (46.6 in August 2025) and declining employment (45.6), suggests a challenging operational environment for businesses. This points to a moderately bearish outlook for China equities, as sustained demand weakness and manufacturing downturns are likely to pressure corporate earnings.
  • Industrial Metals: The consistent weakness in new orders and the overall manufacturing PMI below 50 imply a softening demand for industrial raw materials. While production remains slightly in expansion (50.8), the forward-looking new orders component suggests a likely future slowdown in industrial activity. Consequently, the outlook for industrial metals is moderately bearish.
  • Precious Metals: The provided manufacturing data does not offer direct or reliable inferences regarding the specific outlook for precious metals.
The overarching risk remains the ongoing contraction of manufacturing activity, particularly the sustained weakness in demand indicated by new orders, which could hinder economic recovery.
