Beige Book Summary: November 2025

National Backdrop

Economic activity remained largely unchanged since the prior report across most Federal Reserve Districts, though the balance shifted slightly with two Districts noting modest declines and only one reporting modest growth [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Overall consumer spending declined further, although higher-end retail activity continued to show resilience [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Manufacturing activity improved somewhat from previously varied conditions, despite ongoing headwinds from tariffs and uncertainty [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Revenues in the nonfinancial services sector were mostly flat to down, while residential construction activity was mixed, seeing declines in some areas and remaining unchanged in others [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. The agriculture and energy sectors were largely stable, an improvement from the prior period's general downturn, yet still faced challenges from low prices [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Outlooks were largely unchanged, with an increased risk of slower activity in coming months, partly influenced by the government shutdown, balanced by some manufacturing optimism [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].

Labour Market Conditions

Employment levels declined slightly over the reporting period, with roughly half of the Districts observing weaker labor demand, marking a softening from the previously stable conditions [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Firms increasingly employed hiring freezes, replacement-only hiring, and attrition to manage headcounts, rather than widespread layoffs, though layoff announcements did see an uptick [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Employers adjusted hours worked to accommodate business volume, and artificial intelligence was noted as directly impacting demand by replacing entry-level positions or boosting worker productivity [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Worker availability generally improved across most Districts, yet specific skilled positions and fewer immigrant workers remained difficult to secure, continuing a trend [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Wage growth generally held at a modest pace, though certain sectors like manufacturing, construction, and health care experienced more moderate wage pressures due to tighter local labor supply [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Rising health insurance premiums continued to exert upward pressure on total labor costs [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].

Prices & Cost Pressures

Prices continued to rise moderately during the reporting period, indicating a slight moderation in the pace of increase compared to the prior report [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Input cost pressures remained widespread and robust, particularly in manufacturing and retail, largely driven by tariffs, along with elevated costs for insurance, utilities, technology, health care, and energy [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Firms' ability to pass through these higher costs varied significantly, contingent on demand, competitive dynamics, and customer price sensitivity, often leading to margin compression or financial strain [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Prices for certain materials, consistent with previous observations, saw declines due to sluggish demand, deferred tariff implementation, or reduced tariff rates [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Contacts largely anticipate persistent upward cost pressures, but plans for raising selling prices in the near term were mixed, reflecting firms' cautious approach to pricing strategy [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].

  • Labour inflections: The continued preference for hiring freezes, attrition, and adjusted hours over outright layoffs signals a strategic, rather than reactionary, management of labor costs by firms [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. The increasing role of artificial intelligence in replacing entry-level positions and enhancing worker productivity suggests a structural shift in labor demand composition [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].

Firms facing margin compression due to these costs, coupled with mixed plans for future price increases, indicate a delicate balance between cost absorption and maintaining market share [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].
  • Capital investment: Caution among commercial clients regarding new long-term investments, often linked to economic uncertainty and high interest rates, points to restrained business expansion [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Conversely, targeted strength in AI-related infrastructure and data center construction suggests specific areas of economic dynamism [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].
  • Financial stress: Rising consumer loan delinquencies, especially among lower and middle-income households, highlight increasing financial fragility and potential for broader credit quality deterioration [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]. Ongoing tightening of credit standards by banks, particularly for business loans, could further constrain access to capital for firms [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html].
