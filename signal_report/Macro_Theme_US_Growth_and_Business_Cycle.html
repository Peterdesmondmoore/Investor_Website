As of 2025-12-13

Gemini Summary

Executive Summary

The US business cycle appears to be in a resilient late-cycle phase, characterized by decelerating but not yet broadly contracting real activity. Quantitative signals present a mixed picture: business investment intent shows a recovery to neutral, core manufacturing demand is bullish, while overall industrial production remains neutral. The housing sector is also neutral, showing mild weakness but recent stabilization in starts.

The Federal Reserve's latest Beige Book characterizes national economic activity as "largely unchanged" with "modest declines" in some districts, and an "increased risk of slower activity" in the coming months. Consumer spending has declined further, labor demand is softening, and pricing pressures are moderating, though input costs remain robust. Credit conditions show mixed loan demand and rising consumer delinquencies, implying underlying fragilities.

[Source: Durable_goods_capex_intent.html] The latest year-over-year growth for durable goods orders stands at 7.26%. [Source: Durable_goods_capex_intent.html]

Beige Book commentary indicates general caution among commercial clients regarding new long-term investments, often linked to economic uncertainty and high interest rates, suggesting restrained business expansion. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] However, there is targeted strength in AI-related infrastructure and data center construction, which suggests specific areas of investment dynamism. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] This suggests corporate investment is undergoing a selective expansion, with a broad baseline pace, but with cautious sentiment for general long-term commitments.

4. Yield-Curve Signals

The US Treasury yield curve relative to the Fed Funds rate generated a Bearish signal as of November 2025. [Source: US_Treasury_Yield_Curve_Slope.html] This is due to the yield spread (GS10-FEDFUNDS) of 0.21% exceeding its 3-month moving average trend of 0.027%, indicating a steepening momentum that is interpreted as Bearish for risk assets in this framework. [Source: US_Treasury_Yield_Curve_Slope.html]

Conversely, the broader Yield Curve Slope composite (using T10Y2Y and T10Y3M spreads) consistently remained in a Flat/Neutral regime throughout 2025, with its composite z-score ranging from -0.09 to 0.64, staying within the ±0.75 threshold. [Source: yield_curve_slope.html] This suggests that while there is steepening momentum in the policy-sensitive part of the curve, the overall structural shape has not entered a decisively steepening or inverted phase, presenting a nuanced picture that neither fully validates nor contradicts the Beige Book's mention of "high interest rates" impacting investment. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]

5. Beige Book Conditions

The Federal Reserve's November 2025 Beige Book indicates that national economic activity was "largely unchanged," with two Districts reporting modest declines and only one noting modest growth. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] Business sentiment suggests an "increased risk of slower activity in coming months," partly due to the government shutdown, but balanced by some manufacturing optimism. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] Consumer spending "declined further," although higher-end retail showed resilience, while residential construction was "mixed." [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]

Labour market conditions softened, with "employment levels declining slightly" and "weaker labor demand" noted by roughly half of the Districts. Firms generally opted for hiring freezes and attrition over widespread layoffs. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] Wage growth was modest, but input cost pressures remained "widespread and robust," driven by tariffs and other expenses. Prices continued to rise moderately, though with a "slight moderation in the pace of increase." [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] Credit conditions were mixed, with declining business loan demand often offsetting slight increases in residential mortgages, and "consumer loan delinquencies increased" in several districts. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]

6. Synthesised US Growth & Cycle Assessment

The synthesis of quantitative signals and Beige Book insights points to a US growth environment characterized by a resilient late-cycle phase, with early indicators suggesting a potential transition towards stall speed. While core manufacturing demand and capex intent show some bullishness and stabilization, overall industrial production is neutral, reflecting moderate demand alongside strong mining activity. [Source: Manufactureres_new_goods.html][Source: Durable_goods_capex_intent.html][Source: industrial_production_signal.html]

The Beige Book's qualitative assessment of "largely unchanged" activity with an "increased risk of slower activity" is consistent with these quantitative signals, especially when considering declining consumer spending, easing labor markets, and mixed credit conditions. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html] Yield curve signals are mixed, with policy-sensitive spreads steepening (Bearish for risk asset focus) while structural spreads remain neutral, implying ongoing vigilance regarding monetary policy's impact on longer-term growth expectations. [Source: US_Treasury_Yield_Curve_Slope.html][Source: yield_curve_slope.html] The dominant cycle regime can be assessed as a "Resilient Late-Cycle with Decelerating Momentum and Emerging Downside Risks."

6. Forward Risks

Upside Risks to Growth

  • Capex or durables acceleration: Sustained momentum in manufacturing and business investment could surprise to the upside.
  • Industrial production re-acceleration: A rebound in broader industrial output could signal renewed economic strength.
  • Curve normalisation signalling improved expectations: A clear, orderly steepening of the yield curve could reflect stronger forward growth expectations.
  • Beige Book improvement in demand or labour supply: A reversal in reported softening of consumer demand or easing labor markets could bolster growth.

Downside Risks to Growth

  • Capex retrenchment: A return to contraction in business investment could signal a broader economic slowdown.
  • Manufacturing stall speed: Deceleration or sustained neutrality in manufacturing activity, despite current strength in durables, would imply limited growth.
  • Policy tightening or renewed curve inversion: Aggressive monetary policy or a renewed, deep yield curve inversion could heighten recession risks.
  • Beige Book deterioration in credit or demand: Further weakening in consumer spending, rising delinquencies, or tighter credit conditions could precipitate a broader downturn.

7. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

This growth regime, characterized by decelerating momentum in a late-cycle environment but with pockets of resilience, suggests broad risk appetite may become increasingly selective. Sectors tied to core manufacturing demand and targeted infrastructure investments (e.g., AI data centers) may show relative outperformance, while discretionary consumer-facing sectors could face headwinds from declining spending and rising delinquencies. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Beige_Book_Summary.html]

The mixed yield curve signals indicate a complex monetary policy outlook; while the structural curve is not deeply inverted, steepening momentum suggests that financial conditions may not be easing for all market segments. This environment emphasizes the importance of discerning momentum versus fragility, with a bias towards quality and defensiveness in broader exposures, while seeking opportunities in areas demonstrating structural demand tailwinds.
