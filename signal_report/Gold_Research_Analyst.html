Gold · Research Analyst

Assessing Gold Outlook Based on Current Research

As of 2026-01-04

Gemini Summary

6–12 Month Stance

Stance: bullish

Confidence: high

Core Narrative

Gold is widely projected to maintain a strong upward trend and potentially reach new record highs through 2026, with some forecasts suggesting prices could soar to $5,000/oz by Q4 2026 (1)(12)(15)(17)(20)(21). This bullish outlook is underpinned by sustained demand from global central banks accumulating gold to diversify reserves and reduce reliance on the US Dollar (2)(4)(6)(13)(17)(21)(22). Furthermore, expectations of Federal Reserve rate cuts and a generally weaker US Dollar are significant tailwinds (1)(7)(8)(11)(15)(17)(20)(21)(22). Persistent geopolitical and geoeconomic uncertainties globally also enhance gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset (5)(8)(7)(15)(16)(19)(21)(22). Strong investment demand, particularly through ETF inflows and bar and coin purchases, continues to be a driving force (6)(7)(10)(12)(19)(22). Gold is increasingly recognized for its role in portfolio diversification and downside protection, especially in an environment where correlations between traditional assets like stocks and bonds have risen (8)(9).

Key Drivers

Central Bank Gold Accumulation

Directional implication: supports gold

Central banks have been significant net buyers of gold, with purchases increasing notably in September and October 2025 and averaging nearly 260 tonnes per quarter since Q1 2022 (2)(4)(6)(13)(22). This trend is strategic, reflecting a desire to diversify foreign exchange reserves and reduce reliance on the US Dollar amidst macroeconomic uncertainty (4)(13)(22). A 2025 survey indicates that 95% of respondents expect central bank gold reserves to increase (4).

Anticipated Federal Reserve Rate Cuts

Directional implication: supports gold

Expectations of further interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve are a dominant driver, as lower rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold (1)(7)(8)(17)(20)(22). Analysts anticipate gold prices to rise through H1 2026 due to these expected policy rate cuts (12). There is an 88% chance of a 25bps Fed rate cut by early December 2025, with further easing expected to remain a key factor through 2026 (16)(17).

Weaker US Dollar Outlook

Directional implication: supports gold

J.P. Morgan Global Research maintains a bearish view on the dollar for 2026, and a weakening US dollar generally makes gold more affordable for international buyers, boosting demand (1)(11). The US Dollar Index (DXY) appears to be turning lower, which is seen as a supportive driver for gold (16). This expectation of renewed dollar weakness encourages investors to reconsider US asset exposures, favoring gold (11)(22).

Geopolitical and Geoeconomic Uncertainty

Directional implication: supports gold

Heightened geopolitical and economic uncertainty, coupled with ongoing market volatility, reinforces gold's role as a safe-haven asset (5)(8)(7)(16)(22). Persistent global geopolitical unease and concerns over the fiscal sustainability of G20 economies contribute to this demand (15)(16)(19)(21)(22).

Robust Investment Demand

Directional implication: supports gold

Investors remain firmly in the driving seat, with huge ETF buying and robust bar and coin demand observed in Q3 2025 (6). Global physically-backed gold ETFs recorded their sixth consecutive monthly inflow in November 2025, reaching new month-end peaks in assets under management and holdings (7). US-listed ETFs accounted for a significant portion of global inflows, surging 160% year-on-year in Q3 2025 (12).

Portfolio Diversification and Downside Protection

Directional implication: supports gold

Gold's capacity to provide diversification and downside protection remains highly relevant amid increasing market shocks and surprises (8). It maintains a low correlation to equities and fixed income assets, making it an efficient source of portfolio diversification, especially when correlations between bonds and equities have risen (9).

Key Risks

Stronger-than-Expected US Economic Growth (Reflation Return Scenario)

A scenario characterized by stronger-than-expected economic growth, potentially driven by successful pro-growth policies (e.g., from a new US administration), could lead to a gold price correction of 5% to 20% (8). Such a development might reduce safe-haven demand and temper expectations for aggressive Fed rate cuts.

Sticky Inflation and Limited Fed Easing

Should inflation remain sticky, particularly in developed economies where it continues to accelerate, central banks might be less inclined to cut rates as expected (1)(5). Persistent sticky inflation, especially if combined with volatility, remains a primary investor risk (23), potentially limiting the extent or pace of Fed easing and thereby reducing one of gold's key tailwinds.

Technical Resistance and Rising US Real Yields

From a technical perspective, gold is struggling to complete a "triangle" continuation pattern and requires a sustained move above resistance at US$4,245/oz (16). Failure to break this resistance could signal a pause in the uptrend. Additionally, persistently rising 10-year US Real Yields could act as a headwind for gold if they break significant resistance levels, increasing the opportunity cost of holding the non-yielding asset (16).

Signposts to Watch

Federal Reserve Monetary Policy Decisions and Guidance

Would confirm: Faster and more aggressive rate cuts than currently anticipated, or dovish forward guidance, would confirm a supportive monetary policy environment for gold (1)(7)(16).

Would invalidate: A delay in expected rate cuts, fewer cuts than anticipated, or hawkish surprises from the Fed would invalidate a key bullish driver for gold (16).

US Dollar Strength/Weakness Trends

Would confirm: Continued depreciation of the US Dollar, particularly if the DXY turns lower as observed, would confirm a supportive environment for gold (1)(11)(16).

Would invalidate: An unexpected strengthening of the US Dollar would invalidate one of gold's significant tailwinds (11)(16).

Central Bank Gold Buying Data

Would confirm: Sustained or an increased pace of net gold accumulation by central banks, continuing the strong buying trend observed in late 2025, would confirm a foundational demand source (2)(4)(6).

Would invalidate: A significant deceleration or a pattern of net selling by central banks would invalidate a core structural support for gold (2)(4)(6).

Global Geopolitical Developments

Would confirm: Escalation of existing global tensions, emergence of new geopolitical flashpoints, or increased geoeconomic uncertainty would confirm continued safe-haven demand for gold (5)(8)(15)(16)(19).

Would invalidate: Significant de-escalation of major conflicts or resolution of key geopolitical uncertainties, such as a peace deal in Ukraine, could reduce safe-haven demand (5).

Scenarios

Base Case (Shallow Slip)

This scenario anticipates a softening US economy, a general decline in risk appetite among investors, and further rate cuts by the Federal Reserve. Under these conditions, gold prices are expected to rise by 5% to 15% in 2026 from current levels (8).

Implied stance: bullish

Key drivers: Federal Reserve Rate Cuts; Declining Risk Appetite

Upside (Doom Loop)

In a more severe macroeconomic environment characterized by a deeper and more synchronized global slowdown, driven by rising geopolitical and geoeconomic risks, gold could see a significant surge of 15% to 30% in 2026 (8). This reflects gold's enhanced appeal as a safe haven during periods of heightened global instability.

Implied stance: bullish

Key drivers: Geopolitical and Geoeconomic Uncertainty; Global Economic Slowdown

Downside (Reflation Return)

Conversely, a scenario where policies lead to stronger-than-expected global growth, particularly if a new US administration's policies succeed in boosting economic expansion, could result in a gold price correction of between 5% and 20% (8). This would likely diminish gold's safe-haven appeal and potentially lead to less accommodative monetary policy.

Implied stance: bearish

Key drivers: Stronger-than-Expected Global Growth; Successful Pro-Growth Policies

Disagreements & Uncertainties

Gold Price Trajectory: Continued Rally vs. Rangebound

There is a slight divergence in views regarding gold's price trajectory for 2026. While many sources anticipate gold will continue its strong rally and reach new heights, some suggest it could remain rangebound under certain conditions.

  • Gold is expected to remain strong and reach yet new heights, with forecasts ranging from $4,500/oz to $5,000/oz by Q4 2026 (1)(12)(15)(17)(20)(21).
  • Should current macro conditions persist, gold could remain rangebound, though the overall outlook still suggests a possibility of surprises (16).

Impact of US Federal Reserve Leadership Changes

The potential impact of the upcoming US Fed chair nomination introduces uncertainty regarding future monetary policy. While a new dovish chair could lead to more rate cuts, there are broader concerns about the Fed's independence and operational stability.

  • A potential nomination of a more dovish Fed chair could lead to more rate cuts than investors currently expect, which would be supportive for gold (15).
  • Regardless of the Fed chair outcome, there is likely to be concern over decreased independence and a shakeup of how the Fed operates (15).
