Gemini Summary

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given strong PERMIT momentum (z: 0.90) and rising HOUST momentum (z: 0.70) against a 0.75 expansion threshold, this setup aligns with Upward-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Trend continuation, not Regime shift.
  • The signal is currently in a persistent Neutral regime but is nearing a transition to Expanding as of the December 2025 observation (1).
  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Improving; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: High.

Methodology Applied:

  • Level smoothing using a 3-month moving average for PERMIT and HOUST to reduce seasonal noise (1).
  • Robust z-scoring of 6-month annualised permit growth and 3-month annualised starts growth to limit outlier influence.
  • Weighted composite construction (0.45 permits, 0.35 starts, 0.20 mortgage penalty) to balance pipeline data with active construction (1).
  • Dataset: FRED Housing Data (PERMIT/HOUST latest: 2025-12-31; MORTGAGE30US latest: 2026-02-28).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is a surge in permit momentum (Z_PERMIT: 0.90), which serves as the earliest sign of a housing upswing.
  • Starts have confirmed this trend (Z_HOUST: 0.70), indicating authorisations are successfully translating into groundbreaking activity.
  • Mortgage rates (Z_MORT: -0.18) remain a minor drag but have stabilised, easing the affordability headwind compared to 2024 levels (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A 3-month composite drop (dC_3m) exceeding 0.60.
  • The signal has shown consistent directional improvement for three consecutive months, suggesting high persistence.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Transition to Expansion supported by permit pipeline growth.
  • Most plausible upside risk: Accelerating activity if mortgage rates drop below the 12-month moving average.
  • Most plausible downside risk: Downward revision of preliminary permit data erasing recent momentum.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) due to the multi-month lag between permitting and completion.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; housing is a critical lead for consumer credit and durable goods demand.

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs the Growth & Business Cycle dimension through residential investment impulses (2).
  • Economic mechanism: Improved credit demand and supply-side construction activity following a period of restrictive rates.
  • Cycle position: Not determined; though momentum is rising, the signal does not define stage mapping.
  • Interacts with US Liquidity signals to determine if financing constraints are truly easing (3).

Regime Context:

  • The signal is in a persistent Neutral state (since late 2024) but is currently strengthening toward the Expanding threshold.
  • Direction of change: Strengthening.

Model Limitations:

  • High revision risk: Permits and starts are frequently revised, making the most recent 1–3 months preliminary (1).
  • Seasonal adjustment instability can amplify errors at cycle turning points.

Data & References:
