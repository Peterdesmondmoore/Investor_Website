Methodology Applied:

  • A weighted composite (0.45 Permits, 0.35 Starts, 0.20 Mortgage Rates) determines Expanding (>0.75) or Contracting (< -0.75) regimes (1).
  • Building permits are prioritized as the primary lead indicator for the construction pipeline (1).
  • Mortgage rates enter the calculation negatively to represent demand destruction and affordability constraints (1).
  • Latest observation date: 2026-04-30 (Mortgage rates only); 2026-01-31 (Housing Starts).

Key Dynamics:

  • Housing starts (HOUST) showed significant momentum through Jan 2026 with a 3-month annualized growth rate of 0.867 (1).
  • Mortgage rates (MORTGAGE30US) have stabilized near 6.37%, resulting in a neutral Z-score of 0.12 (1).
  • Internal tension exists between strong secondary confirmation (Starts) and the total absence of the primary lead (Permits).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A 3-month change in the (currently missing) composite below -0.60 would signal rapid deterioration (1).
  • Signal stability cannot be assessed due to the missing permit component required for the composite.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant; risks balanced due to incomplete data inputs.
  • Most plausible upside risk: Strong Building Permit data reveals an Expanding regime (>0.75).
  • Most plausible downside risk: Mortgage rates rising above historical Z-scores, creating demand destruction.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months); the methodology focuses on construction momentum and turning points (1).
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Low; the signal is currently incomplete and lacks its primary predictive component.

Macro Relevance:

  • Economic mechanism: Measures the supply-side response to mortgage-driven affordability and demand constraints (1).

Regime Context:

  • Direction of change: Indeterminate.

Model Limitations:

Data & References:
