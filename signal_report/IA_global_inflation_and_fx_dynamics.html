Global Inflation and FX Dynamics
Interprets global inflation and FX conditions through US dollar regime and G10 currency positioning to characterise disinflationary pressures and cross-currency stress dynamics.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the cyclical Bearish regime in the broad U.S. Dollar Index, with price trending below its 12-month moving average (1).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes this high-confidence cyclical signal over tactical noise (1).
  • However, tactical crowding in commodity currencies creates significant fragility (2)(3).
  • AUD positioning was downweighted due to internal conflict between bullish flow and bearish price action (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Reinforcement exists between the JPY carry-trade unwind and the broader USD cyclical weakness (4)(1).
  • Tensions are high in CAD and AUD, where extreme speculative long builds (z-scores > 2.8) lack price confirmation (2)(3).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime because global USD liquidity proxies remain stable or neutral (5)(6).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistent USD weakening amid tactical volatility as crowded long positions face periodic liquidation pressure.
  • Primary upside risk: A sharp tactical rally in the USD if DXY 6-month momentum reverses toward the +5% threshold (1).
  • Primary downside risk: A non-linear price decline in commodity currencies triggered by a China-led growth shock (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The U.S. Dollar Index closing above its 12-month moving average of 120.91 (1).
  • A reversal of speculative flow in the Japanese Yen back to significant negative values (4).
  • Broad speculative exit (long liquidation) in growth-sensitive currencies like AUD (3).
