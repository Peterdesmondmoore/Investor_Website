Macro Theme : US Inflation & Price Dynamics

US Inflation & Price Dynamics: synthesising realised inflation, price indices, wage costs and market-implied expectations.

As of 2025-11-30

Gemini Summary

Synthesising realised inflation, price indices, wage costs, and market-implied expectations.

Executive Summary

The overarching US Inflation Signal indicates a COOL regime as of November 2025, suggesting prevailing disinflationary pressures or deflation risk. This follows a period where the composite signal unwound from prior HOT and NEUTRAL readings, reflecting a significant moderation in broad inflationary dynamics across the US economy.

While the market-implied inflation expectations remain STABLE, a notable deceleration in short-term expectations and a neutral term structure indicate an easing forward outlook. Wage and labour market tightness signals also point to softening cost-push pressures, reinforcing the disinflationary narrative.

  • Realised inflation (overall composite) is exhibiting a disinflationary trend.
  • Market-implied inflation expectations are stable, but with negative short-term momentum.
  • Wage growth and labour market tightness indicate moderating cost-push pressures.
  • The overall inflation risk bias is positioned towards the downside, consistent with a cooling environment.

1. Realised Inflation: CPI–PPI Dynamics

As of October 2025, the Consumer Price Index (CPI) year-over-year growth was 2.79%, while the Producer Price Index (PPI) year-over-year growth registered 3.66%. The PPI continues to increase at a faster rate than CPI, yielding a positive PPI minus CPI divergence z-score of 0.234151. This places the CPI-PPI signal in a NEUTRAL regime.

The positive divergence, while not extreme, indicates that producers are experiencing higher input cost inflation than consumers, which could signal latent cost-push pressures in the supply chain. However, this level of divergence is within historical norms for a neutral state and does not currently suggest an imminent re-acceleration of consumer prices.

2. Policy-Relevant Inflation: PCE & Core PCE

For August 2025, headline Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) inflation stood at 2.74% year-over-year, with core PCE (excluding food and energy) at 2.91% year-over-year. The composite z-score for PCE, which equally weights headline and core measures, was -0.321531, classifying it in a NEUTRAL regime.

These figures indicate that both headline and core PCE measures, while still above the central bank's 2% target, have moderated from previous highs and are currently near their historical average trend. This suggests a balanced policy-relevant inflation environment, without immediate signals for significant acceleration or sharp deceleration.

3. Market-Implied Inflation Expectations

Market-Implied Inflation Expectations (MIIE) are currently in a STABLE regime, with a composite z-score of 0.034273 as of November 2025. This indicates that market participants expect inflation to remain near its long-term average, suggesting well-anchored expectations.

However, an examination of short-term momentum reveals a disinflationary bias. The 3-month annualised z-scores for 5-year and 10-year breakeven inflation rates are -0.820327 and -1.094457 respectively, reflecting a recent easing in forward inflation pricing. This implies that while the overall level of expectations is stable, the short-term trajectory points downwards, potentially in tension with persistent realised inflation if any.

4. Term-Structure of Inflation

The Inflation Term-Structure Composite registers at -0.315806 for November 2025, indicating a Neutral regime. Both the 5-year breakeven inflation rate (Z_T5: -0.357561) and the 10-year breakeven inflation rate (Z_T10: -0.486518) show negative z-scores, positioning them below their rolling historical medians.

The inverted real yield z-score (Z_mReal: -0.089421) is near its historical median, suggesting real financial conditions are not excessively tight or loose from this perspective. The overall neutral regime and negative breakeven z-scores suggest a moderating inflation outlook across the intermediate term, without a pronounced steepening or flattening that would signal extreme reflationary or disinflationary risks.

5. Wage Growth, Labour Tightness & Cost-Push Pressures

As of August 2025, the Wage Growth and Inflation Composite, which incorporates wage pressure and labour market tightness, resides in a NEUTRAL regime, with a smoothed composite z-score of -0.526283. The Wage Pressure component specifically recorded a z-score of -0.705149 in August 2025, having previously entered a COOL regime in January 2025 with a z-score of -1.162677.

The Labour Tightness z-score is also negative at -0.449661, briefly touching zero in March 2025. This collectively indicates a sustained softening in wage growth dynamics and a normalisation of labour market tightness. These factors suggest a reduction in underlying cost-push inflation pressures, aligning with the broader disinflationary trend.

6. Synthesis: US Inflation Regime Assessment

The current US inflation landscape presents a nuanced picture characterised by a predominant disinflationary trend, albeit with some areas maintaining a neutral posture. The overall US Inflation Signal, being in a COOL regime, indicates a broad deceleration in price pressures and a reduced risk of sustained high inflation. This is supported by softening wage dynamics and a neutral inflation term structure with a disinflationary tilt.

While policy-relevant PCE measures are neutral and above the target, their trend is moderating. Market expectations, though stable at current levels, are signalling easing momentum. The CPI-PPI divergence, while positive, remains within a neutral range, implying some pipeline pressure but not an immediate strong pass-through to consumer prices.

7. Forward Risks

Upside Risks to Inflation

  • Persistent positive CPI-PPI divergence, if sustained or widening, could eventually translate into increased consumer price pressures.
  • Unexpected re-acceleration in global commodity prices, particularly energy, could reignite headline inflation.
  • A rebound in short-term market inflation expectations could signal renewed conviction in future price increases.

Downside Risks to Inflation

  • Further deceleration in wage growth, indicating a more pronounced softening of the labour market.
  • Faster-than-anticipated disinflation in sticky components of core inflation, such as shelter.
  • A significant weakening of aggregate demand, reducing pricing power for firms and leading to broader disinflation.

8. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

An environment characterised by a COOL overall inflation signal, coupled with moderating wage pressures and neutral to disinflationary signals across other price indices and market expectations, typically influences asset classes by reducing the demand for overt inflation hedges. Fixed income assets may experience increased duration sensitivity as the prospect of higher-for-longer policy rates diminishes, contingent on growth stability. For equities, this regime could be supportive if it accompanies stable economic growth, as it implies lower discount rates and reduced input cost pressures, favouring growth-oriented segments.
