Silver Research Analyst

6–12 Month Stance

Stance: bullish

Confidence: high

Core Narrative

Precious metals, particularly Gold and Silver, are widely expected to extend their significant gains into 2026, building on a strong performance in 2025 (2)(5). Gold is anticipated to remain strong, potentially reaching new highs, with various forecasts placing it around $4,200-$4,530/oz (3)(4)(5)(6). Silver is projected to demonstrate even greater upside potential, potentially outperforming gold, with forecasts generally ranging from $50-$65/oz (3)(4)(5)(6). This constructive outlook is primarily supported by anticipated Fed rate cuts, a weaker US dollar, sustained central bank gold purchases, and persistent global geopolitical uncertainties (2)(4)(5). For silver, additional strength is derived from structural supply deficits and growing industrial demand, especially from green energy and AI-related applications (4)(5)(6)(7)(9).

Key Drivers

Anticipated Fed Rate Cuts

Directional implication: supportive

Expectations of lower rates from the Federal Reserve and an easing cycle are seen as a key macro factor supporting precious metals (2)(4)(5). A potential 25bps Fed rate cut this week and dovish guidance could influence yields, the dollar, and gold (1). Lower rates will sustain investor enthusiasm for gold, even if real rates remain static due to sticky inflation (5).

Weaker US Dollar

Directional implication: supportive

A weaker or depreciating US dollar is identified as a supportive driver for gold (1)(4)(5)(6). The US Dollar (DXY) appears to be turning lower, contributing to the bullish outlook for gold (1).

Sustained Central Bank Gold Purchases

Directional implication: supportive

Robust central bank demand for gold, with purchases averaging "almost 260 tonnes per quarter since Q1 2022", is a significant factor in gold's strength (2)(4)(5). Central bank diversification is a broader theme contributing to gold's role as a store of value (8).

Geopolitical Uncertainty and Unease

Directional implication: supportive

Ongoing geopolitical uncertainties, such as tensions in Russia and Venezuela, showed no signs of easing and act as a hedge against geopolitical stress (1)(2)(4). A "fraught global geopolitical environment" adds support for a bullish gold case (5).

Industrial Demand for Silver

Directional implication: supportive

Silver's "dynamic bull case" is powered by its dual role as a monetary hedge and an industrial metal (8). Strong industrial demand from solar (photovoltaics), automotive (especially EVs), and data centers/AI is expected to continue (4)(5)(7)(9). Electric vehicles consume significantly more silver than internal combustion engines, and the build-out of EV charging infrastructure further drives demand (7).

Tight Supply and Deficits for Silver

Directional implication: supportive

A persistent supply deficit in the silver market, which has seen "a deficit for four consecutive years," leads to "physical tightness" (4)(6). Structural supply deficits are anticipated (8), with another significant deficit of 117.6Moz forecast for 2025 (9).

Fiscal Dominance and Currency Debasement

Directional implication: supportive

Concerns over the fiscal sustainability of G20 economies focus investors' attention on gold as a haven (5). Government spending dictating monetary policy, termed "Fiscal Dominance and the Debasement Trade," leads to a "secular shift toward hard assets as fiat currencies rapidly lose purchasing power" (8).

Key Risks

Rising US Real Yields

10-year US Real Yields continuing to edge higher could act as a "headwind for Gold" if they break significant resistance levels (1).

Technological Thrifting in Solar PV

Innovation in photovoltaic technologies could "reduce silver consumption per watt by 15–20% in 2025" (6). Industrial fabrication for silver is forecast to "flat-line due to thrifting in PV" (9).

Impact of US Subsidy Cuts and Trade Tariffs

US subsidy cuts and trade tariffs pose risks to solar photovoltaic growth (7). Additionally, mixed government incentives and rising tariffs impacting affordability could affect automotive silver demand (7), and fears of potential US tariffs have led to silver outflows from London vaults (7). US trade and foreign policy is a general uncertainty (9).

Sizable Silver Stockpiles

Despite a forecast deficit for 2025, the price impact for silver is expected to be limited by "still sizable stockpiles of silver" (9). This could mitigate immediate price surges driven by supply tightness.

Central Bank Gold Purchase Trends

Would invalidate: A significant slowdown or reversal in central bank gold buying patterns (5).

Disagreements & Uncertainties

Impact of Silver Stockpiles on Price

There is a divergence on whether current silver stockpiles will limit the impact of anticipated deficits on price.

  • The silver market's "fourth consecutive deficit" in 2024 and an anticipated significant deficit in 2025 imply strong fundamentals and justify a "record-breaking rally" (6)(9).
  • While "Another significant deficit of 117.6Moz is forecast for 2025," its price impact is expected to be "limited by 'still sizable stockpiles of silver'" (9).

Gold ETF Inflow Trends

Reports offer conflicting signals on gold ETF inflows, either indicating slowing interest or room for further participation.

  • Gold's strength was partially offset by "slowing gold ETF inflows (-0.1%)" (1).
  • "ETF holdings across all precious metals are still well below their highest levels," suggesting "room for further investor participation" (5).

Effectiveness of Silver Thrifting in Solar PV

While technological advancements reduce silver per solar cell, their net impact on demand is subject to debate due to new cell technologies.

  • Technological "thrifting" is expected to "reduce silver consumption per watt by 15–20% in 2025," and industrial fabrication is forecast to "flat-line due to thrifting in PV" (6)(9).
  • However, "new next-gen solar cells (TOPCon, SHJ) require more silver, potentially offsetting reductions" from thrifting technologies (7).
