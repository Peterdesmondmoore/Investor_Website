Methodology Applied:

  • The stance_gap is calculated as policy_real_proxy minus rstar_proxy, with +/- 0.50 thresholds defining regime boundaries. (1)
  • Policy real proxy is derived via the Fisher relationship: FEDFUNDS minus 10-Year Breakeven Inflation (T10YIE). (1)
  • Neutral drift measures the three-month change in the neutral proxy, with values below -0.10 classified as "Falling." (1)
  • Confidence is downgraded to "Medium" when only one r* component (e.g., rstar_market_real) is available. (1)

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the contraction of the stance_gap to -0.35, moving policy out of the "Accommodative" zone (≤ -0.50). (1)
  • Stabilisation is evident as the neutral_drift has shifted to "Falling," suggesting the market-implied neutral anchor is descending toward the policy rate. (1)
  • Conditional Invalidation: A stance_gap move back below -0.50 or above +0.50 would trigger a regime reversal. (1)
  • Persistence Assessment: The move into "Near neutral" has been sustained for approximately two weeks following a prolonged accommodative phase. (1)

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Policy remains in a "Near neutral" context, providing neither significant headwind nor tailwind to growth.
  • Most plausible upside risk: A re-acceleration in inflation expectations (T10YIE) pushes policy back into an "Accommodative" regime.
  • Most plausible downside risk: A sharp decline in market real yields (DFII10) increases apparent policy restrictiveness despite unchanged nominal rates.
