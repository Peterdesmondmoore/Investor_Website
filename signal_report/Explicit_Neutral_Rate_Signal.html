Gemini Summary

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a stance gap of -0.42 and a stable neutral drift, this setup aligns with Indeterminate price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Regime shift. (1)
  • The signal has transitioned from an accommodative stance to a near-neutral regime as the stance gap narrowed. (1)
  • Conviction Band: Low; Interpretation Confidence: Low Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: Low.

Methodology Applied:

  • The Stance Gap is calculated by subtracting the Neutral Proxy from the Real Policy Proxy; a gap between -0.50 and +0.50 defines a "Near Neutral" regime. (1)
  • Neutral Drift is classified as "Stable" when the three-month change in the r* proxy is within ±0.10. (1)
  • Confidence is downgraded to "Medium" in the methodology when only one r* component (Market Real Rate) is available, as seen in the current dataset. (1)
  • Data: Federal Funds Effective Rate, 10-Year TIPS Yield, and 10-Year Breakeven Inflation (Latest: 2026-02-25). (1)

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the rstar_proxy level of 1.78 versus a policy_real_proxy of 1.36, resulting in a -0.42 stance gap. (1)
  • Momentum has stabilised following a period of "Rising" neutral rate drift in early January. (1)
  • Conditional Invalidation: A move in the stance_gap below -0.50 would revert the signal to "Accommodative." (1)
  • Signal stability is high, with the "Near neutral" classification persisting since mid-February. (1)

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Stance remains near neutral as market-implied real rates and policy settings remain aligned.
  • Upside risk: Shift to restrictive regime if inflation expectations (T10YIE) fall sharply while nominal rates hold.
  • Downside risk: Return to accommodative regime if 10-year real yields (DFII10) rise further, widening the negative gap.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months); neutral rate proxies reflect structural market pricing of long-term real returns.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Low; downgraded due to the absence of the term-premium adjusted component.

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions by measuring the gap between current policy and the estimated "fair" real rate. (1)
  • Implied mechanism: A narrowing negative gap suggests the Federal Reserve's stance is becoming less stimulative relative to market neutral. (1)
  • Cycle position: Not determined.

Regime Context:

  • Regime Transition: The signal recently exited an "Accommodative" phase (Jan) into a "Near Neutral" phase (Feb).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising.

Model Limitations:

  • Requires at least one r* component; current output relies solely on the 10-year market real rate (DFII10). (1)
  • The proxy is market-anchored and may deviate from the Federal Reserve's internal structural r* estimates. (1)

Data & References:

  • Dataset: r* Proxy Signal components (DFII10, T10YIE, FEDFUNDS) through 2026-02-25. (1)
  • Influential Datapoints: stance_gap (-0.42) and neutral_drift (Stable).
