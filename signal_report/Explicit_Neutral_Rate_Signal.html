Methodology Applied:

  • Stance Gap: Values ≤ -0.50 indicate an accommodative policy stance, supporting growth and risk assets (1).
  • Neutral Drift: Drift > +0.10 indicates a rising neutral rate context, supporting a "higher for longer" policy narrative (1).
  • Regime Bias: Negative stance gap values correspond to an accommodative/expansionary macro regime (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the stance gap of -0.75, which has widened (moved further below -0.50) throughout March, deepening the accommodative signal (1).
  • Neutral drift has shifted to "Rising" (r* proxy increased from 1.72 to 2.08 since late February), which effectively reduces the restrictiveness of the current policy rate (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A contraction in the stance gap to > -0.50, shifting the regime back to "Near neutral."
  • The signal shows persistent directional movement away from the neutral threshold, suggesting a strengthening trend in policy accommodation (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Continued support for risk assets as the stance gap remains deeply accommodative.
  • Upside risk: Rising neutral rates (r*) allow for higher policy rates without inducing economic contraction.
  • Downside risk: Sharp drop in market-implied r* (falling drift) would increase the effective restrictiveness of the current stance.
