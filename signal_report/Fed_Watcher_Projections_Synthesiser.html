Fed Projections Synthesiser Summary

Inference of the Federal Reserve-s internally prepared future economic state, based exclusively on Fed-authored projections and communications.

1. Fed-Implied Growth Outlook

The Federal Reserve is preparing for a transition from a modest growth environment in 2025 to a more resilient expansion in 2026 (1). Projections indicate a median real GDP growth of 1.7 percent for 2025, which is expected to accelerate to 2.3 percent in 2026 (2). This outlook is underpinned by the assumption that consumer spending remains solid and business fixed investment continues to be bolstered by structural tailwinds, specifically spending on data centers and artificial intelligence (3). While the current quarter reflects temporary weights from fiscal disruptions, the internal baseline assumes these effects are offset by a reopening-related rebound (4).

2. Fed-Implied Inflation Trajectory

The internal preparedness scenario acknowledges that inflation remains elevated and is currently subject to a "one-time shift" in the price level caused by external cost pressures (3). The Fed's projections place PCE inflation at 2.9 percent for 2025, with a prepared path that sees it declining to 2.4 percent in 2026 and eventually reaching the 2.0 percent goal by 2028 (2). Communication emphasizes that while disinflation in services continues, the committee is preparing for goods inflation to remain volatile in the near term as pre-tariff inventories are depleted and higher input costs are passed through (5)(6).

3. Fed-Implied Labour Market Trajectory

The Fed is preparing for a "less dynamic" and "gradually cooling" labour market characterized by low levels of both hiring and layoffs (1). The preparedness state assumes the unemployment rate will edge up to a median of 4.5 percent by late 2025 before stabilizing at approximately 4.4 percent in 2026 (2). Qualitative language suggests the Fed is increasingly focused on downside risks to employment, noting that the balance of risks has shifted as labour supply growth slows and demand softens (3)(4).

4. Dominant Risk Narrative

A "challenging tension" between two-sided risks dominates current Fed preparation (3). The primary preparedness scenario is defined by asymmetric tail risks: upside risks to inflation (persistent tariff-related pass-through) and downside risks to employment (sharper-than-expected weakening in demand) (1). Internal assessments indicate that the Committee is preparing for a scenario where these two goals may remain in conflict, necessitating a "deliberate" and "careful balancing" of the dual mandate (4)(6).

5. Evidence Map (Concise)

  • Key SEP signals: Median GDP projections upgraded for 2026 (2.3%); unemployment projected to hold near 4.4-4.5%; PCE inflation expected to peak in early 2026 (2).
  • Key Beige Book themes: Slight to modest growth reported in most districts; price sensitivity rising among low-to-moderate income consumers; moderate wage growth becoming "normal" (5).
  • Key language or framing: Jan 2026 statement maintains that inflation is "somewhat elevated" while job gains remain "low"; Powell highlights the "range of plausible estimates of neutral" for the policy rate (3)(6).
