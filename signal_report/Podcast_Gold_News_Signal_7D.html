The gold market is currently defined by high volatility and conflicting macro signals. While the metal remains up approximately 17% on a year-to-date basis, we have observed a period of consolidation with prices trending toward a second consecutive weekly loss. The primary pressure points include a strengthening dollar and "higher-for-longer" interest rate sentiment following sticky inflation data, even as conflict in the Middle East continues to provide a structural floor for safe-haven demand.

What is driving the news flow

A significant driver of the current environment is the tightening link between energy prices and precious metals. We are seeing rising crude oil prices dampen hopes for imminent Federal Reserve rate cuts, which traditionally weighs on non-yielding assets like gold. Simultaneously, an emerging trend of resource nationalism is taking hold in major producing regions, with governments moving to capture a larger share of the "gold rush" through aggressive new tax and royalty regimes.

Supply & Mining

This sector dominated the news flow, highlighted by Ghana’s implementation of a new sliding-scale royalty system that ties state revenue directly to surging gold prices. We also noted significant corporate maneuvers, including Freeport-McMoRan securing long-term rights at the Grasberg mine and Lundin Mining expanding its footprint in Chile. Furthermore, the high-price environment is triggering a shift in supply dynamics, as seen in Japan’s pivot toward "urban mining" and recycling to bypass traditional extraction costs. However, rising energy prices remain a looming threat, with analysts suggesting that $100 oil could increase gold mining costs by nearly 10%.

Investment & Financial Markets

Market participants are showing a growing preference for gold over silver during this period of heightened volatility. We observed resilient wholesale demand and 16 consecutive months of central bank purchases by the People’s Bank of China, which has helped offset the impact of a stronger dollar. In the futures market, April contracts have faced pressure, testing support levels near 5,000 as traders recalibrate for a potential delay in Federal Reserve policy easing. Despite these fluctuations, institutional sentiment remains supportive of gold as a "geopolitical shock absorber" within diversified portfolios.

Regulatory & Policy

Gold is increasingly being framed within the context of strategic and critical minerals, particularly regarding its role in defense and the burgeoning AI-energy nexus. New tiered royalty structures in Zimbabwe and windfall tax discussions in Ghana signal a broader shift in how mineral-rich nations manage their subsoil assets. We also see heightened regulatory scrutiny on "blood gold" and illegal mining operations, particularly in South America and Southeast Asia, which could lead to tighter ESG-driven supply constraints in the future.

Geopolitical Risk

Tensions in the Middle East, particularly involving Iran and potential disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, remain the primary catalysts for sudden price spikes. However, we have observed a paradoxical trend where gold occasionally fades during active conflict as investors flee to the extreme liquidity of the U.S. dollar. This creates a volatile environment where gold must compete with the "greenback" for dominance as the ultimate hedge against military and economic instability.

What to watch next

We are monitoring the 5,000 level in April futures as a critical technical anchor for the current bull cycle. Key signposts for the coming weeks include the next U.S. CPI print and the stability of the 10-year Treasury yield, which will dictate the dollar's trajectory. We should also watch for the ripple effects of Ghana’s royalty regime, as it may serve as a template for other African producers seeking to recalibrate their fiscal terms. The news flow points toward a period of stabilization rather than a fresh breakout, with investable opportunities emerging in junior miners that can prove high-grade continuity amid rising operational costs. We expect Harmony Gold and Artemis Gold to remain in focus as they navigate production adjustments and cost pressures.
