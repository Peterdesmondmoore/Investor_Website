Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Silver narratives this week were characterized by extreme volatility as the metal was pulled between safe-haven geopolitical support and macro-driven headwinds. Early in the week, prices found support from escalating tensions in the Middle East and disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, which briefly made silver an outlier for gains compared to other industrial metals. However, this support was consistently eroded by a strengthening U.S. dollar and rising Treasury yields following "sticky" CPI inflation data. While long-term supply narratives remain active through drilling expansions and the rise of "urban mining" in Japan, the immediate narrative focused on silver's sensitivity to interest rate expectations. Ultimately, the week closed with a predominantly negative tone as the dollar’s strength outweighed geopolitical risk premiums.
