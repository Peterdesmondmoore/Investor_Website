Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Silver narratives this week were characterized by a sharp tension between strong physical fundamentals and shifting macroeconomic expectations. The Silver Institute highlighted a sixth consecutive year of market deficits and robust investment demand, even as prices moderated from January's record highs. Significant headwinds emerged from a major beat in U.S. non-farm payrolls data, which tempered expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts and bolstered the U.S. dollar. Supply-side risks gained attention following reports of mining disruptions and security concerns in Mexico and Chile. While silver futures showed intraday resilience with a notable surge above $86 early in the week, the broader trend reflected high volatility, prompting Indian regulators to propose price bands for silver ETFs. Narrative sentiment remains balanced between long-term structural undersupply and the cooling impact of a resilient U.S. labor market on precious metals.
