Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Silver experienced strong positive momentum early in the week, repeatedly reaching new all-time highs, primarily driven by increasing global demand from AI and data center infrastructure, ongoing geopolitical tensions, and a weaker U.S. dollar. This upward trajectory was further supported by reports of tightening physical supplies, a reduction in registered inventories creating a scarcity premium, and a projection of a continued market deficit throughout 2026 by Fitch’s BMI. However, prices saw a sharp pullback towards the end of the week, attributed to easing geopolitical concerns and the U.S. government holding off on critical minerals tariffs. Despite the intraday volatility, a disconnect between paper market movements and physical supply tightness was noted, while Bank of America downgraded mining stocks, signaling caution. Experts presented a mixed outlook, forecasting potential for silver to reach $100/oz but warning of an impending sharp correction and key risks.
