US Labour Market
Synthesises employment composition, labour-market tightness, wage and hours signals, and worker flows/sentiment to assess the prevailing US labour-market regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Dominant drivers indicate weakening conditions. Consumer sentiment is bearish, signaling a demand headwind (1). Labor market turnover is bearish, reflecting loosening labor conditions (2). Underemployment risk is rising, approaching a stress-escalation threshold (3).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes these high-weight signals, which exhibit high conviction and interpretation confidence. Several medium-weight signals, such as Employment Composition (4), are neutral but near thresholds indicating underlying fragility.
  • The Labour Market Momentum signal (5) was downweighted due to its low conviction band. This limits its influence despite high interpretation confidence, maintaining its neutral stance.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals reinforcing a weakening outlook include Consumer Sentiment (1), Labor Market Turnover (2), and Underemployment Risk (3). The Employment Composition signal (4) also suggests conditions are close to slackening.
  • Tensions exist with several neutral signals, including Explicit Slack Gap (6), Sectoral Employment Diffusion (7), Job Flows (8), Weekly Hours (9), and Labor Market Tightness (10).
  • These neutral readings primarily reflect stability rather than strength. The clear negative bias from high-weight indicators, coupled with other signals nearing negative thresholds, indicates underlying fragility.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: The labor market will persist in a fragile balance, characterized by persistent slack and demand headwinds.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained and significant re-acceleration in job flows and labor demand, leading to stronger overall growth.
  • Primary downside risk: A confirmed shift to a "COOL" regime across multiple neutral labor market indicators, alongside further deterioration in sentiment.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A reversal in the Labor Market Turnover Differential signal's robust z-score above -0.50 (2).
  • The Underemployment Risk signal's composite z-score consistently declining below 0.75 (3).
  • A sustained increase in the University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment signal's robust z-score above -0.5, combined with positive momentum (1).
