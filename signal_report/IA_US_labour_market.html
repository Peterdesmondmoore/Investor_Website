US Labour Market
Synthesises employment composition, labour-market tightness, wage and hours signals, and worker flows/sentiment to assess the prevailing US labour-market regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight structural and cyclical signals currently exhibit significant divergence (1)(2).
  • Job Flows and Employment Composition remain neutral, suggesting stable headline dynamism (2)(3).
  • However, Underemployment Risk is at a high-stress "HOT" level (1).
  • Labor Market Turnover and Labor Momentum were downweighted due to internal conflicts and low confidence (4)(5).
  • The combination of neutral flows but rising internal stress supports a transitional label.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Job Flows, Employment Composition, and Weekly Hours align in a Neutral state (2)(3)(6).
  • Tensions exist between stable headline employment and deeply bearish Consumer Sentiment (7).
  • Further tension is evident in Underemployment Risk flagging high stress despite neutral payroll breadth (1)(8).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime but confirm the fragile nature of the current balance.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A gradual drift toward a Defensive regime as underemployment stress persists.
  • Primary upside risk: A sharp rebound in consumer sentiment and worker confidence (quits) (7)(4).
  • Primary downside risk: Widening labor slack gaps and deteriorating job-finding duration (9)(1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Slack Gap widening beyond +0.50 pp would signal a shift to Defensive (9).
  • Sectoral Employment Diffusion dropping below -0.75 z-score would confirm broad-based contraction (8).
  • A move in Job Flows to a "COOL" regime (z-score < -0.75) would invalidate the current neutral dynamism (2).
