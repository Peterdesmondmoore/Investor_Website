US Labour Market
Synthesises employment composition, labour-market tightness, wage and hours signals, and worker flows/sentiment to assess the prevailing US labour-market regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Regime Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The regime is driven by a combination of high-conviction demand deterioration and rising underlying labor market stress, despite headline stability. Consumer sentiment is strongly Bearish, indicating a significant demand headwind (1). Concurrently, the Underemployment Risk signal has escalated to HOT, signaling rising broad labor market slack and longer job-finding durations (2).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized these signals due to their High Aggregation Weight Hint, High Interpretation Confidence, and High Conviction. Most other labor market signals remain in a Neutral regime but carry Medium Aggregation Weight.
  • The Labor Market Turnover Differential signal was downweighted (3). This was due to its Mixed Interpretation Confidence and Internal Conflict Flag (Yes), where the raw differential remains positive despite the z-score triggering a Bearish regime (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals generally reinforce a narrative of underlying weakening. Bearish consumer sentiment (1) aligns with rising underemployment risk (2), suggesting broader economic fragility. Momentum in the Explicit Slack Gap signal also hints at widening slack, even within a Neutral regime (4).
  • Tensions exist with several other cyclical signals, including Employment Composition (5), Sectoral Employment Diffusion (6), Job Flows (7), Labour Market Momentum (8), Weekly Hours (9), and Labor Market Tightness (10), which all indicate Neutral conditions.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call. The high-weight signals pointing to rising stress and demand headwinds are more influential. The "Neutral" readings may mask underlying deterioration captured by deeper slack indicators.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: The labor market remains in a fragile balance, characterized by persistent consumer cautiousness and increasing underemployment, even as headline employment metrics appear stable.
  • Primary upside risk and trigger: A sustained rebound in consumer sentiment, coupled with a reversal in underemployment trends, with the composite z-score for Underemployment Risk falling below +0.75 (2).
  • Primary downside risk and trigger: Further deterioration in consumer sentiment and a continued widening of the slack gap, pushing the Explicit Slack Gap beyond +0.50 pp (4).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained increase in the University of Michigan Sentiment Level_z above -0.5, coupled with positive momentum (1).
  • The Underemployment Risk composite z-score consistently falls below +0.75, moving out of its HOT regime (2).
  • The Employment Composition signal's z-score moves above +0.75, indicating a HOT labor market driven by stronger participation or employment growth (5).
