US Labour Market
Synthesises employment composition, labour-market tightness, wage and hours signals, and worker flows/sentiment to assess the prevailing US labour-market regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight indicators for Job Flows (1) and Employment Composition (2) remain neutral, suggesting a stable headline labor market.
  • The high-weight Underemployment Risk Signal has reached a "HOT" stress state (3), indicating rising cyclical slack and elongated job-finding.
  • The Sectoral Employment Diffusion Signal was downweighted due to internal conflict and extreme z-score anomalies, despite signaling a cooling breadth (4).
  • The Labour Market Momentum (5) and Labor Market Turnover (6) signals were downweighted due to "Low Confidence" and "Internal Conflict" flags.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals for Job Flows (1), Employment Composition (2), and the Explicit Slack Gap (7) reinforce a neutral headline state.
  • Tension exists between stable headline employment and bearish consumer sentiment (8) combined with restrictive manufacturing hours (9).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as gross job flows and headline participation remain resilient.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Labor market normalization toward equilibrium as momentum widens toward positive slack (7).
  • Primary upside risk: A reversal in momentum if the unemployment rate falls and job-finding duration shortens (3).
  • Primary downside risk: A rapid transition to "Slackening" if the 3-month gap change accelerates beyond thresholds (7).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A Slack Gap reading ≥ +0.50 pp would trigger a shift to a Defensive / Risk-Negative regime (7).
  • The Underemployment z-score falling below +0.75 would signal stabilization and return to an Expansionary regime (3).
