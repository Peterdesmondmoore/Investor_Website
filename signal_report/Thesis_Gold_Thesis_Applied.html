Gold – Relative Attractiveness Assessment

Based on Gold Thesis + US & Global Macro Themes.

1. Executive Summary

The consolidated assessment for Gold suggests a broadly supportive environment, with current macro conditions aligning closely with regimes favorable for the asset. A backdrop of easing liquidity, a weakening USD trend, and declining real interest rates provides structural support. While US inflation shows a cooling bias, emerging credit fragility and extreme speculative short positioning in gold and silver highlight underlying risks and undervaluation.

  • Strategic Attractiveness: Moderate-High.
  • Tactical Attractiveness: Moderate-High.
  • Key Supportive Channels:
    • Falling real yields. [Source: us_liquidity]
    • Weakening USD trend. [Source: global_fx]
    • Expansionary liquidity and monetary conditions. [Source: us_liquidity]
    • Light speculative positioning in gold and silver. [Source: global_growth]
    • Emerging US credit fragility and systemic risk concerns. [Source: us_credit]
  • Main Headwinds:
    • Cooling US inflation dynamics. [Source: us_inflation]
    • Calm equity market volatility (VIX). [Source: cross_asset]
    • Resilient global industrial demand (though juxtaposed with defensive precious metals positioning). [Source: global_growth]

2. Theme-by-Theme Evaluation

Map each macro theme to the Gold Thesis signal categories (real yields, USD trend, liquidity, inflation, positioning, risk, physical demand, central-bank behaviour, technical trend where applicable).

US Macro Themes

  • US Inflation & Price Dynamics: The overall US inflation landscape is in a "COOL" regime, with CPI and PCE tracking below 3% YoY and market expectations stable to cooling. Wage growth and labor market tightness signals are "NEUTRAL" and decelerating, reducing cost-push pressures. This points to a disinflationary environment, which, when combined with policy easing, can lead to falling real yields, a supportive factor for gold. [Source: us_inflation]
  • US Labour Market: The US labor market is characterized as "Neutral with Emerging Slack and Demand Headwinds." Key indicators like the unemployment rate (4.4% in Sep 2025) and U-6 underutilization are rising, while JOLTS tightness eases, and turnover signals are "Bearish." This suggests moderating wage pressure and a disinflationary impulse, which influences real yields and overall growth risk. [Source: us_labour]
  • US Growth & Business Cycle: The US economy is in a "Decelerating Late-Cycle with Emerging Credit Fragility." While core manufacturing shows "Bullish" momentum, overall industrial production is "Neutral." The policy-sensitive yield curve (GS10-FEDFUNDS) is "Bearish" (steepening from inversion), signaling adverse risk. The Beige Book highlights softening demand, easing labor, and rising consumer loan delinquencies. This points to increasing systemic risk and a deteriorating economic outlook, a supportive factor for gold. [Source: us_growth]
  • US Credit Conditions: US credit conditions are "Broadly Neutral" but show emerging pressures. Credit spreads are widening from historically tight levels, and the STLFSI4 component signals "Bearish" conditions, indicating elevated stress. Rising consumer loan delinquencies further highlight financial fragility. This suggests rising systemic risk and potential for financial instability, which is supportive for gold. [Source: us_credit]
  • US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: The liquidity regime is "Expansionary," driven by a significant unwind of the ON RRP facility and a "Bullish" M2 money supply signal (re-expansion). Real interest rates have transitioned to a "Neutral" regime after an extended period of restrictiveness, implying a reduction in active tightening pressure. This overall expansionary and easing monetary backdrop is a strong supportive factor for gold. [Source: us_liquidity]
  • Cross-Asset Positioning & Sentiment: Equity market volatility (VIX) is "CALM." However, specific commodity markets exhibit concentrated positioning risks: Metals are crowded long, Agriculture is crowded short, and a "Supply Stress" macro theme is active due to deep hedger long positions in Energy and Agriculture. Gold and silver themselves show extreme speculative short positioning. Light speculative positioning in gold is a tactical buy signal. [Source: cross_asset] [Source: global_growth]

Global Macro Themes

  • Global Growth: The global growth regime is characterized by "Resilient Industrial Demand with Defensive Precious Metals Positioning." A strong "Metals Tailwind" (copper residual) indicates robust underlying industrial demand. Conversely, gold and silver CoT show extreme speculative short positioning and ongoing long reduction, reflecting a defensive or hedging stance amidst broader macroeconomic uncertainty. This presents a mixed picture, with industrial strength acting as a headwind, but defensive positioning in gold itself providing a tactical opportunity. [Source: global_growth]
  • Global Trade & Demand: This theme highlights a "Weakening" USD regime and an active "Supply Stress" macro theme from positioning. Global positioning tone is "Neutral." The weakening USD generally eases global financial conditions and supports commodity prices, while "Supply Stress" in key commodity sectors implies physical market tightness. This environment is broadly supportive for gold as a hedge against supply-side commodity price pressures and a weakening fiat currency. [Source: global_trade]
  • Global Inflation & FX Dynamics: The Broad U.S. Dollar Index is in a "Weakening" regime, contributing to a global disinflationary bias. Speculative positioning in USD futures is significantly net short, creating high positioning tension with hedgers. While specific G10 FX like CAD show high tension, others are neutral. The weakening USD is a strong supportive factor for gold. [Source: global_fx]
