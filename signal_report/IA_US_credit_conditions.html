US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance.
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence.
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight tactical stress has transitioned to a Bearish classification (1).
  • Cyclical funding indicators remain in Neutral territory (2)(3) but show deteriorating internal dynamics.
  • The Private Credit Impulse is improving but remains within the stable transition zone (4).
  • The Financial Stress Composite was downweighted due to missing data and mixed signals (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Credit Spreads (2) and Financial Stress Composite (5) reinforce a view of low absolute funding costs.
  • Tension exists between high sentiment-driven stress (VIX) and low corporate funding spreads (3).
  • Tactical Bearish signals (1) oppose the improving cyclical credit momentum (4).
  • Tensions do not overturn the call as primary stress filters are actively flagging regime-shift risks (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistent neutrality in credit funding despite rising equity and sentiment volatility.
  • Primary upside risk: Entry into an "Accelerating" credit regime if Private Credit Impulse exceeds thresholds (4).
  • Primary downside risk: Spillover of sentiment stress into corporate funding markets, triggering a "Tightening" regime (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Credit Spread Composite z-score rising above 0.5, signalling a shift to TIGHTENING (2).
  • The Private Credit Impulse composite exceeding 0.75 for two consecutive periods (4).
  • Stress_z falling back below 0.5, returning tactical sentiment to Neutral (1).
