US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile (Deteriorating momentum within a neutral state)

Why This Regime:

  • Dominant drivers include corporate credit spreads and systemic stress indices, which have transitioned from "Easy" to "Normal/Neutral" levels (1)(2).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes signals with "High Interpretation Confidence" and "Medium Aggregation Weight," all of which confirm that funding costs remain historically low but lack expansionary momentum (3)(4).
  • The Private Credit Impulse signal was downweighted due to "Low Interpretation Confidence," "Internal Conflict," and missing data components for February 2026 (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • High-weight cyclical signals reinforce each other, showing credit spreads (HY OAS 2.98%) and systemic stress (STLFSI4 -0.60) anchored near long-run medians (1)(4).
  • Tension exists between equity-implied volatility (VIX z-score +0.50) and corporate bond spreads (z-scores -0.28 to -0.38), suggesting equity markets price more stress than credit markets (3).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as both remain within the Neutral classification thresholds (3).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Continued stability in corporate funding as spreads reflect standard risk compensation (1).
  • Primary Upside Risk: Shift to an Expansionary regime if the Credit Spreads Composite z-score drops below -0.5, indicating surplus liquidity (1).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Transition to Defensive/Tightening if credit momentum accelerates widening by 50bps over three months (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A move in the Credit Conditions smoothed index above +0.75, signaling active tightening (3).
  • Two consecutive monthly closes of the Private Credit Impulse above 0.75, confirming a shift to accelerating credit creation (5).
  • Re-inversion of the 10Y-2Y yield curve slope combined with systemic stress levels rising above 0.0 (2)(4).
