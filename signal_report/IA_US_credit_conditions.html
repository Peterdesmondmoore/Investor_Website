US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Primary drivers are high-confidence indicators showing a conflict between neutral cyclical anchors (2) and tactical bullish shifts (5).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes the High Confidence neutral composite (2) over the Accelerating credit impulse (4), which currently lacks two-period confirmation and full data.
  • Credit Spreads (1) and Credit Conditions (3) were downweighted due to low interpretation confidence and internal conflict between equity volatility and risk premia.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Favorable funding conditions and compressed corporate risk premia reinforce a low-stress environment (1)(5).
  • Internal tensions exist where elevated equity volatility (VIX) offsets tight credit spreads (3).
  • These tensions prevent a definitive risk-positive call, as broad cyclical composites remain within neutral historical ranges (2).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Range-biased price paths and mean reversion within current thresholds (2)(3).
  • Primary upside risk: Transition to an Easing/Risk-Positive regime triggered by a decline in equity volatility (3).
  • Primary downside risk: Systemic shock propagation triggered by a rapid 50 bps widening in high-yield spreads (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A reversal of the stress z-score to above -0.5, indicating a tactical return to higher systemic strain (5).
  • Failure of the credit impulse to maintain acceleration over two consecutive periods (4).
  • High Yield spreads breaching the 3.5% threshold for neutral credit availability (1).

Credit Spreads Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given HY_OAS at 2.86% and IG_OAS at 0.80%, this setup aligns with Upward-biased price paths and Compressed volatility, where the dominant risk is Trend continuation, not Mean reversion.
  • The signal reflects a low-stress credit environment characterized by abundant liquidity and low perceived default risk (1).
  • Conviction Band: Low; Interpretation Confidence: Low Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: Yes. Signal Stability Assessment: Improving; Threshold Proximity: Moderate; Revision Sensitivity: Unknown.

Methodology Applied:

  • A High Yield (HY) spread below 3.5% indicates abundant credit availability and low perceived default risk (1).
  • A rapid tightening or widening is assessed via a 50 bps move over a 3-month window (1).
  • The methodology prioritizes HY components for cyclical sensitivity and requires level/momentum agreement for high-confidence regime classification (1).
  • Data used: Credit Spreads Signal dataset (latest observation: 2026-04-30) (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the HY_OAS level of 2.86%, which sits well below the 3.5% threshold for "neutral" conditions (1).
  • Momentum shows stabilization after a brief expansion in March 2026 (3.28%), indicating a return to easy financial conditions (1).
  • Internal Tension: While raw levels suggest a risk-on regime, the absence of calculated CSC z-scores prevents full validation of the "EASY" classification (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: High Yield spreads rising by more than 50 bps within a 3-month period (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Favorable environment for equities and HY debt sustained by spreads below 3.5%.
  • Upside risk: Further compression toward historical lows if corporate profitability exceeds expectations.
  • Downside risk: Systemic shock propagation if spreads widen rapidly toward the 5.0% tightening threshold.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) as spreads lead asset volatility and capex trends (1).
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Low due to the absence of derived z-score and momentum metrics in the provided data.

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs the Pricing and Liquidity macro dimensions by measuring risk premia and credit availability (1).
  • Economic mechanism: Low spreads imply reduced liquidity compensation requirements and supportive corporate funding conditions (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Typically interacts with VIX and TED spreads; divergence between these and OAS can signal early credit deterioration (1).

Regime Context:

  • The regime appears persistent, with HY spreads remaining largely below 4% since late 2023 (1).
  • Direction of change: Improving (reversing the Q1 2026 widening).

Model Limitations:

  • Potential for bid-ask spread inflation during liquidity shocks to cause false regime flips (1).
  • Reporting lags of 1-2 days and potential vendor revisions (1).

Data & References:

  • Credit Spreads Signal (2026-04-30) (1).
  • Datapoints: HY_OAS (2.86), IG_OAS (0.80).
  • Integration with VIX and central bank reserve levels would improve signal reliability (1).

Credit Conditions Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a HY OAS of 2.86, BBB OAS of 1.00, and a VIX of 18.71, this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Trend continuation, as the composite remains within the methodology's neutral thresholds (1).
  • The signal is currently in a Neutral regime, indicating a balanced funding environment where spreads and volatility are within typical historical ranges (1).
  • Conviction Band: Low; Interpretation Confidence: Mixed Signals; Internal Conflict Flag: Yes. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Far; Revision Sensitivity: Moderate.

Methodology Applied:

  • Standardized z-scores for HY spreads, BBB spreads, and the VIX are averaged to define the composite index (1).
  • Values between -0.75 and 0.75 signify a Neutral environment with no strong directional bias (1).
  • Rising values indicate a transition toward risk-off regimes, while falling values suggest a move toward risk-on expansion (1).
  • Credit Conditions Signal: Last observation 2026-04-30.

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary drivers are divergent: credit spreads (HY Z: -0.57, BBB Z: -0.61) suggest easing, while equity volatility (VIX Z: 0.51) exerts a tightening impulse (1).
  • Momentum has stabilized near the zero-bound after a brief spike in risk aversion during March 2026 (1).
  • Internal Tension: Strong compression in corporate risk premia is being offset by elevated equity market uncertainty.
  • Conditional Invalidation: A composite breach of the 0.75 threshold would signal a shift to a Tightening regime.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Neutrality persists as tight credit spreads buffer moderate equity volatility.
  • Upside risk: A decline in the VIX aligns with credit spreads, triggering a transition to an Easing regime.
  • Downside risk: Credit spreads widen to match equity market uncertainty, forcing a Tightening regime shift.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) as the signal reflects medium-term corporate funding health and risk appetite (1).
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; the neutral state requires confirmation from liquidity or USD strength metrics (1).

Macro Relevance:

  • This signal informs the broad Liquidity and Pricing dimensions by measuring the cost and availability of corporate credit (1).
  • Economic mechanism: The current Neutral reading implies stable corporate financing costs and standard credit supply.
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Interaction: Typically used alongside USD strength and credit supply metrics to confirm financial regimes (1).

Regime Context:

  • Persistent Neutral regime; the signal has remained within the -0.75 to 0.75 range since October 2024 (1).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising following a recent mean-reversion from the March 2026 peak.

Model Limitations:

  • The VIX component may reflect equity-specific tail risks rather than underlying credit market fundamentals (1).
  • Static thresholds may not account for structural shifts in historical spread norms.

Data & References:

  • Credit Conditions Signal (2026-04-30): HY_OAS (2.86), BBB_OAS (1.00), VIX (18.71) (1).
  • Dominant influence: Convergence of tight credit spreads against a moderately elevated VIX.
  • Additional public datasets: Federal Reserve Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey (SLOOS) and USD Index (DXY).

Methodology Applied:
