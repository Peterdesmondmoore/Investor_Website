US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile due to late-cycle crowding and cross-asset volatility divergence.

Why This Regime:

  • High-yield spreads are at the 9th percentile, indicating extreme compression but limited room for further easing (1).
  • Broad financial stress metrics remain Neutral, reflecting stable but non-expansionary funding markets (2)(3).
  • Weighting prioritized the High Confidence "Neutral" signals from credit and funding composites over volatile tactical shifts (4).
  • The Private Credit Impulse was downweighted due to Low Confidence and material data gaps in early 2026 snapshots (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Credit spreads and financial stress indices align within historical medians, suggesting systemic stability (1)(2).
  • Tensions exist as rising equity volatility (VIX z: 0.83) conflicts with the complacency in anchored credit spreads (3)(6).
  • Inverted yield curve moderation reduces the composite stress contribution but remains a cyclical headwind (4)(7).
  • Tensions do not overturn the call because smoothed credit indices filter out transient equity-market noise (3).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant: Neutral funding conditions persist as long as credit spreads remain anchored near historical lows.
  • Primary upside risk: Broad-based credit expansion if C&I and consumer lending accelerate simultaneously (5).
  • Primary downside risk: Transition to Tightening triggered by equity volatility spilling into credit and liquidity premia (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A rise in the Credit Spreads Composite z-score above +0.5, signalling an upward drift in default premia (1).
  • A move in the Financial Stress Index level above 0.0, indicating acute funding market dysfunction (2).
  • A sustained decline in the Private Credit Impulse composite below the 0.75 threshold into deceleration (5).

Methodology Applied:

Scenario Balance:

  • Upside risk: A shift to a Bullish regime if the Stress_z falls below -0.5, indicating exceptionally loose liquidity.
  • Downside risk: Re-entry into a Bearish regime triggered by a sudden spike in STLFSI4 levels above 0.0.
