US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • State the regime clearly using one label.
  • State the Regime Confidence Index.
  • State whether the regime is stable or fragile.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is stable.

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant evidence from highly-weighted cyclical signals indicates stable financial and credit conditions. The Credit Spreads signal is in a NORMAL regime (1), suggesting stable corporate funding. Similarly, the Financial Stress Composite is Neutral (2), reflecting below-median stress across its components. These signals receive higher weighting due to their Medium Aggregation Weight Hints and High Interpretation Confidence. Their cyclical time horizon provides a foundational view of market conditions.
  • Signals such as the Credit Conditions (6), Private Credit Impulse (7), and Financial Stress Index (10) were downweighted. This was due to their Low Aggregation Weight Hints, Mixed Signals interpretation confidence, and presence of Internal Conflict Flags. The Financial Stress Index also has a Tactical time horizon, limiting its influence on the broader regime.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Credit Spreads (1) and Financial Stress Composite (2) signals strongly reinforce a non-stressed, stable environment. Even the downweighted Credit Conditions signal indicates Easing conditions (6), supporting a generally accommodative outlook. The Private Credit Impulse signals Stable credit growth overall (7).
  • Tensions exist with the Financial Stress Index signal, which is classified as Bearish due to its z-score (10). However, this tactical signal's underlying STLFSI4 level is negative, indicating looser-than-average conditions, creating an internal conflict (10).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call. The conflicting Financial Stress Index signal (10) carries a low weight due to its internal inconsistencies, mixed interpretation confidence, and tactical nature, making it insufficient to outweigh the consistent message from the higher-weighted cyclical signals.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Corporate funding conditions and broader financial stability remain normal to neutral. This supports continued economic activity and risk appetite.
  • Primary upside risk: A further decline in credit spreads or a steepening yield curve. This could push conditions back into an "Easy" or "Low Stress" regime, potentially driven by stronger economic data.
  • Primary downside risk: A material widening of credit spreads or a significant increase in the St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index. This would indicate increasing financial stress and a shift to a more defensive regime.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained rise in High Yield Option-Adjusted Spreads (HY OAS) above 3.5%, moving the Credit Spreads composite Z-score above +0.5 (1).
  • A sustained increase in the Financial Stress Composite Z-score above +0.75, signalling broad financial tightening (2).
  • A broad deceleration in private credit, with the Private Credit Impulse composite dropping below -0.75 (7).
