US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Regime Stability: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is predominantly driven by the Credit Conditions signal (1), which indicates an Easing regime with High Conviction and High Aggregation Weight. This points to a supportive funding environment.
  • The Financial Stress Index (2), also with a High Aggregation Weight, moved from a Bearish to a Neutral regime, signaling an easing of financial stress.
  • Other key signals, including the Financial Stress Composite (3) and Private Credit Impulse (4), are classified as Neutral or Stable, reinforcing the absence of systemic stress.
  • Signals were weighted qualitatively, prioritizing those with High Aggregation Weight Hints and High Interpretation Confidence.
  • The Credit Spreads signal (5) moved from an Easy to a Normal regime. This component was not explicitly downweighted but its transition from "Easy" suggests a maturation of very loose conditions rather than a deterioration.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Strong alignment is observed across financial stress indicators. The Credit Conditions signal (1) shows Easing conditions, supported by low High Yield Option-Adjusted Spreads (HY OAS) and subdued VIX (6).
  • This is reinforced by the Neutral readings from the Financial Stress Index (2) and Financial Stress Composite (3), which both confirm the absence of acute systemic stress.
  • A minor tension exists with the Credit Spreads signal (5), which transitioned from Easy to Normal. Additionally, the Private Credit Impulse (4) is Stable rather than Accelerating.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as "Normal" and "Stable" still represent supportive conditions. They indicate a mature, rather than aggressively expanding, phase of ease in credit conditions.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Corporate funding conditions remain stable and supportive, reflecting low credit spreads and contained financial stress.
  • Primary upside risk: A further decline in spreads or a renewed surge in risk appetite could lead to a deeper easing of credit conditions.
  • Primary downside risk: A material increase in systemic stress or widening corporate spreads, driven by economic deterioration, could shift conditions towards tightening.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained rise in High Yield OAS above 3.5% or a composite Z-score for Credit Spreads exceeding +0.5 (5).
  • The Credit Conditions composite rising above its -0.75 threshold or a sustained spike in the VIX (1).
  • The Financial Stress Index rising above 0 or its robust z-score exceeding +0.5, signaling a renewed increase in financial stress (2).
