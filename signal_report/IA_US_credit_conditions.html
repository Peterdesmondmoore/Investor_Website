US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • Dominant drivers are anchored corporate spreads and neutral systemic stress levels. (1)(2)
  • Weighting prioritised high-confidence cyclical signals over tactical volatility and missing data. (1)(3)
  • Credit Conditions were downweighted due to internal conflict and mixed signal interpretation. (4)
  • Private Credit Impulse was downweighted due to low confidence and incomplete data components. (5)

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Credit Spreads and the Financial Stress Index align in stable, neutral territory. (1)(3)
  • Tension exists between benign corporate spreads and elevated equity volatility. (4)
  • Credit creation momentum shows unconfirmed transition risk with volatile bank credit spikes. (5)
  • Core funding costs remain below stress thresholds, preventing a downside regime shift. (1)

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued Neutral regime as low spreads offset rising equity market uncertainty. (4)
  • Primary upside risk: Transition to Low Stress triggered by VIX mean-reversion. (2)
  • Primary downside risk: Shift to Tightening triggered by spreads reacting to volatility stress. (4)

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A rise in high-yield OAS above 3.38%, marking a 50 bps three-month increase. (1)
  • A broad financial stress composite print exceeding a +0.75 z-score. (2)
  • Two consecutive monthly credit impulse readings above the 0.75 acceleration threshold. (5)

  • Conviction Band: Low; Interpretation Confidence: Mixed Signals; Internal Conflict Flag: Yes. Signal Stability Assessment: Volatile; Threshold Proximity: Moderate; Revision Sensitivity: Unknown.

Methodology Applied:

  • Composite Construction: Arithmetic mean of robust z-scores for HY_OAS, BBB_OAS, and VIXCLS, smoothed by a 3-month EMA. (1)
  • Regime Thresholds: Values > +0.75 indicate Tightening (stress); < -0.75 indicate Easing (supportive). (1)
  • Driver Diagnosis: Distinguishes between spread-led (funding supply) and VIX-led (risk aversion/uncertainty) movements. (1)
  • Dataset: FRED Credit Spreads and CBOE VIX; latest observation: 2026-02-28. (1)

Key Dynamics:

  • Main Drivers: Low corporate spreads (HY z-score -0.418) are being challenged by rising equity volatility (VIX z-score +0.754), creating an internal offset. (1)
  • Momentum: The raw index has moved from -1.09 (Nov 2024) to -0.05 (Feb 2026), indicating a steady erosion of "Easing" conditions. (1)
  • Internal Conflict: Strong divergence between credit market stability and equity market uncertainty.
  • Conditional Invalidation: A sustained move in the smoothed Credit_Conditions index above +0.75.
  • Signal Stability: Volatile; the smoothed index is trending upward towards the tightening boundary.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Credit conditions remain neutral as spread compression offsets VIX spikes.
  • Upside risk: Return to Easing regime if VIX mean-reverts while spreads remain pinned.
  • Downside risk: Tightening regime entry if corporate spreads catch up to equity volatility stress.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) due to the 3-month EMA smoothing of corporate and volatility inputs.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; credit conditions are a primary filter for risk-asset appetite but currently lack directional alignment.

Macro Relevance:

  • Macro dimension: Funding liquidity and systemic risk appetite.
  • Economic mechanism: Measures "excess bond premium"—the gap between fundamental default risk and investor risk compensation. (1)
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Interaction: Typically leads business cycle fluctuations; current neutral state suggests no immediate credit-driven contraction.

Regime Context:

  • Persistent Neutral; the signal exited the Easing regime in March 2025 and has remained Neutral for 12 months.
  • Direction of change: Weakening (moving from deep negative values toward the zero-line and tightening threshold).

Model Limitations:

  • VIX spikes may reflect temporary variance risk premia rather than fundamental credit deterioration. (1)
  • Static ±0.75 thresholds are heuristic and may not capture non-linear stress transitions.

Data & References:

Financial Stress Index Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement (mandatory): Given STLFSI4 at -0.6208 and a robust Stress_z of -0.002, this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Trend continuation.
  • The signal is currently in a Neutral regime, having drifted from a Bearish (risk-off) state in late 2025 as systemic stress levels converged with their long-run median (1).
  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No.

Methodology Applied:

  • Regime classification: Combines absolute level (STLFSI4 threshold 0) and robust z-score (threshold +/- 0.5) to define Bullish, Neutral, or Bearish states (1).
  • Metric standardisation: Uses rolling 156-week median and MAD to compute robust z-scores, minimizing sensitivity to crisis outliers (1).
  • Pillar Decomposition: Optionally evaluates Volatility (VIX), Credit (HY OAS), and Liquidity (TED) to identify specific drivers of financial stress (1).
  • Latest dataset observation: 2026-02-13.

Key Dynamics:

  • The STLFSI4 level remains negative (-0.6208), but the Stress_z has moderated significantly from the +1.68 peaks seen in November 2025 (1).
  • Stabilisation: The z-score of -0.002 indicates that financial conditions are perfectly aligned with the multi-year average, suggesting a lack of directional momentum.
  • The signal has fully retraced its Bearish regime from Q4 2025, moving through a series of "Neutral" weekly closes (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A sustained move in Stress_z below -0.5 would trigger a Bullish (risk-on) transition (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Systemic stress remains near median levels, supporting range-bound risk assets.
  • Upside risk trigger: Rapid easing in credit spreads and volatility pushing z-score into the Bullish zone (< -0.5).
  • Downside risk trigger: A spike in interbank funding stress or implied volatility pushing STLFSI4 above 0 (2).

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Tactical (weeks); the signal utilizes a weekly Friday cadence to capture rapid shifts in systemic risk (1).
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; this serves as a high-level barometer that requires confirmation from specific credit or liquidity signals (3).

Macro Relevance:

  • Macro dimension: Market Liquidity and Risk Sentiment.
  • Cycle position: Late-cycle stabilisation or mid-cycle consolidation.
  • Interaction: Typically leads or confirms shifts in the broader Credit Spreads Signal and VIX Market Volatility regimes (3)(2).

Data & References:

  • STLFSI4 (St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index), latest observation: 2026-02-13 (1).
  • Datapoint Influence: The Stress_z value of -0.002 is the primary driver of the current Neutral regime classification.
  • Related Datasets: ICE BofA High Yield OAS (BAMLH0A0HYM2) for credit pillar depth and VIXCLS for volatility context (3)(2).
