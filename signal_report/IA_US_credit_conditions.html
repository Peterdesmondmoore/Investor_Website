US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: Fragile Balance

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the accelerating private sector credit growth, which signals a transition to a risk-on financing environment (1).
  • Corporate funding remains stable with credit spreads holding in a normal, range-bound regime (2).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized these High-Weight, High-Confidence signals over lower-weight tactical indicators.
  • The Financial Stress Index (3) and Stress Composite (4) were downweighted due to Low Confidence ratings and missing data sub-components.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Alignment exists between accelerating credit creation (1) and stable High Yield spreads (2), supporting cyclical expansion.
  • A significant tension is identified in equity volatility, where a spike in VIX momentum conflicts with stable credit spreads (5).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime because financing costs have not yet restricted liquidity or triggered a regime shift in core credit markers (5).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Sustained cyclical expansion driven by easing bank credit availability and healthy corporate demand.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Further compression in credit spreads leading to an "Easy" regime if benchmark risk allocations fall (2).
  • Primary Downside Risk: A sentiment-led shock where credit spreads widen to "catch up" with rising equity volatility (5).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in the Private Credit Impulse composite below 0.75 for two consecutive periods (1).
  • A move in High Yield spreads above 3.67%, representing a 50bps widening from current levels (2).

Credit Spreads Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a HY_OAS of 3.17%, an IG_OAS of 0.86%, and a CSC_z of -0.08, this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Regime shift (1).
  • The signal is currently in a NORMAL regime, characterized by credit conditions aligned with historical averages and stable risk premia (1).
  • Conviction Band: High; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Near (HY level); Revision Sensitivity: Moderate.

Methodology Applied:

  • NORMAL regime is defined by the CSC z-score remaining between -0.5 and 0.5, implying neutral benchmark risk allocations (1).
  • TIGHTENING risks are triggered if HY spreads rise >50bps in 3 months or the CSC z-score exceeds 0.5 (1).
  • The methodology prioritizes High Yield (HY) components for cyclical sensitivity and default risk expectations (1).
  • Dataset: ICE BofA US High Yield and Corporate Master OAS; Latest observation: 2026-04-30 (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the stabilization of HY_OAS at 3.17%, which sits just below the 3.5% threshold for EASY conditions but is held in NORMAL by the neutral CSC_z of -0.08 (1).
  • Momentum is currently flat; the 3-month annualized momentum z-scores (hy_d3m_ann_z: 0.81) have moderated significantly from the 2025-04 peak (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A rise in HY spreads above 3.67% (a 50bps move from current) would trigger a TIGHTENING regime (1).
  • Signal stability is high, as the regime has persisted in the NORMAL zone for 12 consecutive months following the brief 2025 volatility spike (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Continued range-bound spreads supported by stable corporate default expectations.
  • Upside risk: Transition to EASY regime if CSC_z drops below -0.5 amid further yield compression.
  • Downside risk: Shift to TIGHTENING triggered by a sudden liquidity shock or 50bps widening in HY spreads.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months); credit regimes typically persist across multi-month windows.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: High; credit spreads are a core pillar for pricing cyclical risk and leverage.

Macro Relevance:

  • Dimension: US Credit Conditions and systemic funding stress (1).
  • Economic Mechanism: Measures corporate borrowing costs relative to risk-free rates; current stability implies low margin pressure and healthy liquidity compensation (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined by methodology.
  • Typically confirms equity market volatility (VIX) and broader liquidity/funding indicators (1).

Regime Context:

  • The current NORMAL regime is persistent, having been maintained since May 2025 (1).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising after the significant TIGHTENING episode in April 2025 (1).

Model Limitations:

  • 1-2 business day reporting lag and potential for bid-ask spreads to artificially inflate levels during shocks (1).

Data & References:

  • Latest Data: 2026-04-30 (Fresh).
  • Influential points: CSC_z (-0.08) and HY_OAS (3.17%) define the current regime boundary (1).
  • Additional datasets: VIX Index and Federal Reserve Liquidity proxies would improve divergence analysis.

Methodology Applied:

  • Indices between -0.75 and 0.75 indicate a Neutral funding environment with range-bound stress (1).
  • Component z-scores must be decomposed to distinguish between funding-driven stress (spreads) and sentiment-driven stress (VIX) (1).
  • Values approaching +/- 0.75 are treated as transitional states rather than binary outcomes (1).
  • Latest observation: 2026-04-30.

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is a sharp spike in VIX momentum (Z_VIX: 1.67), reflecting high sentiment-driven stress (2).
  • Internal conflict exists as HY and BBB corporate spreads remain near historical averages (Z_HY: -0.01; Z_BBB: -0.17) (3).
  • Conditional Invalidation: An Index print above 0.75 would confirm a regime shift to "Tightening."
  • Signal stability is deteriorating, with the composite index rising from -0.10 to 0.38 over the last three periods.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Funding remains available despite rising equity volatility, keeping the regime neutral.
  • Upside risk: Rapid mean reversion of VIX if sentiment stabilizes without infecting credit markets.
  • Downside risk: Credit spreads widen to "catch up" with VIX, triggering a systematic tightening event.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Tactical (weeks); current stress is dominated by the fast-moving VIX component.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; neutral readings require confirmation from secondary liquidity or supply signals.

Macro Relevance:

  • This signal informs the US Credit Conditions (4) macro dimension.
  • The economic mechanism is currently "Sentiment-driven stress," as financing costs (spreads) have not yet moved to restrict liquidity.
  • Cycle position: Not determined.

Regime Context:

  • The current Neutral regime is persistent but weakening toward the tightening threshold.
  • Direction of change: Strengthening stress (transitioning from Easing to Neutral to Tightening-proximate).

Model Limitations:

  • EMA(3) smoothing introduces lag, potentially masking the speed of the recent VIX-led spike (1).
  • VIX includes non-credit equity variance, which may overstate actual funding stress (1).

Data & References:
