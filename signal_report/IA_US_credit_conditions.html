US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Medium Confidence
  • Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • A High-weight expansion in private credit velocity (4) is countered by High-weight tactical stress spikes (5).
  • Credit spreads have breached the momentum threshold, transitioning into a newly entered Tightening regime (1).
  • The Credit Conditions signal was downweighted due to internal conflict between equity volatility and corporate spreads (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Financial Stress Index (5) and widening HY spreads (1) reinforce a shift toward risk-negative conditions.
  • Primary tension exists between robust cyclical credit creation (4) and deteriorating broad funding stability (2).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime call as high-weight indicators are currently balanced between expansionary flows and tightening risk premia.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued spread widening as default risk reprices higher amid elevated systemic stress (1)(5).
  • Primary upside risk: Stabilization of funding markets and mean reversion to a Normal regime (1).
  • Primary downside risk: Transition to a "Stressed" regime if high-yield spreads exceed 6.5% (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • High-yield spreads falling more than 50 basis points over a three-month window (1).
  • Two consecutive monthly private credit impulse prints falling below the 0.75 expansionary threshold (4).

Methodology Applied:

  • Directionality: Rising values imply risk-off transitions, tightening financial conditions, and potential economic slowdown (1).
  • Validation: Methodology requires confirmation with market volatility (VIX) and liquidity indicators (2)(3).
  • Credit Spreads Signal: Latest observation March 31, 2026 (1).

  • Downside risk: Transition to "Stressed" regime if HY OAS exceeds 6.5% or CSC z-score surpasses 1.0 (1).

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; the signal effectively flags turning points in corporate funding stress (1).

  • The economic mechanism is the increasing cost of corporate borrowing, implying margin pressure and potential capex contraction (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
