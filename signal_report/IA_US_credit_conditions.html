US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Low Confidence
  • Status: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the Financial Stress Index Signal (1), which maintains a high-weight Bearish regime with high conviction.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes this systemic stress indicator over neutral cyclical readings from the Financial Stress Composite (2) and Credit Conditions signal (3).
  • The Private Credit Impulse (4) was downweighted due to low interpretation confidence, internal data conflicts, and missing sub-pillar data for the latest observation.
  • Credit Spreads (5) were slightly downweighted due to mixed interpretation confidence despite showing significant momentum toward easing.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Financial Stress Composite (2) and Credit Conditions (3) reinforce a neutral baseline for funding liquidity.
  • Significant tension exists between the high-weight tactical stress reading (1) and the unconfirmed cyclical breakout in credit growth (4).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime because the most conservative label is required when high-weight signals indicate persistent risk-off pressure (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Continued range-bound volatility with a persistent risk-off bias as systemic stress lingers.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Transition to Expansionary if the VIX reverts to sub-15 levels while spreads remain tight (3).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Transition to Defensive if corporate credit spreads widen by more than 50bps over three months (5).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in the Financial Stress Index Robust Z-score below the 0.5 threshold (1).
  • Two consecutive periods of the Private Credit Impulse remaining above 0.75 with full data confirmation (4).
  • The Credit Spreads composite (CSC_z) moving below -0.5, signaling an entry into the EASY regime (5).
