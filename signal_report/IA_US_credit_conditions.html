US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance.
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence.
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Systemic stress has breached the +0.5 z-score threshold, shifting into a Bearish classification (1).
  • Cyclical credit spreads show accelerating momentum with a 3-month z-score of 1.03 (2).
  • Tactical sentiment-driven stress is currently testing the tightening boundary (3).
  • The Private Credit Impulse was downweighted due to internal conflict and missing consumer credit data (4).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals reinforce a trend of rising systemic and funding pressure across different horizons (1)(5).
  • Tension exists as nominal credit spreads remain within historical norms despite rising volatility (2)(3).
  • Tensions do not overturn the call because high-weight tactical indicators are already signaling a bearish transition (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistent stress deterioration as credit momentum tests historical thresholds.
  • Primary upside risk: Return to "Normal" stability if equity volatility (VIX) mean-reverts sharply.
  • Primary downside risk: Transition to "Risk-Negative" if high-yield spreads breach the 3.5% methodology threshold (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • Systemic stress z-score falling back below the 0.5 threshold (1).
  • A reversal in credit-spread momentum metrics toward negative z-scores (2).
