US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Low Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the Credit Spreads Signal (1), which remains in a stable "Normal" regime with high conviction.
  • This stability is challenged by the Private Credit Impulse (2), which has shifted into an "Accelerating" cyclical regime.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized the high-confidence spread data but acknowledges the divergence from tactical stress metrics, which have moved into "Bearish" territory (3).
  • The Financial Stress Composite (4) was downweighted due to Low Interpretation Confidence, internal conflicts, and missing sub-component data.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • High Yield and BBB corporate spreads reinforce each other, remaining near historical averages (1)(5).
  • A major tension exists between accelerating credit volume (2) and a sharp spike in equity-linked sentiment stress (6).
  • Tensions do not yet overturn the regime because corporate funding costs have not widened to levels that restrict liquidity (1)(5).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued range-bound spreads supported by stable corporate default expectations (1).
  • Primary upside risk: Transition to a Risk-Positive regime if tactical stress fades while credit acceleration persists (2).
  • Primary downside risk: Transition to Defensive/Tightening if credit spreads "catch up" to the recent volatility spike (5).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A rise in High Yield spreads above 3.67%, representing a 50bps widening from current levels (1).
  • The Private Credit Impulse composite falling below the 0.75 acceleration threshold for two consecutive periods (2).

  • The methodology prioritizes High Yield (HY) components for cyclical sensitivity and default risk expectations (1).

  • Conditional Invalidation: A rise in HY spreads above 3.67% (a 50bps move from current) would trigger a TIGHTENING regime (1).
  • Signal stability is high, as the regime has persisted in the NORMAL zone for 12 consecutive months following the brief 2025 volatility spike (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Continued range-bound spreads supported by stable corporate default expectations.
  • Upside risk: Transition to EASY regime if CSC_z drops below -0.5 amid further yield compression.
  • Downside risk: Shift to TIGHTENING triggered by a sudden liquidity shock or 50bps widening in HY spreads.

  • Aggregation Weight Hint: High; credit spreads are a core pillar for pricing cyclical risk and leverage.

  • The current NORMAL regime is persistent, having been maintained since May 2025 (1).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising after the significant TIGHTENING episode in April 2025 (1).

Model Limitations:
