US Credit Conditions
Integrates credit spreads, systemic-stress indicators, and private-credit growth signals to assess the prevailing US credit-conditions regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The environment is defined by abundant credit availability and accelerating private sector credit creation (1)(4).
  • High Yield spreads below 3.5% indicate low perceived default risk and easing corporate funding conditions (1).
  • Primary cyclical drivers moved toward an accelerating regime in early 2026 (4).
  • Credit Spreads and Private Credit Impulse were downweighted due to internal conflicts and low interpretation confidence (1)(4).
  • The assessment relies on the stable Neutral baseline established by broad cyclical composites (2)(3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals reinforce each other through normalized financial stress levels and positive yield curve slopes (2)(5).
  • Credit conditions have stabilized near the zero-line after a brief period of tightening in March (3).
  • Tension exists as the private credit acceleration relies on incomplete April bank data (4).
  • The Credit Spreads signal currently lacks composite z-score validation (1).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the broader trend reflects systemic stabilization (5).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Risk-on expansion supported by spreads remaining below 3.5% (1).
  • Primary upside risk: Further spread compression toward historical floors triggered by corporate profitability (1).
  • Primary downside risk: Re-tightening toward High Stress regimes triggered by sudden yield curve re-inversion (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • High Yield spreads rising above 3.5% or increasing 50 bps in a rolling 3-month window (1).
  • The Private Credit Impulse composite falling below 0.75 for two consecutive months (4).
  • The Financial Stress Index Robust Z-score rising back above the 0.5 risk-off threshold (5).
