Gemini Summary

The market is transitioning into a period of notable liquidity expansion, as indicated by the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite moving from a "Neutral" to "Expansion" regime since July 2025. The latest Composite value is 1.30 as of November 30, 2025. This expansion is predominantly fueled by a significant decline in Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage, reflected in a "Bullish" signal (inverted RRP YoY at -96.17%). Despite persistent "Bearish" signals from Federal Reserve Total Assets (YoY -5.11%) and Reserve Balances (YoY -10.26%), suggesting ongoing quantitative tightening, the substantial RRP unwinding is providing systemic liquidity, fostering an easing financial backdrop.

Technical Summary: Technically, the sustained shift to a "Liquidity Expansion" regime since mid-2025, with the Composite consistently above 0.75, suggests a supportive environment for risk assets like equities and industrial metals. The strong "Bullish" contribution from ON RRP (indicating substantial liquidity injection) is a key technical driver, effectively counteracting the "Bearish" pressure from the Fed's balance sheet reduction and reserve drains. This implies underlying demand for liquidity is being met, leading to an overall easing of conditions. The recent moderation of the M2 signal to "Neutral" on 2025-11-30 warrants monitoring but doesn't immediately negate the broader expansionary trend.
