The Lithium Composite Pricing Signal aggregates multiple lithium equity pricing signals into a single, coherent view of lithium market pricing dynamics. Lithium Pricing Regime — Qualitative Interpretation

Executive Summary

Lithium pricing behavior is currently characterized by a widespread loss of short-term momentum across all observed cohorts. While most participants have broken below their 50-day moving averages, the Miner cohort remains positioned above long-term 200-day trends (1)(2). The Project cohort exhibits the most significant deterioration and internal dispersion, with specific participants trading near 52-week lows (3). Technology and processing signals align with the general breakdown of short-term support while experiencing elevated realized volatility (5). Overall, the regime appears transitional as participation in the prior trend narrows and internal consistency among cohorts diminishes (4).

Cross-Cohort Alignment

Cohorts are currently partially aligned. All three groups—Miners (1), Projects (3), and Technology (5)—show a consistent breakdown below 50-day averages. However, they are divergent regarding long-term support, with Miners leading by maintaining levels above 200-day averages while specific Project cohort members lag significantly, testing annual lows (3).
