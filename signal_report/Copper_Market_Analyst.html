Current View: The institutional outlook for copper prices in 2026 has shifted significantly upward over the last quarter. Recent data indicates a median price target of $12,511 per tonne, representing a substantial increase from the prior median of $11,500. This upward distribution reflects a growing consensus among major banks that supply constraints and emerging demand drivers are outstripping previous expectations of a market surplus.

Recent Predictions: Forecasts issued in April 2026 demonstrate high conviction in elevated price levels. ING Economics & Commodities maintains a particularly bullish stance, targeting a Q2 2026 peak of $13,500 per tonne and an annual average of $12,500. Similarly, RBC Capital Markets has projected a 2026 average of approximately $12,963 per tonne, citing support from macroeconomic factors such as inflation expectations and "HALO" trade support alongside structural deficits.

Historical Context and Shifts: While recent forecasts are aggressively bullish, they contrast with earlier 2026 outlooks published in late 2025 and early January 2026. For instance, Goldman Sachs previously forecast a decline to $11,000 per tonne by the end of 2026, anticipating that a refined supply surplus and fading tariff risks would eventually weigh on record highs. However, the subsequent move by institutions like BMO Capital Markets and Deutsche Bank toward targets exceeding $12,125 suggests that the market has pivoted toward a "higher-for-longer" narrative driven by immediate supply shocks.

Key Market Drivers: The current upside bias is consistently attributed to a combination of supply-side disruptions and structural demand shifts. Institutions highlight severe mine disruptions and tight refined supply as the primary catalysts for current deficits. On the demand side, the rapid expansion of AI data centers, global grid modernization, and the ongoing energy transition are viewed as resilient drivers that are less sensitive to traditional cyclical headwinds.

Data Comparability and Limitations: The dataset presents a mix of direct bank point forecasts, such as those from J.P. Morgan and Citigroup, and broader survey averages from the CME Group. While point forecasts are currently clustered between $12,000 and $13,500, the CME Group’s survey average of $12,511 reflects a wider underlying range from $11,023 to $14,000. Furthermore, industry bodies like the International Copper Study Group (ICSG) and the IEA provide essential context regarding refined deficits and mid-stream smelter pressures but do not issue specific price targets, limiting direct numeric comparison with banking institutions.

Outlook Conviction: The outlook is characterized by high conviction in near-term volatility with a strong upward bias. The shift in the target distribution over the last 90 days suggests that analysts are increasingly discounting the "surplus" scenarios envisioned at the start of the year. Instead, the focus has moved to a persistent deficit environment where trade-flow distortions and geopolitical uncertainties act as additional risk premiums on top of fundamental supply shortages.
