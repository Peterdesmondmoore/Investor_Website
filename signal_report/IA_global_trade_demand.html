Global Trade and Demand
Assesses global trade intensity and capital flows by combining USD regime signals, macro positioning (e.g., supply stress), and speculative risk tone to characterise how currency dynamics and demand conditions influence cross-border trade and investment activity.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Status: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is a Strong Risk-On regime in global positioning breadth, evidenced by a robust z-score of 2.03 (1).
  • This is reinforced by the cyclical weakening of the U.S. dollar, which remains below its 12-month moving average (2).
  • High weights were assigned to the USD Index for its cyclical relevance and the Global Positioning Tone due to its extreme conviction and high confidence (1)(2).
  • The "Supply Stress" theme was qualitatively downweighted as a secondary narrative filter because it represents tactical physical market tightness rather than a broad regime shift (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Strong alignment exists between extreme speculative breadth and the durable bearish trend in the U.S. dollar (1)(2).
  • A tension exists where physical shortages in energy and agricultural markets indicate supply-side stress (3).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime call because high speculative appetite and easing dollar conditions currently override physical market constraints (1)(2).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant: Broad speculative alignment and a weakening dollar support continued upward pressure on risk assets.
  • Primary upside risk: A short-covering squeeze in lagging sectors accelerates the aggregate risk-on move (1).
  • Primary downside risk: Supply-side tightness transitions into a broader "Inflation Impulse," challenging the current expansion (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The U.S. dollar Index closing above its 12-month moving average, currently 120.91 (2).
  • A collapse in global speculative breadth that pushes the net tone z-score below the 1.0 threshold (1).
