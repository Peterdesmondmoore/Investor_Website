Global Trade and Demand
Assesses global trade intensity and capital flows by combining USD regime signals, macro positioning (for example, supply stress), and speculative risk tone.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance.
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence.
  • Status: Fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the conflict between positive speculative sentiment and emerging supply-side friction.
  • Global positioning maintains a Risk-On tone (1) supported by a weakening U.S. Dollar (2).
  • However, high-weighted cyclical signals show intense Supply Stress in Energy and Agriculture (3), which acts as a cost-push headwind.
  • Weighting prioritized the "Deteriorating" stability of the positioning tone and the recent volatility in USD signals over tactical momentum (1)(2).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Positioning breadth and USD weakness reinforce a pro-cyclical bias (1)(2).
  • Significant tension exists in Metals, where rising speculative shorts suggest emerging growth concerns (3).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime call because the net positioning tone remains positive and the USD discount to its 12-month average persists (1)(2).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Continued risk-positive appetite contingent on USD remaining below its moving average.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Re-acceleration of positioning breadth (z-score > 1.0) alongside falling Energy stress.
  • Primary Downside Risk: Metals shorts expand into a broad Global Slowdown regime, triggered by growth deceleration (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The CoT Market Tone z-score falls below 0.5, shifting the environment to Neutral (1).
  • The USD Index closes above its 12-month moving average of 120.02, tightening financial conditions (2).
  • A sharp decline in Energy hedger deep long share, invalidating the Supply Stress thesis (3).

Global Risk-On / Risk-Off Positioning Tone

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a CoT Market Tone z-score of 0.93 and a net tone of 0.040 across 372 markets, this setup aligns with Upward-biased price paths and Indeterminate volatility, where the dominant risk is Trend continuation, not Regime shift.
  • The signal currently occupies the Risk-On regime, reflecting positive speculative sentiment and a favorable environment for pro-cyclical assets (1).
  • Conviction Band: High; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Deteriorating; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: Unknown.

Methodology Applied:

  • Speculative sentiment is classified as "Risk-On" when the CoT Market Tone z-score is between 0.5 and 1.0 (1).
  • The signal measures the net ratio of crowded long versus crowded short positions to identify global speculative appetite (1).
  • Rising z-score values indicate a transition toward risk-on/expansion; falling values indicate a transition toward risk-off/slowdown (1).
  • Global Risk-On / Risk-Off Positioning Tone dataset; latest observation: 2026-04-21 (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the breadth of crowded long positions (95) exceeding crowded shorts (80), maintaining a positive net tone (1).
  • The signal shows weakening momentum, having recently decelerated from the "Strong Risk-On" threshold (z > 1.0) held throughout February and March (1).
  • Internal coherence is high as both long share and net tone support the Risk-On classification without divergence.
  • Conditional Invalidation: The interpretation reverses if the CoT Market Tone z-score falls below 0.5, signalling a shift to a Neutral speculative environment (1).
  • Signal stability is deteriorating, as the z-score has declined from a peak of 1.92 in late March to 0.93 currently (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Positive speculative appetite continues to support pro-cyclical assets as long as the z-score holds above 0.5.
  • Upside risk: Speculative re-acceleration above the 1.0 z-score threshold, triggered by a renewed expansion in crowded long breadth.
  • Downside risk: A transition to Neutral or Risk-Off regimes if crowded short positions begin to outpace long liquidations.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Tactical (weeks); methodology defines the signal as a sentiment filter for tactical allocation (1).
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: High; the signal aggregates positioning across a broad universe of 372 futures markets (1).

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs global speculative sentiment and the breadth of risk appetite across asset classes (1).
  • The economic mechanism is a liquidity-driven demand impulse, where broad long alignment facilitates capital flows into equities and commodities.
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Typically requires confirmation from VIX or credit signals to validate the sustainability of the positioning tone (1).

Regime Context:

  • The Risk-On regime has been persistent since late January 2026, though it has recently transitioned from "Strong" to moderate intensity (1).
  • Direction of change: Weakening (moving toward the Neutral boundary).

Model Limitations:

  • May mask sector-specific positioning divergences and is sensitive to thin liquidity in specific markets (1).
  • Revision window cannot be assessed from provided content.

Data & References:

  • Latest observation: 2026-04-21 (1).
  • Influential datapoints: CoT_Market_Tone_z (0.93) and net_tone (0.040).
  • Volatility (VIX) and USD strength datasets would improve interpretation reliability as defined in the methodology (1).

USD Index Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given the USD Index value of 118.08 remaining below its 12-month moving average of 120.02, this setup aligns with Downward-biased price paths and Compressed volatility, where the dominant risk is a Regime shift back to persistent weakness, not trend continuation (1).
  • The signal is currently classified as Bearish, indicating a weakening U.S. Dollar regime following a brief one-month Bullish deviation in March 2026 (1).
  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Volatile; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: Low.

Methodology Applied:

  • Regime classification: A Weakening (Bearish) signal is triggered when the index is below its 12-month moving average or the 6-month change is below -5% (1).
  • Economic interpretation: Bullish signals imply tightening financial conditions, while Bearish signals imply easing conditions and a risk-on bias (1).
  • Dataset: Broad trade-weighted U.S. dollar index; latest observation April 1, 2026 (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the price position relative to the 12-month MA, currently at a -1.6% discount (1).
  • Negative momentum is present (-2.72% over 6 months) but has not yet reached the -5% "reinforcing" threshold defined in the methodology (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: An index close above 120.02 or a 6-month growth rate exceeding +5% (1).
  • Stability is currently low as the signal transitioned from Bearish to Bullish and back to Bearish within a three-month window (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant base case: Continued USD weakness and easing financial conditions as the index stays below its trailing average.
  • Upside risk: A shift to a Bullish regime triggered by a sustained move above the 120.73 level (MA hysteresis).
  • Downside risk: Accelerated USD decline if the 6-month momentum breaches the -5% reinforcing threshold.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) due to the 12-month smoothing and 6-month momentum windows.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: High; the USD serves as a primary proxy for global liquidity and risk sentiment (1).

Macro Relevance:

  • The signal informs global liquidity and risk appetite dimensions (1).
  • Transmission mechanism: Dollar weakness provides tailwinds for commodities and non-USD assets by easing financial conditions (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined by methodology.

Regime Context:

  • The current Bearish regime was re-entered in April 2026, following a 10-month Bearish streak that ended in February (1).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising in a Weakening state.

Model Limitations:

  • Lagging nature due to the use of moving averages and multi-month momentum windows (1).
  • Potential for sampling noise at end-of-month intervals (1).
