Macro Advisor: Portfolio Guidance

This report synthesises current global and US macro regimes with the portfolio's structural characteristics to provide regime-based allocation guidance, focusing on implications for growth, commodities, and real assets.

CURRENT MACRO REGIME

The prevailing macro state is characterized by late-cycle moderation globally and in the US, with disinflationary pressures taking hold alongside expansionary liquidity conditions, primarily from the unwinding of the US Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) facility. While core manufacturing shows some resilience, the labor market exhibits underlying softening and elevated slack. Credit conditions remain generally benign, but global trade is contending with persistent "Supply Stress" from commodities, leading to upward pricing pressures despite the broader disinflationary trend. Cross-asset positioning indicates normalized volatility overall, yet with concentrated speculative crowding in specific sectors, and a nuanced US Dollar dynamic showing an official "Bullish" signal amidst underlying weakening metrics.

PORTFOLIO ALIGNMENT DIAGNOSTIC

  • Where the portfolio is aligned:
    • The portfolio's core growth equity and thematic exposures are aligned with expansionary liquidity conditions and declining real yields, which typically support valuation multiples.
    • Existing moderate-to-high allocations to commodities and real assets provide an explicit inflation hedge and exposure to the persistent "Supply Stress" theme, offering ballast against commodity-driven pricing pressures.
    • The elevated cash position provides optionality to deploy capital during potential market dislocations or to enhance defensive posture.
  • Where it is vulnerable:
    • High exposure to public equities, particularly in technology and high-beta thematic sectors, renders the portfolio highly sensitive to potential further deceleration in global growth or a pronounced shift in risk sentiment.
    • The deliberate underweight in traditional bonds limits duration-based diversification and downside protection in a scenario of sharp economic contraction or flight-to-safety.
    • Significant embedded USD exposure creates mixed effects; while it can support precious metals, a strengthening USD could exert pressure on EM growth exposures.
    • High-growth technology assets remain sensitive to valuation compression if inflation were to re-accelerate and force aggressive monetary tightening.
  • What macro channels matter most for this portfolio:
    • Liquidity expansion versus contraction, and the trajectory of real rates.
    • The overall level and volatility of the US Dollar, particularly its impact on EM and commodity prices.
    • Global growth breadth and the dynamics of international trade, especially in relation to industrial demand.
    • The evolution of inflation, and its implications for monetary policy.
    • Changes in cross-asset risk sentiment and persistence of "Supply Stress" in commodities.
    • Credit spreads and the private credit impulse as indicators of financial stability.

TACTICAL TILTS (0–90 days)

  • Asset classes that benefit from the current regime:
    • Public Equities (Developed & Emerging Markets), particularly innovation-led segments, supported by easing real yields and expansionary liquidity.
    • Commodities and Precious Metals, benefiting from underlying "Supply Stress" and a potentially weakening USD trend.
    • Real Assets / Resource Equities, supported by ongoing energy transition themes and metals demand.
  • Asset classes that face near-term risk:
    • High-beta thematic equity exposures if late-cycle moderation accelerates into a deeper slowdown.
    • Certain Emerging Market exposures, if the underlying USD weakening trend reverses into strength.
    • Specific commodity types exhibiting concentrated crowding, due to tactical reversal risks.
  • Regimes where caution is warranted:
    • A pronounced and broad-based deterioration in global labor market conditions.
    • A rapid and sustained re-acceleration of the US Dollar.
    • An intensification of commodity supply stress leading to a resurgence of headline inflation.
    • An abrupt reversal or fading of the current expansionary liquidity impulse.
    • Significant widening of credit spreads, particularly in high-yield segments.
  • Regimes where upside asymmetry exists:
    • A sustained and broadening rebound in global industrial demand, especially beyond Q2 2025 levels.
    • Further disinflationary progress enabling more aggressive monetary policy easing globally.
    • A resolution or significant easing of current commodity "Supply Stress" leading to lower input costs.
    • Continued, robust expansionary liquidity from ON RRP unwinding providing systemic market support.

STRATEGIC BIAS (6–24 months)

  • What type of environment this regime is evolving into:
    • The environment is evolving towards sustained late-cycle moderation with persistent disinflationary forces, potentially accompanied by regional growth divergences.
    • Structural "Supply Stress" in commodities is likely to remain a feature, influencing pricing dynamics and trade flows.
    • Monetary policy settings are likely to remain accommodative or move towards further easing, contingent on the disinflationary path.
  • What exposures become strategically advantaged:
    • Real Assets and Commodities, as structural supply constraints continue to support their value proposition and act as an inflation hedge.
    • Select Public Equities, especially those with strong secular growth drivers that can capitalize on lower real yields and ample liquidity, assuming the moderation does not transition into a deep recession.
    • Targeted Emerging Market segments, if a persistent weakening trend in the US Dollar alleviates external pressures and supports local growth.
  • What becomes gradually more fragile:
    • Highly speculative and high-beta growth equity segments, if global growth moderation deepens into a contraction or if real yields experience a renewed upward trajectory.
    • Traditional long-duration assets (outside the portfolio's current minimal exposure) if disinflation proves transient or commodity-driven inflation re-emerges forcefully.
    • Emerging Market exposures with high external financing needs, should global financial conditions tighten unexpectedly due to a stronger USD.

KEY RISKS & FAILURE MODES

  • Regime-break risks:
    • Global growth deterioration accelerating significantly beyond moderation into a contractionary phase, invalidating the current late-cycle soft-landing narrative.
    • A persistent and broad-based re-emergence of global risk-off sentiment across asset classes, impacting market confidence and capital flows.
    • Re-acceleration of inflation, particularly from intensifying commodity supply stress, compelling central banks to reverse policy easing expectations and adopt a tightening stance.
  • Liquidity reversal scenarios:
    • An abrupt cessation or reversal in the unwinding of the US ON RRP facility, for instance, due to increased counterparty usage or a shift in policy.
    • A faster-than-anticipated pace of central bank balance sheet contraction, leading to a significant and sustained drain of systemic liquidity.
  • FX or credit shocks:
    • A sharp and sustained re-acceleration of the US Dollar, tightening global financial conditions and exerting significant pressure on non-USD denominated assets and Emerging Market growth exposures.
    • Broad-based widening of credit spreads, particularly in high-yield segments, signaling an increase in default risk or tightening funding conditions within the financial system.

OPPORTUNITY THEMES

  • Risk-seeking themes:
    • Technology & Innovation: Targeting segments poised to benefit from lower real yields and sustained liquidity expansion.
    • Select Emerging Market Growth: Identifying opportunities in regions where a persistent USD weakening trend could alleviate external pressures and support domestic demand.
    • Resource Equities: Emphasizing exposure to strategic metals and energy transition themes that benefit from structural demand and supply constraints.
  • Defensive hedges:
    • Precious Metals: Maintaining existing allocations as a hedge against monetary debasement and as a tail-risk ballast during periods of heightened uncertainty.
    • Cash & Short-Term Instruments: Leveraging the elevated cash position for optionality and as a buffer against market volatility without increasing traditional bond exposure.
  • Counter-cyclical opportunities:
    • Strategic Deployment of Cash: Utilizing the elevated cash position to opportunistically invest in high-quality growth assets during market dislocations driven by temporary growth deceleration or sentiment-driven drawdowns.
    • Consideration of Defensive Equities: Exploring selective exposure to under-represented defensive equity sectors during phases of more pronounced global growth moderation to reduce overall portfolio beta without exiting equity markets entirely.

MONITORING TRIGGERS

  • Regime change thresholds:
    • US Labour Market: A sustained and accelerating increase in the unemployment rate, or a definitive shift to "HOT" signals within underemployment indicators.
    • Global Growth: Persistent stagnation in US industrial production, consistent negative readings in global leading indicators, or a clear and sustained decline in the copper residual.
    • Inflation Trajectory: A marked re-acceleration of core PCE inflation, accompanied by a significant shift in market-implied inflation expectations towards the upside.
  • Leading indicators:
    • Credit Spreads: Monitoring the direction and momentum of credit spreads, particularly in high-yield markets, for early signs of systemic stress.
    • Liquidity Dynamics: Tracking the pace of the US ON RRP unwind and broader M2 growth for sustained shifts in systemic liquidity.
    • FX Movements: Observing the US Dollar Index relative to its 12-month moving average and the sentiment in speculative FX positioning for reversals.
    • Global Demand: Monitoring global manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Indices (PMIs) and business investment intentions for shifts in economic activity.
  • Signals that matter most for this portfolio:
    • The trajectory and stability of real rates globally.
    • The consistency and durability of the current liquidity expansion.
    • The evolution of the "Supply Stress" theme, particularly in energy and agricultural commodities.
    • Broad shifts in cross-asset risk sentiment and investor positioning.
    • The direction and volatility of the US Dollar against major currencies, especially for its impact on Emerging Market and commodity exposures.
