US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant driver is the Fed Inflation Signal, which remains in a COOL regime, indicating sustained disinflationary pressure and a late-cycle period (1). This signal carries a High Aggregation Weight Hint and High Conviction.
  • This conclusion is shaped by dynamically weighting the Fed Inflation Signal as the primary input. It reflects broad-based weakening in inflation drivers.
  • The Inflation Term-Structure Signal was downweighted due to its Low Aggregation Weight Hint and Low Conviction (2). While structurally neutral, its influence on the immediate regime call is limited.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Fed Inflation Signal's COOL assessment (1) reinforces underlying soft pressure noted in Market-Implied Inflation Expectations, which is STABLE (3).
  • Tensions exist because the strong disinflationary call from the Fed Inflation Signal (1) is not fully mirrored by other key cyclical signals. Wage Growth & Inflation (4), Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) (5), and CPI vs PPI Divergence (6) are all in NEUTRAL regimes.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the NEUTRAL/STABLE signals reflect stabilization following disinflation, rather than re-acceleration. This creates a delicate equilibrium, confirming a fragile balance. The CPI vs PPI divergence also indicates upward momentum from prior cool conditions (6).

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is that disinflationary risks persist, but other inflation metrics stabilize at neutral levels (1)(5).
  • The primary upside risk is a material re-acceleration in wage growth or producer price pass-through, pushing composite inflation signals back towards 'HOT' regimes (4)(6).
  • The primary downside risk is further weakening global demand or commodity prices, intensifying deflationary risks, and moving core inflation to 'COOL' or 'FALLING' regimes (1)(3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained acceleration in core CPI or 10-year breakeven inflation rates, pushing the Fed Inflation Signal composite z-score above -0.5 (1).
  • The PCE Composite z-score consistently moving above 0.75 (Hot) or below -0.75 (Cool) (5).
  • A significant and sustained upward movement in both 5-year and 10-year breakeven inflation rates, shifting the Inflation Term-Structure composite above +0.75 (2).
