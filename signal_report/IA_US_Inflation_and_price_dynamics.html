US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile, characterized by structural turning and transition-biased price paths.

Why This Regime:

  • The aggregate US Inflation Signal has shifted to a transition state (z-score 0.0005), indicating a structural turning point (1).
  • High-weight signals for realized inflation (3) and market expectations (5) remain in neutral or stable regimes.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes the aggregate signal's transition status despite the stability of consumer (3) and producer price pass-through (6).
  • The Wage Growth signal was downweighted due to low aggregation weight and interpretation confidence (2).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Realized inflation levels (3) and pipeline price relationships (6) reinforce a neutral near-term outlook.
  • Tension exists between the weakening aggregate momentum (1) and market-implied term structures nearing reflationary thresholds (4).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the transition state explicitly accounts for indeterminate volatility and regime-flip risk (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Structural drift toward disinflation (COOL) as aggregate momentum weakens.
  • Primary upside risk: Breakout into a "HOT" reflationary regime if market-priced expectations exceed historical norms (4).
  • Primary downside risk: Accelerated cyclical slowdown pushing the inflation composite deep into negative territory (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A PCE composite move exceeding the 0.75 "Hot" threshold (3).
  • Consumer-producer price divergence exceeding 0.35 z-score units, signaling renewed cost-push or demand-pull pressure (6).
