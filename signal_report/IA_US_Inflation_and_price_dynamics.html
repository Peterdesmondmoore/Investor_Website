US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • State the regime clearly using one label.
  • State the Regime Confidence Index.
  • State whether the regime is stable or fragile.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant signals indicate a neutral inflation environment. The high-weighted Wage Growth and Inflation Composite signals a NEUTRAL regime (1).
  • Medium-weighted signals reinforce this view. The Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal (2), Inflation Term-Structure signal (3), and CPI vs PPI Divergence signal (4) all report neutral or stable conditions.
  • The Fed Inflation Signal (5) was downweighted due to its low aggregation weight, low conviction, mixed interpretation confidence, and an internal conflict in its regime classification.
  • Market-implied inflation expectations (6) was also downweighted due to mixed signals and an internal conflict, reflecting disinflationary momentum despite a 'STABLE' regime call.
  • This regime reflects a moderation from prior inflationary pressures, balanced by persistent disinflationary forces.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals generally reinforce a neutral inflation outlook. The Wage Growth and Inflation Composite (1), Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal (2), Inflation Term-Structure signal (3), and CPI vs PPI Divergence signal (4) are consistently neutral or stable.
  • Tensions arise from the Fed Inflation Signal (5), which reports "COOL" but has a z-score near zero, and Market-implied inflation expectations (6), which is 'STABLE' but shows negative underlying momentum.
  • These tensions highlight persistent disinflationary undercurrents within a largely neutral environment, indicating fragility rather than an outright regime shift.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is for inflation to remain in a neutral and balanced state, consistent with current growth rates and market expectations.
  • The primary upside risk is a sustained re-acceleration of wage growth or a material rebound in growth-sensitive components.
  • The primary downside risk involves broader economic weakness or persistent negative momentum, pushing price pressures into deeper disinflation.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained shift of the Wage Growth and Inflation Composite z-score above +0.75 or below -0.75, moving out of its NEUTRAL band (1).
  • A sustained move of the Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal composite z-score above 0.75 (Hot) or below -0.75 (Cool) (2).
  • A sustained drop of the Market-implied inflation expectations composite z-score below -0.5, signaling FALLING expectations (6).
