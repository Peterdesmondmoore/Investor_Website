US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • State the regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Primary drivers are Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) (1) and Inflation Term-Structure (2).
  • These high-weight signals confirm neutral, trend-following inflation paths with high interpretation confidence (1)(2).
  • Market-implied expectations remain stable despite decelerating momentum (3).
  • The US Inflation Signal was downweighted due to mixed interpretation confidence and internal conflict (4).
  • Wage Growth was downweighted because of low aggregation weight and mixed confidence (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Real yields and breakeven rates align within historical neutral bands (2).
  • CPI and PPI data show a stable relationship between producer and consumer costs (6).
  • Tensions exist where tactical composite scores have breached "COOL" thresholds (4).
  • Fragility stems from the disconnect between high-weight structural stability and declining tactical momentum (3)(4).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Disinflationary drift continues while anchoring near recent historical medians.
  • Primary upside risk: A sharp rebound in core services prices or 3-month annualised CPI momentum.
  • Primary downside risk: Deflationary impulse triggered by a sudden contraction in liquidity or USD strength (4).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A PCE composite z-score breach above +0.75 or below -0.75 (1).
  • Wage Pressure z-scores exceeding +1.0 or falling below -1.0 (5).
  • The Inflation Term-Structure composite crossing outside the ±0.75 historical range (2).

Methodology Applied:

  • A COOL regime triggers if the z-score is ≤ -1.0, or ≤ -0.5 when CPI YoY is ≤ 1.5% (1).

Key Dynamics:
