US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The high-weight structural lead signal has newly re-entered a COOL state (1).
  • However, the primary policy-relevant gauge (3) remains in a persistent NEUTRAL zone.
  • The assessment reflects the tension between cooling structural liquidity (1) and sticky realizations.
  • Wage growth was downweighted due to low conviction and mixed interpretation confidence (2).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Market-implied metrics show high alignment in anchoring inflation compensation near long-term medians (4)(5).
  • Cost-push dynamics are neutral, as PPI and CPI trends have converged (6).
  • Tensions exist between persistent PCE normalization (3) and the volatile drop in structural inflationary pressure (1).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime because momentum is not yet persistent across time horizons.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Sticky disinflation where realized prices remain range-bound despite cooling monetary anchors.
  • Primary Upside Risk: A rebound in commodity inputs (PPI) or core service acceleration triggering a transition to HOT.
  • Primary Downside Risk: Escalating demand contraction pushing structural cooling into realized price deflation (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A structural composite shift above +0.25 indicating a return to neutral or warming liquidity (1).
  • Realized Core PCE breaking its 28-month Neutral persistence via a z-score move beyond +/- 0.75 units (3).
  • Market-implied breakevens breaking out of their 120-month historical distribution (5).

Methodology Applied:

  • A robust z-score is derived using a 60-month rolling median and Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) to standardise the gap (1).

  • Conditional Invalidation: A shift to a HOT regime requires $z_t \ge 1.0$ or ($z_t \ge 0.75$ and $CPI \ge 3.0\%$) (1).
