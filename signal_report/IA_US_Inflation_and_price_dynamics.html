US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Defensive / Risk-Negative
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant driver is the US Inflation Signal, a structural indicator (1). It is weighted highly and shows high conviction in a "COOL (disinflation/deflation risk)" regime (1). This structural disinflationary pressure defines the overall regime.
  • Cyclical signals largely indicate neutral or stable inflation conditions, preventing a classification of extreme stress. The Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal (3), Wage Growth & Inflation Signal (2), Inflation Term-Structure Signal (4), and CPI vs PPI Divergence Signal (6) all report "Neutral" conditions. These cyclical signals carry medium aggregation weight, reinforcing a balanced view for the near term.
  • No signals were specifically downweighted based on the mandated rules. All signals had high interpretation confidence and no internal conflict flags.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal (3), Wage Growth & Inflation Signal (2), Inflation Term-Structure Signal (4), and CPI vs PPI Divergence Signal (6) are aligned, all indicating "Neutral" inflation dynamics. This suggests stable, non-extreme cyclical conditions.
  • Tension exists between the structural "COOL" reading from the US Inflation Signal (1) and the largely "Neutral" cyclical signals. The Market Implied Inflation Signal (5), a high-weight cyclical signal, indicates "STABLE" expectations with an underlying upward bias, creating further tension with the structural disinflationary view.
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime call because the structural signal, with its high weight and longer time horizon, remains the primary regime definer (1). The cyclical stability suggests moderation but does not negate the underlying disinflationary trend.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued disinflationary pressure, maintaining a "COOL" structural inflation regime, while cyclical inflation remains largely neutral to stable (1)(3).
  • Primary upside risk: A gradual return to a "NEUTRAL" structural inflation regime, triggered by unexpected strength in economic activity or commodity prices (1). Market-implied inflation expectations could also shift to a "RISING" regime if momentum accelerates (5).
  • Primary downside risk: Deeper disinflation or outright deflation, driven by persistent weakness across demand indicators and sustained decline in cost pressures (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained and broad-based rebound in underlying inflation components (e.g., CPI, breakevens) and/or growth proxies (e.g., Industrial Production, copper) that pushes the US Inflation Signal's z-score above -0.5 would reverse the COOL regime (1).
  • A material re-acceleration in wage growth or significant labor market tightening that pushes the Wage Growth & Inflation composite z-score above +1.0 would invalidate the current neutral interpretation (2).
  • A sustained upward momentum in market-implied inflation expectations, pushing the Market-Implied Inflation composite z-score to 1.0 or higher, could signal a shift towards a Reflationary regime (5).
