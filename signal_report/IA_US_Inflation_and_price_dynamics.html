US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • The regime is fragile due to extreme threshold proximity across core indicators.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by a high-weight cluster of Neutral signals, including Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) (1), Market Implied Inflation (2), and the Inflation Term-Structure (3).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes these signals due to their high interpretation confidence and Medium aggregation weights.
  • The US Inflation Signal (4) was downweighted because of low interpretation confidence and internal conflict between its label and its z-score.
  • Wage Growth and Inflation (5) was downweighted due to low aggregation weight and lagged data inputs.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • High alignment exists between market-based expectations (2) and realized policy-relevant data (1), both confirming range-bound pricing.
  • The CPI vs PPI Divergence signal (6) reinforces stability by showing producer-to-consumer price gaps within historical noise thresholds.
  • Tension exists where the US Inflation Signal (4) flags a "COOL" regime, but its composite z-score of 0.03 lies within the methodology’s defined transition range.
  • Tensions do not overturn the call as Neutral signals exhibit superior stability and higher conviction bands (1)(2).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Range-bound persistence as inflation oscillates slightly above the 2% target with anchored expectations.
  • Primary upside risk: A cost-push shock triggered by PPI acceleration exceeding CPI rates (6).
  • Primary downside risk: A disinflationary collapse in demand indicated by a sharp decline in M2 momentum (7).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A move in the PCE Composite exceeding the 0.75 threshold into a "HOT" regime (1).
  • Three-month annualized CPI momentum rising above the Year-over-Year rate (4).
  • A transition in Market Implied Inflation to a "FALLING" regime, signaled by a z-score below -0.5 (2).
