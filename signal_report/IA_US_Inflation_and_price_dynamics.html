US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by Medium-weight signals in Policy-Relevant PCE (1) and Market Implied Inflation (2), which currently sit in stable/neutral zones despite elevated YoY levels.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes the High Interpretation Confidence of these Cyclical signals (1)(2) over the aggregate US Inflation Signal.
  • The primary US Inflation Signal was downweighted due to "Low Confidence," an "Internal Conflict Flag," and a "Low" aggregation weight hint (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Market-implied expectations (2), term-structure stability (4), and wage-inflation convergence (5) reinforce a neutral, range-bound environment.
  • Tension exists within the aggregate US Inflation Signal, which shows a sharp deceleration toward "Transition" while its underlying data table indicates "Cool" (3).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime because the higher-confidence PCE and Market-Implied signals remain anchored within their respective neutral corridors (1)(2).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistence of range-biased price paths and normal volatility as long as the PCE composite remains neutral.
  • Primary upside risk: A "Reflationary" shift triggered by 5Y inflation breakevens breaching the +0.75 z-score threshold (4).
  • Primary downside risk: Entry into a "Cool" regime if the aggregate US Inflation composite breaches -0.50, signaling demand deceleration (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A move in the PCE_Composite above 0.75 or below -0.75, ending the multi-year neutral persistence (1).
  • A composite Market Implied Inflation z-score reaching ≥ 1.0, indicating a breakout in inflation compensation (2).
  • Sustained movement of the aggregate US Inflation composite outside the -0.25 to +0.25 transition range (3).
