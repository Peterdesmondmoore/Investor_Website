US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by a balance of signals indicating both ongoing disinflationary forces and stable, albeit higher, market expectations. The Market Implied Inflation signal, a structural indicator with a high aggregation weight, points to stable expectations leaning higher (1). This is supported by the Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) signal (2) and the Inflation Term-Structure signal (3), both indicating a Neutral regime with high aggregation weight and confidence.
  • Conversely, the Fed Inflation Signal, also with a high aggregation weight and high confidence, indicates a COOL regime with disinflation/deflation risk (4). This creates a tension that prevents a clear "Expansionary" or "Defensive" classification.
  • Signals like Wage Growth & Inflation (5) and CPI vs PPI Divergence (6) were downweighted to medium aggregation weight. They both indicate a Neutral regime, reinforcing the sense of balance but not overriding the core tension among high-weight signals.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Reinforcing signals for a neutral or stable outlook include the Market Implied Inflation signal (1), Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) (2), Inflation Term-Structure (3), Wage Growth & Inflation (5), and CPI vs PPI Divergence (6).
  • A primary tension exists with the Fed Inflation Signal, which registers a COOL (disinflation/deflation risk) regime (4). This signal's high conviction and confidence highlight persistent disinflationary pressure.
  • The "COOL" signal does not overturn the "Transitional / Fragile Balance" call because multiple high-weight signals, including the structural Market Implied Inflation (1), indicate stability or neutrality. This suggests a current disinflationary phase within a broader context of stable expectations.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario involves inflation remaining in a fragile balance. This is characterized by ongoing moderation in realized prices, counteracted by stable market-implied expectations.
  • Primary upside risk is a re-acceleration of market-implied inflation expectations or a sustained increase in core PCE. This would push high-weight signals into a more reflationary stance.
  • Primary downside risk is further deceleration in headline and core inflation measures, potentially leading to explicit deflationary risk. This could be triggered by a significant economic slowdown.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained rise in the Fed Inflation Signal composite z-score above -0.5, shifting its regime back to NEUTRAL (4).
  • A sustained decline in both 5-year and 10-year breakeven inflation rates, causing the Market Implied Inflation composite z-score to fall below -0.5 (1).
  • A shift in the Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) composite z-score below -0.75, indicating a "Cool" regime (2).
