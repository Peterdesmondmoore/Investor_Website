US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant driver is the Fed Inflation Signal indicating a COOL regime, suggesting disinflation or deflation risk and a strengthening disinflationary trend (1). This comprehensive signal influences macro policy.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes the Fed Inflation Signal due to its explicit "COOL" regime call and emphasis on core inflation and breakeven rates (1). Its comprehensive nature and explicit weighting give it higher qualitative importance.
  • The Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) Signal (2) and Market-Implied Inflation Expectations (MIIE) Signal (3) remain Neutral or Stable, preventing a more definitive disinflationary regime call. These signals are considered important due to their policy relevance and market-based insights.
  • Signals remaining Neutral or Stable, such as the CPI vs PPI Divergence (4), were interpreted as indicating balance rather than a strong counter-trend.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Fed Inflation Signal's "COOL" (1) is reinforced by the Wage Growth and Inflation Signal showing moderate disinflationary pressure (5). The Inflation Term-Structure Signal (6) and MIIE Signal (3) also show negative momentum or a downward drift, aligning with easing inflation.
  • Tensions exist with the Policy-Relevant PCE Signal (2) and CPI vs PPI Divergence Signal (4), which remain in a Neutral or Stable regime. These reflect current stability at moderate levels.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the consistent downward momentum across several signals, coupled with the comprehensive "COOL" call, indicates a dominant disinflationary trend. The "Neutral" signals primarily reflect stability, not a re-acceleration.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is a continued disinflationary trend, bringing inflation closer to target without significant economic stress (1)(5).
  • The primary upside risk is inflation re-acceleration. This would be triggered by a shift in policy-relevant or market-implied inflation signals from Neutral/Stable to HOT (2)(4).
  • The primary downside risk is a deeper deflationary spiral or significant economic slowdown. This is implied by the disinflationary risk from the Fed Inflation Signal (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Fed Inflation Signal (1) moving back to a NEUTRAL or HOT regime.
  • The Policy-Relevant PCE Signal (2) shifting to HOT, signaling renewed inflationary pressures.
  • A significant re-acceleration in wage pressure, as indicated by the Wage Growth and Inflation Signal (5).
