US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • The regime is stable but displays a mild disinflationary drift.

Why This Regime:

  • Primary weight was assigned to Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) due to its high aggregation weight and high confidence (1).
  • Neutrality is confirmed by aligned high-confidence signals across market expectations (2) and term structures (3).
  • The US Inflation Signal was downweighted due to internal conflict between its "COOL" label and neutral z-score (4).
  • Wage signals were downweighted due to low conviction and mixed interpretation confidence (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Strong alignment exists between core PCE (-0.45 z-score) and CPI/PPI divergence (-0.048 z-score) (1)(6).
  • Expectations remain anchored, with 5-year and 10-year breakevens clustered near historical medians (2)(3).
  • Tensions in the broad inflation composite (4) do not overturn the call. The high-weight PCE signal firmly anchors the regime in the neutral zone.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Range-biased pricing. Inflation tracks near trend levels, supporting a data-dependent policy stance (1).
  • Primary upside risk: Liquidity-driven spike. Triggered by rapid M2 expansion or a surge in Fed assets (4).
  • Primary downside risk: Deepening disinflation. Triggered by a significant contraction in producer prices relative to consumer costs (6).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • PCE composite z-score breaching +0.75 would signal a transition to a "Hot" regime (1).
  • Market-implied inflation z-score moving below -0.50 would signal a shift to a "Falling" expectations regime (2).
  • CPI/PPI divergence z-score exceeding ±1.0 would force a regime exit (6).
