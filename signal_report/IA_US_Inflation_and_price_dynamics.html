US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • State: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary anchor is Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE), which remains in a persistent Neutral state (1).
  • Market-based expectations and terminal structure signals align with this range-bound environment (2)(3).
  • The US Inflation Signal was downweighted due to internal conflict between its z-score and regime label (4).
  • The Wage Growth signal was downweighted due to low weight and unknown data freshness (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • PCE and Market-Implied Inflation reinforce a stable, non-accelerating pricing environment (1)(3).
  • A tension exists where the US Inflation Signal classifies the current state as COOL, indicating disinflationary impulses (4).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime call as most medium-to-high weight signals are range-bound (6).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued Neutral regime where inflation remains sticky but non-accelerating (1).
  • Primary upside risk: Energy shocks or core services re-acceleration trigger a shift to a HOT regime (6).
  • Primary downside risk: A sharp contraction in consumer demand drives a shift to a COOL regime (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • Policy-Relevant PCE composite score moving above +0.75 or below -0.75 z-score units (1).
  • Market-based inflation z-scores crossing above 1.0 or below -0.5 (3).
