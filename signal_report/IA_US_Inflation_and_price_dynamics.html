US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile due to deteriorating stability in aggregate indicators.

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight signals for PCE and market-implied inflation show range-bound, neutral behavior (1)(2).
  • The broad US Inflation Signal moved into a transition state with a z-score of 0.003 (3).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritised stable PCE data over conflicting tactical indicators.
  • Wage Growth and CPI/PPI signals were downweighted due to low confidence and missing producer-level data (4)(5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Breakevens and PCE levels align on anchored expectations above the 2% policy target (1)(2).
  • Tensions exist within the US Inflation Signal between its "COOL" label and quantitative transition state (3).
  • Internal conflict in CPI/PPI data stems from missing PPI components (5).
  • These tensions signal regime-flip risk rather than a confirmed trend continuation.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Neutral persistence with inflation oscillating slightly above central bank targets.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Reflationary breakout triggered by 5-year breakevens exceeding 2.60 (6).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Structural disinflation confirmed by a composite decline below -0.50 (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A PCE_Composite move exceeding +0.75 would confirm a shift to a Hot regime (1).
  • Market-implied inflation z-scores rising above 1.0 would signal a RISING expectations regime (2).

  • Falling z-scores imply a disinflationary impulse or demand deceleration, suggesting potential for monetary easing (1).

  • Internal Conflict: The provided data labels 0.003 as "COOL," while the methodology defines values between -0.25 and +0.25 as "TRANSITION" (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A reversal in z-score momentum exceeding +0.25 would negate the current disinflationary transition.
  • The signal demonstrates low stability, having shifted regime labels twice in the last four months.

  • Base case dominant; risks balanced due to the directionless nature of the transition zone.
  • Upside Risk: A re-acceleration of 3-month annualized CPI above the YoY rate, triggering a regime flip toward "HOT."
  • Downside Risk: A continued decline below -0.50, confirming a structural disinflationary trend.

  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Low, justified by the high regime-flip risk and internal classification conflicts.

  • Economic mechanism: The signal implies a disinflationary impulse, which typically serves as a tailwind for fixed income and a warning of cyclical demand slowdown (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • This signal should be cross-referenced with M2 liquidity and Copper demand to distinguish between cost-push and demand-pull dynamics.

Regime Context:

  • Direction of change: Weakening towards the disinflationary boundary.

Model Limitations:
