US Inflation and Price Dynamics
Systematically synthesises realised inflation, cost pressures, and market expectations to assess the prevailing US inflation regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile, characterized by neutral levels with significant upward momentum.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by high-confidence neutral readings in Policy-Relevant PCE (3) and Market-Implied Inflation (5).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized these signals due to High Interpretation Confidence and Medium Aggregation Weight Hints.
  • The US Inflation Signal was downweighted due to Low Interpretation Confidence and an internal label conflict (1).
  • Wage Growth and Inflation was downweighted due to a Low Aggregation Weight Hint and Low Interpretation Confidence (2).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Inflation Term-Structure (4) and Market-Implied signals (5) align, showing rising breakeven momentum.
  • A tension exists between stable realized PCE (3) and accelerating upstream PPI costs (6).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime call as most metrics remain within historical neutral bands.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Range-biased price paths as expectations remain anchored within historical norms (5).
  • Primary upside risk: A "regime-flip" to Reflation triggered by further breakeven expansion (4).
  • Primary downside risk: A shift to "Cool" disinflation if demand decelerates and breaches the -0.50 threshold (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A PCE Composite breach of the ±0.75 z-score threshold (3).
  • The CPI vs PPI Divergence z-score reaching 1.0 or 0.75 with persistent high CPI (6).

Inflation Term-Structure Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given 5-Year Breakeven Inflation (0.77 z-score) and 10-Year Breakeven Inflation (0.54 z-score), this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Regime shift, not Mean reversion.
  • The signal remains in a persistent Neutral regime as the composite score is below the 0.75 reflation threshold (1).
  • Conviction Band: Medium, Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence, Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Improving, Threshold Proximity: Moderate, Revision Sensitivity: Moderate.

Methodology Applied:

  • A Composite score ≥ 0.75 defines a Reflation regime, favoring pro-cyclical assets and commodities (1).
  • A Composite score ≤ -0.75 defines a Disinflation regime, favoring defensive assets and growth equities (1).
  • Values between these thresholds indicate a Neutral regime, serving as a weak macro conditioning variable (1).
  • Inflation Term-Structure Signal dataset, latest observation date: 2026-04-30 (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is rising inflation compensation, with T5YIE and T10YIE reaching multi-month z-score highs (1).
  • Upward momentum in breakevens is partially offset by a relatively stable 10-year real yield (Z_mReal: -0.10).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A Composite score reversal below -0.75 would trigger a Disinflation regime shift (1).
  • The signal shows directional consistency, improving toward a Reflation transition over the last three months (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Continued Neutral regime as breakevens stabilize within current historical bands.
  • Upside risk: Transition to Reflation if breakevens rise further, signaling easier financial conditions (1).
  • Downside risk: Return to Disinflation if real yields spike significantly, indicating tighter real conditions (1).

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months). Market-implied inflation expectations typically lead realized macro shifts over monthly horizons.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium. This provides essential conditioning for macro factor tilts in balanced portfolios (1).

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs pricing and liquidity by measuring the degree of market-implied inflation compensation (1).
  • Implied economic mechanism: Rising values suggest easing financial conditions and potential risk-on sentiment (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Interacts with commodity price trends and survey-based inflation expectations to confirm regime shifts (1).

Regime Context:

  • The Neutral regime has been persistent since mid-2023 without breaching either directional threshold (1).
  • Direction of change: Strengthening toward Reflation as the Composite rises from negative territory toward +0.75 (1).

Model Limitations:

  • TIPS liquidity premia can distort breakeven rates during periods of extreme market stress (1).
  • The rolling window logic makes this a relative measure rather than an absolute one (1).

Data & References:

CPI vs PPI Divergence Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a rising divergence z-score of 0.64 and CPI YoY at 3.29%, this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Regime shift, not Mean reversion. (1)
  • The signal currently occupies a NEUTRAL regime, though it is rapidly approaching the HOT threshold due to accelerating producer prices. (1)
  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Volatile; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: Unknown.

Methodology Applied:

  • A HOT regime is triggered if the divergence z-score (z) is ≥ 1.0, or if z ≥ 0.75 while CPI YoY remains ≥ 3.0%. (1)
  • The NEUTRAL regime, defined by an absence of extreme divergence, implies stable price relationships and no strong directional bias. (1)
  • Rising values in the "PPI minus CPI" spread imply increasing inflationary pressure and potential late-cycle expansion. (1)
  • CPI vs PPI Divergence Signal (latest observation: 2026-03-31). (1)

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the sharp acceleration in PPI YoY (6.03%) relative to CPI YoY (3.29%), lifting the z-score from 0.09 to 0.64 in two months. (1)
  • This represents a significant inflection toward upstream cost-push pressure, although it remains within the methodology's neutral band. (1)
  • Conditional Invalidation: The signal transitions to HOT if the z-score reaches 0.75 while CPI remains above 3.0%. (1)
  • The signal is currently volatile, ending a period of stability that persisted since late 2023. (1)

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Continued NEUTRAL regime as the divergence has not yet breached the 0.75/1.0 thresholds.
