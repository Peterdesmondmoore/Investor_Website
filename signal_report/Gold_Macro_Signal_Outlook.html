Gold Macro Outlook

Regime Assessment

Current conditions align with the Liquidity Expansion regime. Dominant drivers include Liquidity Expansion and Elevated Stress.

1-Month Outlook

  • Directional Bias: Up
  • Probability: 65%
  • Volatility Profile: Medium

3-Month Outlook

  • Directional Bias: Up
  • Probability: 75%
  • Structural Drivers: Expanding liquidity conditions with expectations for continued monetary easing (2), coupled with persistent non-economic central bank demand and robust investment flows into gold ETFs (72)(71).

Conflicts & Watchpoints

  • The Real Interest Rate Trend presents a key conflict, as falling real rates typically underpin a Liquidity Expansion regime. Inconsistencies here could invalidate a core macro tailwind (1).
  • Despite "Elevated Stress" being a dominant force, the reliability of this driver is tempered by conflicts within the Financial Stress Composite Signal and the Market Volatility VIX Signal (14)(16).

Self-Healing

  • RSS Documents Needed:
    • Crude Oil CoT Signals — Source: Provides specific commodity insights to further dissect broad market divergence trends (22).
  • Weaknesses in Provided Inputs:
    • The `USD_Signal` column in `USD_Index_Signal` indicates "Bullish" but `Value_Monthly` is below `USD_12Month_MA`, which per methodology implies "Weakening" (4). This internal inconsistency affects USD regime interpretation.
    • M2 Money Stock data is missing for 2025-11-30 and 2025-12-31 in the `Federal_Reserve_Liquidity_Composite_Signal` (2). This prevents a complete assessment of the M2 component.
    • The `Gold_Individual_Market_Analysis` dataframe only contains 15 rows of data, insufficient for the 52-week lookback required by its methodology for robust z-score calculation and regime identification (9).
    • The `Price` column in various CoT dataframes (`USD_CoT_Signals`, `AUD_CoT_Signals`, `EURO_CoT_Signals`) is either static or incomplete, hindering comprehensive time-series analysis required by methodology (5)(17)(18).
  • Data Behaviour Anomalies:
    • Future-dated `Report_Date` values are present in some CoT dataframes (e.g., AUD, EURO, Squeeze & Exhaustion Risk) and potentially for the `Cross_Sector_Positioning` signal, which impacts data currency assumptions (13)(10).
