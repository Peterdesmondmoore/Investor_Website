US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • The regime is stable.

Why This Regime:

  • Strong momentum in high-weight cyclical leads drives the assessment (1)(2).
  • Housing starts and capital expenditure intent show significant historical strength (1)(2).
  • Industrial Production was downweighted due to internal conflicts and low interpretation confidence (3).
  • Durable Goods ex-Transportation was downweighted because of data uncertainty and low conviction (4).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Housing and Capex signals reinforce a robust private-sector demand impulse (1)(2).
  • Financial conditions remain supportive, with the policy rate below the neutral proxy (5).
  • Tension exists in the Treasury curve, which indicates a bearish steepening transition (6).
  • Steepening reflects normalization and does not currently overturn strong real-sector growth leads (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued cyclical expansion as business investment and construction pipelines remain elevated.
  • Primary upside risk: Acceleration in core orders outstripping the long-term trend (2).
  • Primary downside risk: Mortgage rates exceeding 7%, triggering a rapid deterioration in affordability (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Capex composite Z-score falling below 0.75, indicating a shift to neutral intent (2).
  • A negative 3-month change in the housing composite exceeding -0.60 (1).
  • Industrial production year-over-year growth contracting below 0.0% (3).
