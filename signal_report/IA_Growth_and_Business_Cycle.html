US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The regime reflects conflicting growth signals and a shifting monetary policy backdrop. The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal points to a Bullish expansion in core manufacturing demand, indicating underlying strength (1). This signal carries a high aggregation weight due to its high conviction and confidence.
  • Conversely, the Industrial Production signal is Bearish, showing contraction in overall industrial output (2). This signal also holds a high aggregation weight, creating a direct tension in the growth outlook.
  • Yield curve signals, both the Slope and 10Y-FedFunds versions, are high-weight cyclical indicators. They indicate a move away from inverted states towards a Flat/Neutral or Bearish (steepening) posture (3)(4). This shift suggests a transition from late-cycle concerns towards a more balanced or early-to-mid-cycle environment.
  • Monetary policy, as indicated by the Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy signal, is Near neutral with a rising underlying neutral rate (5). This signal had a medium aggregation weight, indicating a balanced policy stance.
  • The Housing Lead signal, which shows a Neutral regime, was downweighted due to its low conviction, mixed signals among its components, and an internal conflict flag (6).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Reinforcing signals include the consistent steepening trend in both US Treasury yield curve indicators, suggesting an easing of prior recessionary pressures (3)(4). The neutral monetary policy stance also aligns with a period of rebalancing (5).
  • The primary tension lies between the Bullish demand for durable goods and the Bearish contraction in industrial production (1)(2). This indicates a disjointed growth picture.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call, as the yield curve's move away from inversion, a historically reliable indicator, points to a transition from a risk-negative phase. However, the conflicting growth signals prevent a confident shift to a fully Expansionary regime.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A continued fragile balance, with offsetting forces in manufacturing demand and industrial output, while monetary policy remains near neutral.
  • Primary upside risk: A broad-based re-acceleration in economic activity, with industrial production rebounding to align with strong durable goods orders. This would be supported by sustained yield curve steepening.
  • Primary downside risk: A deeper contraction in industrial production, alongside a significant deceleration in durable goods orders, signaling a broader economic slowdown.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained decline in Durable Goods ex-Transportation year-over-year growth below -3%, signaling demand contraction (1).
  • Industrial production year-over-year growth rising above +3%, signaling a clear expansion in output (2).
  • A renewed inversion of the US Treasury yield curve with composite z-scores falling below -0.75, signaling heightened recession fears (3).
