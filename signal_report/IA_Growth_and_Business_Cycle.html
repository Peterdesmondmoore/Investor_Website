US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Regime Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The regime is driven by strong positive momentum in core manufacturing demand and a normalizing yield curve.
  • The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal is Bullish with high conviction and confidence (1), indicating expanding underlying demand. This high-weight cyclical signal strongly supports an expansionary view.
  • Both yield curve signals, also high-weight cyclical indicators, show a clear transition away from restrictive conditions. The Yield Curve Slope is Flat/Neutral but moving from inversion (5), while the US Treasury Yield Curve is in a "Bearish" (steepening) regime, suggesting anticipation of policy easing or improving growth (8).
  • Signals for Industrial Production (3), Capex Intent (2), Housing Lead (7), and Explicit Neutral Rate (12) were weighted as Medium or Low. These indicators currently present a Neutral or mixed picture, suggesting the expansion is not yet broad-based or deeply entrenched.
  • The Housing Lead signal (7) was significantly downweighted due to its Low Conviction, Mixed Signals confidence, and flagged Internal Conflict, reducing its influence on the overall regime call.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Key signals reinforce each other by indicating a positive shift in economic momentum. Durable Goods ex-Transportation shows robust demand (1). The yield curve signals collectively point to an end of restrictive monetary conditions and market anticipation of growth or easing (5)(8).
  • Tensions exist with the Industrial Production (3) and Capex Intent (2) signals, which remain Neutral, and the Housing Lead signal (7) which is neutral with mixed dynamics.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the positive shifts from high-weight signals indicate a clear directional trend, while the neutral signals represent stability rather than contraction. They suggest the expansion is still maturing.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued moderate expansion, supported by improving manufacturing demand and a normalizing yield curve, with policy remaining near neutral (12).
  • Primary upside risk: Acceleration in industrial production and capital expenditure, pushing these signals into expansionary territory.
  • Primary downside risk: A renewed deceleration in durable goods orders (below -1% for 3M growth) (1) or a re-inversion of the yield curve (5), signaling heightening recession risks.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in Durable Goods ex-Transportation year-over-year growth below -3% (1).
  • A significant decline in yield spreads, leading to a deep re-inversion of the yield curve (composite below -0.75) (5).
  • A sustained decline in Capex Intent below -0.75, indicating a contraction in business investment (2).
