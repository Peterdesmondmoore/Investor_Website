US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the high-conviction Expansion in business investment (1), coupled with a cyclical transition in the yield curve (2).
  • Dynamic weighting emphasizes high-confidence cyclical steepening (3) as the economy exits deep inversion.
  • Core activity signals for manufacturing (4) and policy neutrality (5) were downweighted due to significant data gaps and low interpretation confidence.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Reinforcement exists between capital expenditure growth (1) and the steepening of the Treasury curve (2).
  • A primary tension exists in Industrial Production (6) and Housing (7), where activity has reached expansionary thresholds but lacks momentum confirmation.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as the leading investment signals remain firmly expansionary while activity signals are merely lagging, not contracting.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A fragile shift toward broad expansion as yield curves exit inversion and investment commitments hold.
  • Primary upside risk: Confirmation of a "Bullish" industrial regime triggered by positive forward-looking orders (6).
  • Primary downside risk: A reversal in the Housing Lead composite $(\Delta_3 C_t > 0.60)$, signaling a failure in cyclical recovery (7).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in the yield spread to 0% or inversion would signal a return to defensive conditions (2).
  • The Capex_Intent composite falling below 0.75 would invalidate the current growth driver (1).
  • Two consecutive month-end readings of the Yield Curve Slope below -0.50 would re-establish an "Inverted" regime (3).
