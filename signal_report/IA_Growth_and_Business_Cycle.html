US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Status: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • Primary drivers show robust cyclical expansion in core real-sector investment and residential construction (5)(6).
  • High aggregation weights for Capex Intent and Housing Lead anchor the assessment in real-economy momentum (6)(5).
  • Policy remains materially accommodative, with the real policy rate significantly below market-implied neutral levels (7).
  • Industrial Production (4) and Durable Goods (1) were downweighted due to low interpretation confidence, internal conflicts, and data gaps.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Business investment and housing activity are strongly aligned, both showing high-conviction expansionary trends (6)(5).
  • Treasury signals reinforce this shift, with the yield curve transitioning toward a steepening profile (2)(3).
  • Tension exists in industrial output, where broad growth lags behind specific mining sector strength (4).
  • This tension does not overturn the regime call as real-sector momentum is led by forward-looking orders and permits (6)(5).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Trend continuation of pro-cyclical growth supported by accommodative financial conditions (7).
  • Primary Upside Risk: A sharp decline in mortgage rates accelerating already strong housing groundbreakings (5).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Policy restrictiveness rising if the market-implied neutral rate falls toward the policy rate (7).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A 3-month decline in the housing composite score exceeding 0.60, indicating a collapse in the construction pipeline (5).
  • The Capex Intent composite Z-score falling below 0.75, signalling a cessation of business investment expansion (6).
  • Yield curve re-inversion characterized by a composite score dropping below -0.75 (2).
