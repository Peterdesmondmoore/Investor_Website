US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by conflicting signals from key cyclical indicators. Core manufacturing demand, as indicated by the Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal, is bullish and expanding (1). This is a primary driver with high aggregation weight and high confidence.
  • However, the US Treasury Yield Curve signal, also a high aggregation weight indicator, points to a flat or inverted curve. This suggests a potentially restrictive or recessionary environment (2). This signal's macro implication leads to a more conservative regime call despite its internal "Bullish" label.
  • Other mid-weight signals, including Industrial Production (3), Housing Lead (4), and Capex Intent (5), are predominantly Neutral. These signals indicate stable but not accelerating activity.
  • The Housing Lead signal was downweighted due to its 'Yes' Internal Conflict Flag, as strong housing starts are balanced by neutral building permits and lingering mortgage rate headwinds (4).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (1), Industrial Production (3), and Capex Intent (5) signals show some alignment. They suggest a stable to moderately expanding mid-cycle environment for industrial activity and business investment.
  • Significant tension exists between this pro-growth industrial momentum and the warning from the US Treasury Yield Curve signal (2). The yield curve's flat/inverted state implies a restrictive monetary environment and potential future slowdown.
  • The Explicit Neutral Rate Proxy signal further reinforces this tension. Its "Near neutral" policy stance combined with a "Rising" neutral rate drift implies an implicitly tightening monetary policy (6). These monetary signals temper the positive growth indicators.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is a fragile equilibrium. Underlying industrial demand remains solid, but monetary conditions present headwinds.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained re-steepening of the yield curve to a neutral or bullish regime, combined with accelerating industrial momentum.
  • Primary downside risk: A deepening of the yield curve inversion or a further rise in the neutral rate. This would signal increasing monetary restrictiveness and heightened slowdown risk.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A reversal in Durable Goods ex-Transportation momentum, with its 3-month percentage change falling below +1%, would signal a loss of core industrial expansion (1).
  • A sustained move of the US Treasury Yield Curve spread above 0%, especially if accompanied by a steepening trend, would alleviate recessionary warnings (2).
  • A shift in the Explicit Neutral Rate Proxy's drift from "Rising" to "Falling" or "Stable" would indicate easing implicit monetary tightening (6).
