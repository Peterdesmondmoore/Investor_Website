US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • The regime is stable.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is primarily driven by high-weight cyclical indicators showing robust expansion. The Housing Lead Signal (1) provides the strongest evidence with high conviction, supported by the Capex Intent Signal (2) which indicates accelerating business investment.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes these lead indicators over neutral lagging data. The Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy Signal (3) confirms that the current policy stance is accommodative, further justifying an expansionary posture.
  • The Industrial Production Signal (4) was downweighted due to an Internal Conflict Flag and mixed signals. The Durable Goods ex-Transportation Signal (5) was downweighted due to low interpretation confidence resulting from a data gap.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Strong alignment exists between leading cyclical indicators (Housing and Capex Intent), which both reflect high-conviction growth momentum (1)(2).
  • Tensions exist within the Industrial Production Signal (4), where headline growth has reached expansionary levels but lacks broader sector confirmation.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call because the Yield Curve Slope Signal (6) and the US Treasury Yield Curve Signal (7) have exited inversion and are currently stabilising near expansionary thresholds.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued cyclical expansion driven by residential investment and core business capital expenditure.
  • Primary upside risk: A bull-steepening of the yield curve if the spread exceeds the 3-month trend and 1.5% threshold (7).
  • Primary downside risk: Material downward revisions to recent housing starts or a sudden spike in financing costs (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A 3-month change in the Housing Lead composite of less than -0.60, signalling rapid cyclical deterioration (1).
  • A reversal in the Yield_Spread to below 0%, indicating renewed curve inversion and restrictive monetary conditions (7).
