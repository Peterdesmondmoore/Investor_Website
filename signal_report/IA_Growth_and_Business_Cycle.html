US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Regime Stability: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is primarily driven by improving growth signals and normalizing monetary conditions. The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal is Bullish, indicating sustained expansion in core manufacturing orders (1). This signal carries high interpretation confidence and a medium aggregation weight, making it a key driver.
  • Crucially, both the Yield Curve Slope (2) and U.S. Treasury Yield Curve Signal (3) indicate a steepening trend from prior inversion or a flat state. The 'Bearish' classification for the U.S. Treasury Yield Curve Signal (3) in this context signifies a shift towards rising growth expectations, moving away from a restrictive stance. These yield curve signals are implicitly high weight due to their structural macro relevance.
  • The Industrial Production Signal is Neutral, reflecting stabilization after a period of contraction (4). This signal also has high confidence and a medium aggregation weight, supporting a stable, non-contracting trend. The Housing Lead Signal is likewise Neutral, contributing to overall stability (5).
  • The Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy Signal indicates a "Near neutral" policy stance (6). This suggests real policy rates are not overly restrictive, further supporting a risk-positive environment.
  • Signals like the Housing Lead (5) were assumed to have medium aggregation weight and confidence, providing a balanced view but not overriding the stronger growth and yield curve indications.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Signals reinforce each other through consistent indications of improving growth and normalizing financial conditions. The Bullish Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (1) aligns with the positive interpretation of the steepening yield curves (2)(3), which signal rising growth expectations.
  • The Industrial Production (4) and Housing Lead (5) signals are Neutral, representing stable conditions rather than explicit expansion. These prevent an "Extreme Expansion" classification but do not contradict the "Expansionary" regime, instead indicating a stable phase within it.
  • Tensions are minor. The Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy Signal (6) has "Medium" interpretation confidence. However, its "Near neutral" regime still supports the overall theme of non-restrictive monetary conditions. This moderate confidence does not overturn the regime call, given the alignment of other high-conviction, high-weight growth indicators.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Moderate economic expansion continues, driven by firming core manufacturing orders and supported by normalizing financial conditions.
  • Primary upside risk: Accelerated economic growth, potentially triggered by a stronger rebound in industrial activity or a sustained, significant steepening of the yield curve.
  • Primary downside risk: A re-inversion of the U.S. Treasury Yield Curve or a notable contraction in core manufacturing orders, signaling renewed economic stress.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (1) shifting to Bearish, with both year-over-year and 3-month momentum falling below their respective negative thresholds.
  • The U.S. Treasury Yield Curve Signal (3) returning to an inverted state, with the 10-year minus Fed Funds yield spread turning negative.
  • The Industrial Production Signal (4) shifting to Bearish, where industrial production growth falls below 0% while the mining share of GDP growth remains positive.
