Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Status: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the Capex Intent Signal (1). It shows a robust expansion with a composite Z-score of 1.185.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes this signal due to its High Aggregation Weight and High Interpretation Confidence (1).
  • Monetary conditions reinforce this via an accommodative Explicit Neutral Rate Proxy (2).
  • The Durable Goods (3) and Housing Lead (4) signals were downweighted. Data gaps resulted in Low Confidence and Internal Conflict flags.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Capex expansion (1) and accommodative policy (2) reinforce the risk-positive regime.
  • Industrial Production (5) and Yield Curve Slope (6) remain in Neutral territory.
  • The U.S. Treasury Yield Curve (7) flags a Bearish (steepening) state.
  • Tensions do not overturn the call because Neutral signals show improving momentum toward expansion thresholds (6).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistent expansion supported by accelerating core business fixed investment.
  • Primary upside risk: Manufacturing re-acceleration pushing industrial output growth above 2.0% (5).
  • Primary downside risk: Policy normalization causing the stance gap to close, reducing liquidity support (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A Capex Composite Z-score falling below the 0.75 expansion threshold (1).
  • The policy stance gap rising above -0.50, indicating a shift toward restrictive territory (2).
  • A contraction in real-sector industrial production volumes below 0% YoY (5).
