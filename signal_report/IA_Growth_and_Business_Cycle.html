US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight forward-looking indicators have shifted into expansionary territory.
  • The Housing Lead Signal (1) and Capex Intent Signal (2) both reflect strengthening cyclical momentum.
  • Monetary conditions are supportive, with the neutral rate proxy showing deepening policy accommodation (3).
  • The Durable Goods ex-Transportation Signal (4) was downweighted due to low interpretation confidence and missing core data.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Forward investment intent aligns with high-conviction expansion in housing starts and permits (1)(2).
  • Tensions exist as real-sector output remains modest. Industrial Production (5) is currently failing to breach expansion thresholds.
  • Treasury term structures are characterized as Flat or Neutral (6)(7), indicating the broader economy has not yet converged with the lead signals.
  • The regime remains transitional because these lagging and concurrent signals haven't confirmed the expansion.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Early-cycle expansion supported by rising capital expenditure and housing demand.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Industrial Production growth exceeding 2% YoY, confirming a broad-based recovery (5).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Resurgent mortgage rates triggering demand destruction in the housing sector (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Housing Lead composite falling below the 0.75 expansion threshold (1).
  • A move in the monetary stance gap above -0.50, signaling a shift toward restrictive policy (3).
  • Yield curve re-inversion signaled by a spread falling below 0% (7).
