US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The regime reflects a balance of mixed cyclical signals. Durable Goods ex-Transportation is Bullish, indicating expansion in core manufacturing orders (1). However, the US Treasury Yield Curve Signal is Bullish due to an inverted 10Y-FFR spread, pointing to a restrictive or recessionary environment (8). Industrial Production is Neutral, indicating stable but not strong growth (7).
  • High aggregation weight signals with high confidence, such as Durable Goods ex-Transportation (1), Industrial Production (7), and the two yield curve signals (3)(8), were primary drivers. The most conservative label, Transitional / Fragile Balance, was selected due to significant underlying tensions.
  • The Housing Lead Signal (16) and Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy Signal (11) were downweighted due to lower conviction or mixed interpretation confidence, respectively.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Bullish Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (1) reinforces a view of underlying demand strength in core manufacturing.
  • Significant tension exists between the positive manufacturing momentum and the US Treasury Yield Curve Signal's (8) indication of a restrictive or recessionary environment. Further tension arises from the Yield Curve Slope Signal (3) showing a Flat/Neutral state, implying stabilization, contrasting with the inversion cited by the US Treasury Yield Curve Signal (8).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime call. The "Transitional / Fragile Balance" classification acknowledges these conflicts without committing to a full risk-on or risk-off stance, given the mixed evidence from high-weight signals.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A fragile equilibrium persists, characterized by moderate industrial growth and manufacturing expansion alongside persistent monetary policy restrictiveness.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained re-steepening of the yield curve, coupled with accelerated industrial production, could signal a shift towards an Expansionary regime.
  • Primary downside risk: A deeper yield curve inversion and a contraction in core manufacturing or industrial production would suggest a Defensive / Risk-Negative shift.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A persistent decline in the 3-month percentage change of Durable Goods ex-Transportation orders below -1% (1).
  • A consistent yield spread (10Y-FFR) above 0% or exceeding 1.5%, moving out of the inverted or flat conditions (8).
  • Industrial production Year-over-Year growth falling below 0% alongside continued mining sector expansion (7).
