US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Status: The regime is stable and strengthening.

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight leading indicators are aligned in expansion.
  • The Housing Lead signal (1) and Capex Intent signal (2) both show high conviction and expanding momentum.
  • Durable Goods ex-Transportation (3) was downweighted due to a lack of observation data and low interpretation confidence.
  • Coincident signals are stabilizing above contractionary thresholds.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Leading metrics align with a deeply accommodative stance gap in the Explicit Neutral Rate proxy (4).
  • Yield Curve Slope (5) and US Treasury Yield Curve (6) both confirm a transition away from restrictive inversion.
  • Tensions exist in Industrial Production (7), which remains in a Neutral regime despite positive momentum.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as leading indicators typically precede real-sector production shifts.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Sustained cyclical expansion driven by robust core business investment and housing demand.
  • Primary upside risk: Building permit authorizations accelerate to match current strength in housing starts (1).
  • Primary downside risk: Rising 30-year mortgage rates trigger rapid demand destruction in the housing sector (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Capex Intent composite Z-score falling below the 0.75 expansion threshold (2).
  • A move in the Treasury yield spread below 0%, re-signaling restrictive curve inversion (6).
