US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is currently fragile due to conflicting high-weight signals.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by a conflict between high-weight cyclical indicators: Capex Intent (1) shows clear Expansion, while the US Treasury Yield Curve (2) maintains a Bearish/Steepening posture.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized signals with High Interpretation Confidence and High Aggregation Weights, specifically business investment and yield curve momentum.
  • The Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (3) was downweighted due to data gaps and low interpretation confidence.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Neutral activity signals reinforce each other, as both the Housing Lead (4) and Industrial Production (5) are currently positioned in Neutral regimes with stabilizing momentum.
  • Significant tension exists between the Expansionary capex commitments (1) and the Bearish steepening of the yield curve (2).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call because the Yield Curve Slope (6) confirms a "uninversion" transition from deep inversion to a Flat/Neutral state, validating a cyclical shift.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Cyclical normalization as policy real rates track a stable neutral rate proxy (7).
  • Primary upside risk: A transition to a Bullish industrial regime triggered by positive momentum in manufacturing confirmers (5).
  • Primary downside risk: A return to yield curve inversion if growth expectations deteriorate, reversing the recent steepening trend (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A move in the yield spread back to 0% or negative would signal a return to a Defensive regime (2).
  • The Capex Intent composite falling below the 0.75 expansion threshold would indicate an end to the current investment upswing (1).
  • Confirmed industrial production contraction (YoY < 0%) would necessitate a downgrade to a Risk-Negative state (5).
