US Growth and Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive.
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence.
  • Status: The regime is stable.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by high-weight cyclical leads in housing (1) and capital expenditure intent (2).
  • Both primary drivers show robust expansionary momentum and high interpretation confidence.
  • Industrial production (3) was downweighted as it remains a neutral, lagging anchor.
  • Durable goods (4) was downweighted due to missing data and low conviction.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Housing (1) and Capex (2) reinforce a strengthening cyclical trend.
  • Monetary conditions are aligned, with policy rates currently in an accommodative posture (5).
  • Yield curve signals (6)(7) confirm the transition from inversion toward expansionary steepening.
  • Neutral output in industrial production (3) reflects a lag rather than a contraction.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Persistent cyclical expansion supported by equipment investment and residential construction.
  • Primary upside risk: Sharp decline in mortgage rates accelerating the housing pipeline (1).
  • Primary downside risk: "Demand destruction" triggered by mortgage rates reverting toward 7.5% (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • Housing composite three-month change falling below the -0.60 cautionary threshold (1).
  • Capex Intent composite Z-score reversing below the 0.75 expansion threshold (2).
