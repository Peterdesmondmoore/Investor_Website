US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile, marked by conflicting growth indicators and an evolving monetary backdrop.

Why This Regime:

  • The most credible interpretation is a transitional regime due to divergence among high-weight signals. Cyclical durable goods orders indicate a bullish expansion (1). Conversely, industrial production shows a bearish contraction (2).
  • Both high-weight yield curve signals are transitioning. The Yield Curve Slope is Flat/Neutral, trending towards steepening (3). The US Treasury Yield Curve signal also indicates steepening, moving away from an inverted state (4). This collective movement from key structural and cyclical indicators defines the transitional nature.
  • The Explicit Neutral Rate Proxy signal, a medium-weight cyclical indicator, shows policy real rates are "Near neutral" with a "Rising" neutral rate, supporting a balanced but evolving monetary stance (5).
  • The Housing Lead Signal was downweighted to a medium aggregation weight hint due to its Low Conviction, Mixed Signals, and Internal Conflict Flag being 'Yes' (6).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Reinforcing signals are the two yield curve indicators, both pointing to a curve steepening or moving towards it (3)(4). This aligns with expectations of early to mid-cycle dynamics or anticipated policy easing. The Bullish Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal also aligns with an expansionary outlook for core manufacturing demand (1).
  • A primary tension exists between the Bullish Durable Goods ex-Transportation signal (1) and the Bearish Industrial Production signal (2). One suggests expansion, the other contraction in industrial activity.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call. The yield curve signals, with high aggregation weight and high confidence, are explicitly transitional and lean towards future risk-positive conditions. The conflicting cyclical growth signals highlight the fragile nature of this transition.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is a continued fragile balance. This involves ongoing mixed economic signals, with some sectors expanding while others contract, and monetary conditions remaining near neutral.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained acceleration in durable goods orders and a rebound in industrial production, alongside continued yield curve steepening, could shift to an Expansionary / Risk-Positive regime.
  • Primary downside risk: A synchronized downturn in durable goods orders and a deepening industrial production contraction. This, coupled with yield curve re-inversion, would indicate a Defensive / Risk-Negative shift.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A reversal in Durable Goods ex-Transportation to bearish (YoY growth below -3% or 3M growth below -1%) (1).
  • A sustained decline in industrial production year-over-year growth to negative alongside rising mining share (2).
  • The Yield Curve Slope composite falling below -0.75, indicating a re-inversion (3).

  • Conviction Band: Low | Interpretation Confidence: Mixed Signals | Internal Conflict Flag: Yes

  • Six-month annualised building permits momentum (Z_PERMIT) is negative at -0.60, while three-month annualised housing starts momentum (Z_HOUST) is positive at 0.16 (1). This divergence creates internal tension.
  • The headwind from 30-year mortgage rates (Z_MORT) is neutral at 0.04 (1), indicating less pressure from this component compared to previous periods.
  • Conditional Invalidation: A sustained decline in housing starts below 1.2M units or a rise in 30-year mortgage rates above 7.0% would invalidate the current neutral assessment towards a Contracting regime.

  • Most plausible upside risk: A rebound in building permits or a sustained acceleration in housing starts, potentially driven by further rate declines, could shift to an Expanding regime.
  • Most plausible downside risk: A synchronized decline in both permits and starts, coupled with renewed upward pressure on mortgage rates, would lead to a confirmed Contracting phase.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months), as changes in housing permits and starts typically precede broader economic shifts over several months.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium, given housing's significant contribution to GDP and its sensitivity to interest rates, making it a key cyclical indicator.

Macro Relevance:

  • This signal primarily informs the demand and credit dimensions of the macro economy.
  • It suggests the economy is in a mid-to-late cycle position, where housing momentum is either slowing or attempting to stabilize after previous pressures.
  • Typically interacts with interest rate signals (via mortgage rates), credit conditions signals (2), and broader US growth signals (3), often acting as a leading indicator for consumer confidence and investment.
