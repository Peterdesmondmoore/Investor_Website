Regime Assessment:

  • Durable Goods (4) was downweighted due to significant data gaps and low interpretation confidence.
  • Industrial Production (5) was downweighted due to internal conflict between expansionary headline growth and weak momentum confirmers.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as high-weight yield curve stability persists (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Cycle normalization as the term structure stabilizes and core investment accelerates.
  • Primary upside risk: A transition to Expansion if housing permits successfully translate into active construction starts (6).
  • Primary downside risk: Re-inversion of the yield curve if real rates rise sharply relative to growth expectations (7).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The YC_Composite falling below -0.75, indicating a return to an Inverted regime (1).
  • The Capex Intent score falling below 0.75, signaling a reversal in business investment commitments (3).
  • Industrial Production YoY growth dropping below 0%, flagging a return to contractionary conditions (5).
