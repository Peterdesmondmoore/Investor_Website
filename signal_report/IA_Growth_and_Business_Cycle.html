US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • High-weight signals for Industrial Production (1) and core manufacturing orders (2) reflect a Neutral state of stable activity.
  • The high-weight Capex Intent signal is in Contraction (3), indicating a significant slowdown in business investment momentum.
  • Housing (4) and yield curve slope (5) were downweighted due to internal conflict and mixed signal confidence.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Real-economy stability is reinforced by Industrial Production (1) and Durable Goods orders (2), which both show modest positive growth.
  • A major tension exists between stable current production and the contractionary trend in future-oriented Capex Intent (3).
  • The US Treasury Yield Curve has shifted into a steepening ("Bearish") posture (6), a common indicator of cyclical transition.
  • The Explicit Neutral Rate Proxy shows policy is "Near neutral" (7), which prevents an immediate escalation into a stress regime.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: A mid-cycle pause where growth remains positive but lacks clear expansionary catalysts.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Acceleration in core orders (>1% 3-month growth) (2) could trigger a shift to Expansionary.
  • Primary Downside Risk: Capex contraction (3) spills over into Industrial Production, leading to negative year-over-year growth.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained decline in Industrial Production YoY below 0% would trigger a shift to Defensive (1).
  • A rebound in Durable Goods orders with both YoY and 3-month growth turning positive would invalidate the Contraction label (3).
  • Yield curve inversion returning (spread < 0%) would invalidate the current steepening transition (6).
