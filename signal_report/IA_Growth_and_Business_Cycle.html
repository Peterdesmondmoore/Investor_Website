US Growth & Business Cycle
Synthesises key growth-related indicators (capex, production, durables, housing, yield-curve and monetary signals) into a cohesive assessment of the current US economic expansion, slowdown or contraction.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The regime is characterized by a balance of conflicting high-weight signals. The Capex Intent signal indicates a Contraction, reflecting a softening trend in business investment (1). Similarly, the US Treasury Yield Curve Signal (spread-based) is Bullish, indicating a flat or inverted curve, consistent with late-cycle or recessionary warnings (2).
  • These contractionary indicators are counterbalanced by the Industrial Production Signal (3) and Durable Goods ex-Transportation (4), both of which are Neutral, suggesting stable rather than contracting activity. The Yield Curve Slope Signal, while Flat/Neutral, shows strong upward momentum, nearing a Steepening regime (5). This indicates a potential easing of prior restrictive conditions.
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized signals with High Aggregation Weight and High Interpretation Confidence, such as Capex Intent (1), both Yield Curve signals (2)(5), and Industrial Production (3). All these key signals operate on a Cyclical time horizon, driving the medium-term assessment.
  • The Housing Lead Signal was downweighted due to its "Mixed Signals" Interpretation Confidence, reducing its influence on the overall regime call (6).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Industrial Production (3) and Durable Goods ex-Transportation (4) signals reinforce a neutral, stable economic picture. The Explicit Neutral Rate (r*) Proxy Signal also suggests a "Near neutral" policy stance, supporting overall stability (7).
  • Tensions exist between the contractionary signals, such as Capex Intent (1) and the inverted/flat US Treasury Yield Curve (spread-based) (2), and the more stable or positively transitioning signals like Industrial Production (3), Durable Goods ex-Transportation (4), and the upward momentum in the Yield Curve Slope Signal (5).
  • These tensions prevent a definitive classification. The presence of upward momentum in one high-weight yield curve signal (5) suggests a potential transition away from outright negative conditions. This leads to a "Transitional / Fragile Balance" rather than a fully "Defensive" regime.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: The economy remains in a fragile balance, with stable industrial activity offsetting softening business investment. Policy rates are near neutral.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained re-steepening of the yield curve, pushing it beyond the Flat/Neutral threshold (5). This would be accompanied by a rebound in capex intent (1).
  • Primary downside risk: A deepening contraction in capex intent (1) combined with a re-inversion of the yield curve (2). This would signal a more pronounced economic slowdown.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained rebound in durable goods orders, leading the Capex Intent signal to shift to Neutral or Expansion (1).
  • A clear and persistent steepening of the US Treasury yield curve, pushing its composite value definitively above 0.75 (5).
  • A decline in Industrial Production year-over-year growth below 0% while the mining share of GDP remains positive (3).
