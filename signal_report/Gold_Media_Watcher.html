Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Gold narratives this week were characterized by high volatility, ultimately concluding with a record weekly high close near $5,069. Initial downward pressure was driven by a significantly stronger-than-expected U.S. nonfarm payrolls report, which dampened immediate interest rate cut expectations and bolstered the U.S. Dollar. However, the narrative shifted toward safe-haven support as investors reacted to heightened geopolitical risks in the Middle East and a sharp retreat in U.S. Treasury yields. Structural demand remains a dominant positive theme, with reports highlighting record-setting investment in China and continued central bank accumulation. Supply-side developments were also supportive, featuring major capital raises for North American mine expansion and the resolution of significant licensing disputes in Africa.
