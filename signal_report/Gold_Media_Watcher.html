Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Gold narrative sentiment remained largely constructive over the last seven days as prices frequently tested and reclaimed the $5,000/oz threshold. Support was primarily driven by safe-haven demand linked to heightening geopolitical tensions in West Asia and a cooling U.S. inflation outlook following soft CPI data. However, these gains were periodically countered by a narrative of "higher-for-longer" interest rates fueled by strong U.S. employment figures and a firming U.S. Dollar. Physical demand remains a critical driver, with robust jewellery and investment activity reported in India, though the Chinese Lunar New Year resulted in a temporary seasonal lull in Asian liquidity. Structural shifts are also gaining media attention, specifically regarding the potential for new BRICS-led market infrastructure and increased consolidation activity in the gold mining sector.
