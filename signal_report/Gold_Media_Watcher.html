Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Narrative sentiment for gold was mixed this week, as safe-haven support from Middle East geopolitical tensions contended with a strengthening U.S. dollar and shifting interest rate expectations. Physical demand remains a pillar of the supportive narrative, highlighted by the People's Bank of China extending its gold purchasing streak to 16 months and resilient wholesale demand in Asia. However, the metal faced headwinds from sticky inflation data and rising U.S. Treasury yields, which have delayed expectations for Federal Reserve policy easing. Supply-side narratives focused on rising "urban mining" in Japan and increased production costs linked to higher energy prices. Overall, the market remains volatile as participants weigh the metal's role as a geopolitical shock absorber against the opportunity cost of elevated real rates.
