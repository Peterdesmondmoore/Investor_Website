Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Gold narratives this week were characterized by high volatility as prices frequently tested the $5,000 psychological threshold. Sentiment was supported by safe-haven demand stemming from geopolitical risks in the Middle East and structural demand from Asian markets, specifically China. However, strong U.S. labor market data acted as a significant headwind, cooling Federal Reserve rate cut expectations and periodically pressuring the metal. Narratives conflicted between bullish technical record closes and bearish macro reactions to persistent U.S. economic resilience. Central bank buying remains a persistent supportive theme, while easing geopolitical tensions and higher Treasury yields provided the primary downward pressure.
