Macro Signal Synthesiser Summary

Conditions-based macro assessment for Federal Reserve policy justification.

1. Macro Conditions Summary

The current US macroeconomic environment is characterized by a decisive transition from a restrictive to a cooling inflation regime, juxtaposed with a labor market that remains at equilibrium but exhibits softening momentum. Realized inflation pressures have moderated into a COOL regime (1), while core policy-relevant PCE measures remain Neutral (2). Growth is moderating, evidenced by a bifurcated consumer and a gradual shift toward more expansionary financial conditions. While the canonical labor slack gap is NEUTRAL and STABLE (3), underlying signals—specifically bearish labor turnover and softening hiring momentum (4)—suggest the balance of risks is shifting from inflation persistence toward growth and labor-market downside. Systemic liquidity has already begun an Expansionary impulse (5), largely driven by broad money re-acceleration (6).

2. Why a Simple Taylor-Style Rule Is Insufficient

Mechanical policy rules are currently inadequate as they fail to account for the time-varying nature of policy reaction coefficients and the risk asymmetry inherent in a regime transition. A simple rule focusing on point estimates of headline inflation would ignore the COOL momentum and the STABLE but fragile state of the labor slack gap (3). In the current regime, the weight of the dual mandate has shifted; with inflation expectations anchored and realized inflation cooling, the uncertainty regarding the "stall speed" of the labor market and elevated financial stress (8) dominates the policy calculus. Rigid rules cannot capture the liquidity impulse or the proximity of the current policy rate to a potentially rising Neutral Rate (r*) (7), which requires a flexible, forward-looking calibration to avoid unintended over-tightening.

3. Reaction-Function Relevant Signals

  • Inflation dynamics: The composite inflation signal is COOL (1), driven by weak momentum, even as PCE remains Neutral (2) and market-implied expectations remain STABLE (12).
  • Labour market: The Explicit Slack Gap is NEUTRAL and STABLE (-0.12%), but momentum is Softening (4) with Bearish worker turnover confidence (13).
  • Growth & demand: The cycle is in a Moderating Expansion phase; while industrial demand tailwinds are active (10), consumer sentiment remains Bearish (14).
  • Financial conditions: Liquidity is Expansionary (5) and M2 is Bullish (6), but a Bearish Financial Stress Index (8) indicates systemic tension. The policy stance is NEAR NEUTRAL relative to r* (7).
  • Risk asymmetry: Upside inflation risks (tariff-driven) are noted, but downside growth/labor risks are increasing in significance as hiring decelerates. High Squeeze Risk (11) in positioning suggests market fragility.
