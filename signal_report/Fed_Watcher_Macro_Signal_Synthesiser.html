Macro Signal Synthesiser Summary

Conditions-based macro assessment for Federal Reserve policy justification.

1. Macro Conditions Summary

The US macroeconomic environment is currently transitioning through a cooling phase characterized by a decisive loss of inflation momentum and an incremental softening of the labour market (1). Inflation dynamics have shifted into a COOL regime, driven by weak realized price momentum and stable market-implied expectations, although policy-relevant PCE measures remain stickier at approximately 2.8% (2)(3). The labour market remains in a Neutral aggregate state, yet signals of underlying fragility are emerging, specifically via a rising U-6 underemployment rate and bearish worker turnover sentiment (4). While the explicit Slack Gap is currently Neutral and Stable, the momentum in job openings and hiring suggests a move toward a wider gap in the forward-looking horizon (5). Growth is moderating toward a mid-cycle stall, while credit conditions remain benign despite elevated systemic stress scores (6).

2. Why a Simple Taylor-Style Rule Is Insufficient

Mechanical policy rules are insufficient in the current regime because they rely on static reaction coefficients that fail to account for the non-linear risks of a cooling labour market. Currently, risk asymmetry dominates point estimates; while inflation remains above target, the downside risk to growth and employment—signaled by waning labour dynamism—carries a higher policy penalty than a marginal inflation overshoot. Furthermore, the time-varying nature of the neutral rate (r*) and shifting mandate weights during regime transitions mean that a fixed rule would likely result in a policy error by ignoring the "long and variable lags" of the current restrictive real-rate stance. Persistence and uncertainty regarding tariff-driven price shocks versus organic disinflation require a flexible reaction function that prioritizes regime stability over mechanical averages.

3. Reaction-Function Relevant Signals

  • Inflation dynamics: The regime is COOL with stable direction and anchored expectations, though core persistence remains a secondary concern (2).
  • Labour market: Composite regime is NEUTRAL but softening; the Explicit Slack Gap is currently NEUTRAL and STABLE with medium confidence (5)(7).
  • Growth & demand: Mid-cycle phase with moderating momentum; demand tailwinds in industrial sectors are offset by price sensitivity in lower-income cohorts (6).
  • Financial conditions: Liquidity impulse is EXPANSIONARY due to ON RRP unwinds (8); policy stance is NEAR NEUTRAL relative to a stable r* (9). Credit conditions are NORMAL but widening momentum suggests tightening risk (10)(11).
  • Risk asymmetry: Downside growth risks (rising U-6 and bearish consumer sentiment) are beginning to outweigh upside inflation risks, given the "COOL" inflation signal (12).

4. Macro Directional Bias (Conditions Only)

HOLD

This reflects economic justification only: While inflation is cooling and labour is softening, the neutral status of the Slack Gap and the proximity of current rates to the estimated neutral rate (r*) suggest that macro conditions do not yet decisively justify a policy departure from the current "Near Neutral" stance.

5. Confidence Score

MEDIUM, as the cooling labour signals (U-6) and "COOL" inflation regime provide a high degree of coherence, but the "Stable" Slack Gap and "Normal" credit spreads act as significant signal contradictions that enforce regime inertia.

This assessment reflects economic justification, not policy timing or decision-making.
