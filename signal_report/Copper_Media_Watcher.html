Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Copper narratives this week were primarily shaped by supply-side concerns, which largely supported prices. Significant global mine disruptions, including a mudslide in Indonesia and labor strikes in Chile, combined with reduced production guidance from Lundin and delayed output peaks from Chile, fueled persistent worries about future supply. This tightness was underscored by copper prices rallying above $13,000 and a spike in LME prompt spreads, indicating strong demand for immediate delivery. However, some counter-narratives emerged, with U.S. Comex inventories rising, Rio Tinto's output beating estimates, and LME spreads easing later in the week, suggesting a potential short-lived nature of the immediate market squeeze. Geopolitical efforts by the DRC to secure supply chains and policy discussions around future tariffs also contributed to the complex market context.
