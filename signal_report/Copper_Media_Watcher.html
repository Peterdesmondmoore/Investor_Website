Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

Copper narratives shifted this week from record-high optimism toward concerns over physical oversupply and macro-economic headwinds. While long-term demand drivers related to electrification and a potential "commodity supercycle" remain a foundational theme, immediate price action was pressured by reports of record-high refined production in China and growing inventories in the U.S. Supportive data from Chinese manufacturing earlier in the period was eventually overshadowed by a strengthening U.S. Dollar, rising yields, and geopolitical instability in the Middle East. Additionally, the commencement of new domestic supply in the U.S. and the resolution of logistics bottlenecks in Africa have further eased concerns regarding near-term supply tightness, contributing to a bearish tone at week's end.
