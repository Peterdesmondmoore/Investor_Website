Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

The copper narrative is currently defined by a tension between record-high physical inventories and aggressive long-term bullishness from major producers. Global exchange stocks surpassed 1 million tonnes this week, reaching a 21-year high, which has exerted significant downward pressure on futures and prompted debates regarding "scarcity vs. plenty." However, corporate fundamentals remain exceptionally strong, with major miners reporting record profits and pivoting exploration budgets heavily toward the "red metal" to meet anticipated AI-driven demand. Regulatory and trade shifts also provided support, specifically the striking down of US tariffs. Overall, the narrative remains constructive on demand-side growth while cautious regarding immediate supply overhangs and macroeconomic headwinds from the Federal Reserve.
