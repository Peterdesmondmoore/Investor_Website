Weekly Narrative Sentiment (Contextual)

The narrative for Copper this week is characterized by a conflict between record-high physical inventories and optimistic long-term industrial demand. Exchange stocks surpassing 1 million tonnes for the first time in over two decades has created a supply overhang that pressures near-term futures. However, this is countered by exceptionally strong corporate earnings from major miners, who are increasingly pivoting their capital toward copper to meet anticipated AI-driven demand. While the removal of certain trade tariffs provided a temporary price catalyst, macro headwinds from cautious Federal Reserve policy and refining bottlenecks in the US remain persistent themes. Overall, the market is navigating a transition where long-term structural scarcity narratives are being tested by immediate physical abundance.
