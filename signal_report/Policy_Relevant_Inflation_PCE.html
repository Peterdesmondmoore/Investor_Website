Gemini Summary

Signal Summary:

  • The Policy-Relevant Inflation (PCE) signal is currently in a "Neutral" regime as of September 2025.
  • Headline PCE inflation is at 2.79% and Core PCE inflation is at 2.83% (1).
  • The composite robust z-score of -0.335789 indicates moderate and stable inflation, well within the neutral range and below historical extremes.

Key Dynamics:

  • The signal is driven by an equal-weight average of the robust z-scores for headline and core PCE year-over-year inflation (1).
  • Both z-scores, Z_PCE (-0.25) and Z_cPCE (-0.42), are negative but have trended upward since April 2025, suggesting a slight re-acceleration from recent lows.
  • While core PCE inflation has been slightly weaker in z-score terms, both components align to maintain a "Neutral" signal.

Macro Relevance:

  • This signal directly informs the pricing dimension of the macro environment and overall inflationary pressures.
  • As a key input to monetary policy, it functions as a mid-cycle indicator, reflecting underlying price stability or instability.
  • It often interacts with wage growth, consumer spending, and interest rate expectations, influencing central bank decisions on liquidity.

Data & References:

  • PCEPI (Headline PCE), PCEPILFE (Core PCE), latest observation 2025-09-30.
  • The PCE_YoY and CorePCE_YoY, and their robust z-scores, most influenced the signal's current state.
  • Additional depth could be gained from CPI (Consumer Price Index) and the ISM Prices Paid Index.
