Gold Research Analyst

6-12 Month Stance

Stance: bullish

Confidence: high

Core Narrative

The outlook for gold over the next 12 months remains overwhelmingly constructive, following a historic performance in 2025 and a surge above the US$5,000/oz threshold in January 2026 (1)(18). This strength is underpinned by structural shifts including aggressive central bank diversification away from US dollar reserves, persistent geopolitical instability, and a "fiscal dominance" environment where government spending priorities outweigh traditional monetary controls (12)(14)(16). While the market experienced a sharp 9% technical reversal in early 2026 after hitting record highs, fundamental demand from both institutions and retail investors, particularly via ETFs, remains at record levels (15)(17). Analysts anticipate prices will average between US$4,500 and US$5,055/oz by the fourth quarter of 2026, supported by a projected easing cycle from the Federal Reserve and a weakening US dollar (1)(13).

Key Drivers

Sustained Central Bank Accumulation

Directional implication: supportive

Central banks are executing a long-term strategic shift to diversify foreign exchange reserves. Purchases reached 328 tonnes in 2025, with major buyers including Poland, Kazakhstan, and China (16). Survey data indicates that 95% of central bankers expect gold reserves to continue increasing as they reduce reliance on the US dollar (3)(14).

Monetary Policy Decoupling and Lower Rates

Directional implication: supportive

Expected Federal Reserve rate cuts are reducing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets. Even as other central banks like the Bank of Japan move to hike rates, the broad trend of Fed easing and a softer US dollar acts as a primary catalyst for gold price appreciation (1)(9)(10).

Fiscal Dominance and Currency Debasement

Directional implication: supportive

Persistent fiscal deficits in G20 economies and a "run-it-hot" policy playbook prioritize growth over inflation control. This structural shift toward hard assets serves as a hedge against the loss of purchasing power in fiat currencies (12)(15).

Key Risks

Reflationary Growth Success

If pro-growth fiscal policies result in a significantly stronger-than-expected economy without a corresponding surge in inflation, gold could face a correction of 5% to 20% as risk appetite returns to equities (4).

Elevated Real Yields

While gold has recently diverged from its traditional inverse relationship with real yields, a breakout of the 10-year US Real Yield above major resistance levels could create a substantial headwind for the asset (5)(14).

Technical Exhaustion and Volatility

After a staggering 14% rally in a single month, gold is susceptible to sharp reversals driven by option market activity and implied volatility. Rapid sell-offs, such as the 9% drop seen in early 2026, demonstrate the risk of "momentum-driven" price overextension (15)(18).

Signposts to Watch

US Federal Reserve Leadership Transitions

Would confirm: The nomination and guidance of a dovish Fed Chair (e.g., Kevin Warsh) would likely reinforce expectations of rate cuts and dollar weakness, supporting the bullish case (5)(17).

Would invalidate: A hawkish shift in guidance or the abandonment of rate cut expectations due to sticky inflation would pressure gold prices downward (18).

Monthly Gold ETF Inflow Persistence

Would confirm: Continued inflows following the record US$19bn added in January 2026 would signal that the retail and institutional "buy-the-dip" mentality remains intact (17).

Would invalidate: Sustained outflows from ETFs would suggest that investors are rotating out of safe havens and into base metals or equities (15)(20).

Scenarios

Sustained Geopolitical and Macro Uncertainty

Gold maintains its upward trajectory, averaging approximately US$4,500 to US$5,000/oz by year-end 2026, driven by central bank buying and gradual Fed easing (9)(13).

Implied stance: bullish

Key drivers: Central Bank Accumulation; Lower Fed Rates

"Doom Loop" / Synchronized Slowdown

A deeper and more synchronized global economic slowdown, combined with intensifying geopolitical and trade risks, could trigger a gold price surge of 15% to 30% from current levels (4).

Implied stance: bullish

Key drivers: Geopolitical Uncertainty; Recession Risks

Reflation and Policy Success

Stronger-than-expected growth fueled by fiscal policy success leads to a significant price correction, potentially as high as 20% (4).

Implied stance: bearish

Key drivers: Risk Appetite; Economic Growth

Disagreements & Uncertainties

Gold's Correlation with Real Yields

There is a notable divergence in how analysts view the traditional inverse relationship between gold and real yields (5)(14).

  • Gold prices are increasingly driven by geopolitical hedging and sovereign diversification, which now outweigh the opportunity cost of rising yields (14).
  • Rising 10-year US Real Yields remain a significant headwind that could cap gold's gains if key resistance levels are broken (5).
