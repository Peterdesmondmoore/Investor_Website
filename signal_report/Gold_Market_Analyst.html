Gold Research Analyst

6–12 Month Stance

Stance: bullish

Confidence: high

Core Narrative

Precious metals, particularly gold and silver, are anticipated to experience sustained gains over the next 6-12 months, extending their strong performance from 2025 (16)(19)(20)(22). A confluence of factors including persistent geopolitical and geoeconomic uncertainty, expectations of a weaker US dollar, and anticipated Fed rate cuts are expected to drive this bullish trend (8)(12)(15)(18)(19)(21). Gold is further supported by robust central bank accumulation and strong investment demand, especially through ETFs (6)(7)(15)(22). Silver, in addition to macro tailwinds, benefits from tight supply, increasing industrial applications, and its role in AI-related hardware (18)(19)(21)(22)(23). Analysts forecast gold prices to reach new highs, with several projections ranging from US$4,200/oz to US$5,000/oz by year-end 2026 (13)(15)(17)(18)(19). Silver is also expected to outperform, with forecasts up to US$65/oz (18)(19)(21).

Key Drivers

Central Bank Gold Accumulation

Directional implication: supportive

Central banks, predominantly from emerging markets, have significantly increased net gold purchases, with a "79% m/m" increase in September 2025 and "the highest month of reported net buying in 2025 so far" in October 2025 (2)(4). This activity is described as strategic, reinforcing gold’s importance amid persistent macroeconomic uncertainty (4). Year-to-date, central banks have accumulated "200t of net gold buying" through September, reaching "254 tonnes" through October (2)(4). Notably, the National Bank of Poland plans to almost double its gold reserves to 100t by 2030, and a 2025 survey indicated that "95% of respondents expect central bank gold reserves to increase" (4). This sustained demand strengthens "Gold’s Role as a Core Store of Value" (22).

Persistent Geopolitical and Geoeconomic Uncertainty

Directional implication: supportive

The outlook for 2026 is shaped by "ongoing geoeconomic uncertainty and continued market volatility" (8). Global growth remains uneven, contending with "policy uncertainty and trade realignments" (5). Geopolitical tensions, particularly in Russia and Venezuela, showed "no signs of easing" (14), while "broader unease over the fiscal sustainability of many G20 economies" and a "fraught global geopolitical environment also adds more support for a bullish gold case" (19). This uncertainty, alongside concerns over "currency debasement," contributes to gold’s "strong tailwinds entering 2026" and safe-haven demand (15)(20).

Expectations of US Dollar Weakness and Fed Rate Cuts

Directional implication: supportive

Gold's strong performance has been supported by "a weaker US dollar" (8). Anticipated US policy rate cuts are a key driver for investment (6). J.P. Morgan expects the Federal Reserve to cut rates by another "50 basis points (bp)" (1), and "further Fed easing expected to remain the dominant driver" for a bearish bias on the USD, with the DXY "declining through 2026" (15). The US Dollar (DXY) "appears to be turning lower again," which is seen as a "supportive driver for Gold" (14)(18)(19). Expectations of "Lower rates from the Fed will keep investor enthusiasm for gold strong" (19).

Robust Investment Demand

Directional implication: supportive

Global gold demand reached a new quarterly record in Q3 2025, driven primarily by investment, including "Huge ETF buying (+222t)" and robust "bar and coin demand (316t)" (6). Global physically-backed gold ETFs recorded their "sixth consecutive monthly inflow in November 2025," reaching a "new month-end peak of US$530bn," with inflows "on track for their strongest year ever" (7). Gold continues to serve as an effective diversifier against "richly valued equity markets" and helps "reduce overall risk" in a diversified portfolio (6)(10). In India, "investment demand holds firm," with cumulative net inflows into gold ETFs reaching "a record INR313bn (US$3.6bn)" for the first 11 months of 2025 (9).

Tight Supply and Industrial Demand for Silver

Directional implication: supportive

Silver's medium-term picture is constructive, benefiting from its "dual role as a precious and industrial metal," supported by tight supply (16). The silver market has experienced a "deficit for four consecutive years," leading to "physical tightness...evident in key markets" (21)(23). Industrial demand is expected to rise with easing monetary policy and greater economic activity (21), driven by "structural gains in the green economy," such as photovoltaics and vehicle electrification, as well as "AI-related consumer electronics" (23). Silver is predicted to outperform gold due to a "persistent supply deficit" and strong industrial demand (18). Its "dynamic bull case" is powered by its dual role as a monetary hedge and an industrial metal (22).

Key Risks

Stronger-than-Expected Economic Growth

A "reflation return" scenario, where "policies set by the Trump administration succeed, resulting in stronger-than-expected growth," could lead to a gold price correction of "between 5% and 20%" (8).

Rising US Real Yields

While the US Dollar appears to be turning lower, "10-year US Real Yields continue to edge higher," which could act as a "headwind for Gold" if they break significant resistance levels (14).

Moderating Gold ETF Inflows

Despite strong overall inflows, "slowing gold ETF inflows" were observed as contributing to gold's rangebound performance in a recent period, suggesting that a significant slowdown could dampen upward momentum (14).

Signposts to Watch

Federal Reserve Monetary Policy Decisions

Would confirm: Further Fed rate cuts, anticipated to be "another 50 basis points (bp)" (1), or the potential for a "more dovish successor to Chair Powell," would reinforce expectations for a weaker dollar and lower yields, supporting gold prices (14)(15).

Would invalidate: A more hawkish stance or unexpected pauses in rate cuts by the Federal Reserve, deviating from easing expectations, could remove a significant supportive driver for precious metals (8)(15).

US Dollar Index (DXY) Movements

Would confirm: Continued weakening of the US Dollar (DXY), which "appears to be turning lower again," would signal a supportive environment for gold (14).

Would invalidate: A reversal of the US Dollar's trajectory to sustained strength could remove a key tailwind for gold, which has been supported by "a weaker US dollar" (8)(12)(15)(18)(19)(21).

Central Bank Gold Purchase Data

Would confirm: Sustained elevated central bank purchases, particularly from emerging markets which were the "primary buyer" in September and October 2025, would indicate ongoing strategic demand and validate gold's importance as a reserve asset (2)(4)(6)(7)(8)(15)(22).

Would invalidate: A significant and sustained decline in net central bank gold purchases, moving away from the "elevated" levels seen, could signal a reduction in a major demand pillar (2)(4)(6).

Geopolitical Developments

Would confirm: An increase in "geopolitical and geoeconomic risk" leading to a "deeper and more synchronised slowdown" would strengthen gold's safe-haven appeal, potentially leading to a "surge 15% – 30% in 2026" (8)(14)(19).

Would invalidate: A significant and sustained de-escalation of global geopolitical tensions could diminish gold's safe-haven appeal, potentially redirecting investment flows to riskier assets (8)(19).

Silver Industrial Demand Trends

Would confirm: Continued or increased industrial demand driven by "AI-related hardware" and "structural gains in the green economy" would strengthen silver's bullish case, especially given its "tight availability" and persistent market deficit (18)(19)(21)(23).

Would invalidate: A slowdown in industrial activity or a significant reduction in silver consumption per watt in photovoltaic technologies due to "thrifting," could dampen demand for silver (21)(23).
