Lithium Research Analyst

6–12 Month Stance

Stance: bullish

Confidence: high

Core Narrative

The global lithium market is projected to experience a sustained recovery and growth over the next 6-12 months, driven primarily by robust demand from the Energy Stationary Storage (ESS) battery sector. This surge in ESS demand is expected to pull the market into a deficit, leading to significant upgrades in both short-term and long-term lithium price forecasts (2)(3). While overall lithium supply is expected to increase, miners have indicated a cautious approach to restarting idle capacity, typically requiring consistent trading at higher price levels, which suggests supply may take time to fully respond (2)(3). The strengthening market fundamentals for lithium are aligned with the broader global energy transition, which is driving record investment in clean energy and increasing demand for critical transition metals (1)(5).

Key Drivers

Energy Stationary Storage (ESS) Demand

Directional implication: supportive

Material upgrades to Energy Stationary Storage battery production estimates (+50%/+43% in 2025/26) are pushing medium-term supply/demand forecasts into a deficit (2). ESS is projected to represent 30% of lithium demand in 2026, rising to 36% by 2030 (2). This sector is emerging as the "most significant and outperforming driver of lithium-ion battery consumption," with forecasts indicating "year on year growth of 40-55% from these segments" such as data centers and grid-scale storage (3)(5).

Global Energy Transition

Directional implication: supportive

The global energy transition is driving "record-breaking global investment in clean energy," projected to reach "USD 2.2 trillion for 2025—double the level of fossil fuel spending" (1). This transformation inherently boosts demand for critical energy transition metals like lithium, copper, nickel, and rare earths (1). The "strengthening market fundamentals across the lithium and rare earth markets" are directly linked to this accelerating demand from technology and energy-related industries (5).

Tightening Supply and Market Deficit

Directional implication: supportive

The global lithium carbonate market surplus is anticipated to reduce to "109,000 metric tons of lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) in 2026, down from 141,000 mt in 2025," driven by demand outpacing supply growth (3). J.P. Morgan's outlook explicitly states that "ESS demand to pull the market into deficit" (2). Supply is expected to take time to respond, as "several miners have stated they won’t look at restarting idle capacity unless the market consistently trades at $1200-1500/t" (2).

Key Risks

Geopolitical Tensions and Supply Shortages

"Potential geopolitical tensions in Nigeria and the cautious approach of Australian miners could lead to periodic supply shortages if prices remain sustained above $1,000/mt for six months" (3). For rare earths, "supply constraints and geopolitical tension converged with accelerating demand" to drive prices sharply higher, indicating similar risks for lithium (5).

Slower-than-expected EV Demand Growth

While "global sales of electric vehicles (EV) are expected to continue growing in 2026," demand growth from the traditional passenger electric vehicle segment is anticipated to be modest (3). If this segment performs below current expectations, it could temper overall lithium demand, even though ESS is the primary driver (3).

Signposts to Watch

Lithium Price Levels

Would confirm: Consistent trading of the market at "$1200-1500/t" (2) or prices remaining "sustained above $1,000/mt for six months" (3) would confirm market strength and incentivize miners to restart idle capacity. Upward trending "China's lithium carbonate prices" in late 2025 and early 2026, with prices moving "above $20,000 per tonne for the first time since November 2023" (3)(5).

Would invalidate: A reversal of the upward price trend or failure of lithium prices to consistently trade above key thresholds, potentially deterring supply response (2)(3).

Energy Stationary Storage (ESS) Deployment Data

Would confirm: Continued "Material upgrades to our Energy Stationary Storage battery production estimates (+50%/+43% in 2025/26)" (2) and sustained "year on year growth of 40-55% from these segments" (5) would validate the primary demand driver.

Would invalidate: Significant deceleration in ESS battery production estimates or actual deployment numbers falling short of forecasts, indicating a weakening of the primary demand driver (2)(5).

Speed and responsiveness of lithium supply to rising prices
