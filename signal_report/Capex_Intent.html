Gemini Summary

The market currently reflects significant volatility in business investment sentiment, as indicated by the Capex Intent signal. After registering an 'Expansion' in May 2025 (Capex Intent: 4.98), the signal sharply reversed to 'Contraction' in August 2025 (Capex Intent: -1.55), reflecting weakening durable goods orders. The latest data for September 2025 shows a rebound to a 'Neutral' regime (Capex Intent: 0.14), with durable goods orders seeing a 7.24% YoY growth and 2.45% 3-month annualized growth. This rapid oscillation suggests underlying uncertainty for corporate earnings, potential headwinds for industrial metals, and mixed signals for broader economic growth impacting bonds and safe-haven assets.

Technical Summary: Technically, the Capex Intent signal exhibits extreme short-term volatility and a lack of sustained trend. The rapid shift from Expansion to Contraction and back to Neutral within months (e.g., +4.98 in May, -1.55 in August, +0.14 in September) suggests an environment where short-term momentum shifts quickly, potentially leading to whipsaws. The rebound to Neutral from Contraction could be seen as a corrective bounce, but the overall pattern suggests continued choppiness in capital expenditure cycles.
