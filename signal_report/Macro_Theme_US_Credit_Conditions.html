US Credit Conditions: synthesising credit spreads, financial stress and private credit dynamics.

As of 2025-12-10

Gemini Summary

US Credit Conditions: synthesising credit spreads, financial stress and private credit dynamics.

Executive Summary

The US credit environment concludes the year 2025 largely in a neutral state, characterized by tight credit spreads and stable private credit growth. However, a divergence emerges within systemic stress indicators, with the St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index signaling elevated stress while other composites remain neutral. This composite view suggests a generally supportive backdrop for credit markets, albeit with an underlying tension from broader financial stress.

  • HY & IG spread regimes: NORMAL
  • Financial stress state: Neutral (Financial Stress Composite) / Bearish (STLFSI4 Index)
  • Credit Conditions Composite classification: Neutral
  • Private credit impulse direction: Stable
  • Overall credit environment: Neutral with noted systemic stress divergence

1. Credit Spreads

As of 2025-12-31, US High Yield (HY) and Investment Grade (IG) option-adjusted spreads (OAS) are classified within a NORMAL regime. HY OAS stands at 2.89% and IG OAS at 0.78%, indicative of historically tight conditions. The Credit Spreads Composite (CSC) registers a z-score of -0.19, consistent with its NORMAL classification.

Individual z-scores for HY (-0.69) and IG (-0.82) levels suggest significant negative stretch relative to historical averages. The 3-month annualised HY z-score of 0.54 indicates some recent upward pressure, despite overall tight levels. Both HY and IG demonstrate extremely low 10-year percentile ranks at 6.7% and 1.7% respectively, reflecting current positioning in the historically tighter range.

2. Systemic Financial Stress

Systemic financial stress signals present a mixed picture. The Financial Stress Composite, integrating STLFSI4, HY OAS, and the inverted T10Y2Y yield spread, registers a value of -0.56 as of 2025-12-31 (with STLFSI4 data as of 2025-11-30), placing it in a Neutral regime. Negative z-scores for HY OAS (-0.51) and the inverted yield curve (-0.61) contribute to this lower stress reading.

Conversely, the standalone St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4) signal, as of 2025-11-28, is classified as Bearish, with its z-score at 0.76. This indicates above-average stress levels within the financial system according to the STLFSI4's methodology, highlighting a divergence between the composite view and the direct STLFSI4 signal.

3. Credit Conditions Composite

The Credit Conditions Composite, as of 2025-12-31, records a value of -0.32, classifying the environment as Neutral. This composite incorporates robust-normalised z-scores of HY OAS, BBB OAS, and the VIX. The current reading reflects a normal funding backdrop, with spreads and volatility near typical ranges.

Individual components, including the HY OAS z-score (-0.46) and BBB OAS z-score (-0.53), indicate an easing bias. The VIX z-score (0.04) suggests near-median equity volatility, which contributes to the overall neutral assessment of credit conditions.

4. Private Credit Impulse

The Private Credit Impulse, as of 2025-11-30 (with partial data for November and full data for October 2025), indicates a Stable regime, with a composite value of 0.49. This follows a period of "Decelerating" credit growth observed in Q1 2025. The component for Total Bank Credit annualised 6-month growth contributes a z-score of 0.49 to the current stable reading.

While Commercial & Industrial Loans and Total Consumer Loans data were not available for November 2025, their October 2025 values indicated z-scores of 0.26 and -0.36 respectively. This suggests a general recovery from earlier deceleration, implying steady credit growth consistent with trend economic expansion in the private sector.

5. Synthesised US Credit Conditions Assessment

The overall US credit environment is predominantly Neutral, characterized by generally tight credit spreads and stable private credit growth momentum. Both the Credit Spreads Composite and the Credit Conditions Composite indicate "NORMAL" or "Neutral" regimes. Similarly, the broader Financial Stress Composite also reflects a "Neutral" state. However, a notable divergence exists with the direct St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4) signal, which is assessed as "Bearish." This implies that while corporate credit markets and a broader financial stress composite remain benign, the underlying STLFSI4, a multi-factor measure, indicates a more elevated level of systemic stress, suggesting a potential tension between market-based credit indicators and a more comprehensive stress measure.

6. Forward Risks

Upside / Supportive

  • Continued or accelerating private credit growth
  • Contained volatility and stable funding conditions
  • Persistently tight spreads and subdued stress readings

Downside / Stress

  • Spread widening across HY and IG
  • Financial stress composite rising
  • Renewed deceleration or contraction in private credit growth

7. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

The current credit regime implies a largely benign risk sentiment environment for corporate credit, supported by tight spreads and neutral funding conditions. This backdrop suggests a favorable environment for refinancing and generally contained immediate default risk, particularly within the investment-grade segment. The "Stable" private credit impulse further supports a constructive view on fundamental credit quality, aligning with an environment conducive to business expansion.

However, the "Bearish" signal from the underlying STLFSI4 index introduces an element of caution, suggesting potential systemic vulnerabilities that are not fully reflected in credit spreads or the broader credit conditions composite. This divergence indicates a need for continued monitoring of broader financial stability metrics, as they may portend shifts in the macro/financial cycle not yet evident in credit pricing.
