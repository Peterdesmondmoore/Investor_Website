Executive Summary

As of late November 2025, the overall US credit environment is characterised by generally benign conditions, with indicators predominantly registering within 'Normal' or 'Neutral' regimes. While credit spreads remain historically tight, some underlying components suggest a moderation or slight upward pressure from extreme lows. Private credit impulse is assessed as 'Stable', contributing to a picture of balanced credit growth momentum, although this specific composite is currently affected by partial data availability.

  • Spread Regime: The Credit Spreads Composite (CSC) is in a NORMAL regime. High Yield (HY) and Investment Grade (IG) spreads are at low absolute levels, though HY momentum shows a recent upward bias.
  • Financial Stress State: The Financial Stress Composite is in a Neutral regime. Systemic stress indicators are within typical ranges, despite an underlying elevation in the STLFSI4 component.
  • Credit Conditions Composite: The broader Credit Conditions Index registers as Neutral. This reflects a transition from an 'Easing' environment earlier in the year towards more typical funding conditions.
  • Private Credit Impulse: The Private Credit Impulse is in a Stable regime. This indicates consistent momentum in private sector credit growth, albeit with some recent data incompleteness affecting the composite calculation.
  • Overall Credit Environment: The synthesised view points to a Neutral to Benign credit environment, balancing historically tight spreads with a moderate financial stress outlook and stable credit growth.

1. Credit Spreads

The Credit Spreads Composite (CSC) is currently in a NORMAL regime, reflecting an environment where corporate funding costs are generally benign. As of November 30, 2025, the ICE BofA US High Yield (HY) Option-Adjusted Spread (OAS) is 3.00% and the US Corporate Master (IG) OAS is 0.82%. Both HY and IG spreads are at historically low levels, with HY level z-score at -0.63 and a percentile rank of 9.17%, while IG level z-score is -0.76 with a 5.0% percentile rank.

Despite these low absolute levels, the 3-month annualized z-score for HY spreads is 0.70, indicating recent upward momentum from its extreme lows. The overall CSC z-score stands at -0.11, reinforcing the 'NORMAL' classification as it remains within the [-0.5, +0.5) threshold, suggesting spreads are slightly below their historical median but not in an 'Easy' or 'Tightening' state.

2. Systemic Financial Stress

The Financial Stress Composite indicates a Neutral regime as of November 30, 2025, with a composite value of 0.045. This places overall systemic financial stress within the typical historical range of [−0.75, +0.75].

An underlying component, the St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4), exhibits a robust z-score of 1.104. This indicates a notable stretch towards higher systemic tension within that specific component. However, this is partially offset by High Yield OAS (z-score: -0.427) and the inverted yield curve (z-score: -0.541), which show less extreme conditions in the composite calculation.

3. Credit Conditions Composite

The Credit Conditions Index, which integrates credit spreads and equity volatility, is categorised as Neutral with a value of -0.29 as of November 30, 2025. This composite reading is within the ±0.75 threshold defining a neutral state. This position reflects a moderation from an 'Easing' regime observed earlier in 2025, when the index was below -0.75 in January.

The transition to 'Neutral' signifies a normalisation of funding conditions, moving away from a period particularly supportive for risk assets. While individual components like VIXCLS briefly spiked in April 2025 (z-score: 1.03), and HY_OAS and BBB_OAS experienced extreme negative z-scores earlier in the year, these have converged to typical ranges by the end of November 2025.

4. Private Credit Impulse

The Private Credit Impulse signal indicates a Stable credit growth regime, with a composite reading of 0.44 as of November 30, 2025. This follows a period of 'Decelerating' momentum earlier in 2025, when the impulse fell to -1.04 in January.

The current 'Stable' assessment is primarily driven by the positive momentum in Total Bank Credit (TOTBKCR), which registered a z-score of 0.44. It is important to note that the composite reading for November 2025 is based on incomplete data, as recent figures for Commercial & Industrial Loans (BUSLOANS) and Total Consumer Loans (TOTALSL) were not available. In earlier periods, Total Consumer Loans demonstrated significant deceleration, with z-scores below -2.00, while Commercial & Industrial Loans also experienced deceleration in mid-2025.

5. Synthesised US Credit Conditions Assessment

The current US credit environment is interpreted as generally benign and neutral across most key indicators. While credit spreads remain tight by historical standards, signs of normalisation are evident, with a moderate financial stress outlook and stable, albeit partially observed, private credit growth momentum.

7. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

The current cross-asset signals suggest a period where risk sentiment is neither overtly bullish nor bearish, settling into a more balanced state. The default risk environment appears contained given the 'NORMAL' spread regime and the 'Neutral' financial stress composite. These conditions generally imply a stable, but not necessarily accelerating, broader growth outlook, as financing conditions remain accessible without showing signs of excessive exuberance or significant tightening pressure.

However, the upward momentum in HY spreads from historical lows, alongside an elevated underlying STLFSI4 z-score, indicate potential for further normalisation or, in a less favourable scenario, an unwinding of extremely tight conditions. The incomplete data in the Private Credit Impulse introduces a degree of uncertainty regarding the full picture of credit growth dynamics.
