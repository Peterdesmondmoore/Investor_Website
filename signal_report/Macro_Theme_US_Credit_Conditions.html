Executive Summary

The US credit environment exhibits a mixed profile, leaning towards stable conditions with underlying indicators suggesting emerging stress. Both the Credit Spreads Composite and the Credit Conditions Composite are classified as Neutral to Normal, indicating generally benign corporate funding conditions. However, the standalone Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4) signals a Bearish regime, highlighting elevated systemic stress relative to its historical distribution.

Credit spreads, while currently at historically low levels, show recent positive momentum, suggesting a tendency to widen. Private credit growth remains stable, primarily driven by total bank credit, though complete data for all components is not available for the latest period. The divergence between broad market conditions and specific stress indicators warrants careful monitoring.

1. Credit Spreads

As of 2025-12-31, the Credit Spreads Composite (CSC) is in a "NORMAL" regime, with a z-score of -0.162404. High Yield (HY_OAS) stands at 2.91% and Investment Grade (IG_OAS) at 0.80%. Both remain near historical lows, with percentile ranks of 0.066667 for HY and 0.029167 for IG, reflecting historically tight funding conditions. [Source: credit_spreads_methodology]

Despite these tight levels, both HY and IG spreads exhibit positive 3-month annualised momentum z-scores of 0.588018 and 0.546884, respectively. This indicates a recent tendency for spreads to widen, counteracting the low absolute levels and preventing a classification of "EASY." HY and IG spreads are moving in a similar direction, with both showing signs of this modest widening tendency. [Source: credit_spreads_methodology]

2. Systemic Financial Stress

The Financial Stress Composite is currently in a "Neutral" regime, with a reading of -0.285930 as of 2025-12-31. This neutral classification is a result of negative contributions from both the inverted yield curve (Z_INV: -0.684081) and high-yield spreads (Z_HY: -0.484383), partially offset by a slightly positive contribution from the STLFSI4 component (Z_STL: 0.310674). [Source: Financial Stress Composite Methodology]

In contrast, the independent Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4) signal is classified as "Bearish" as of 2025-12-05, driven by a robust z-score of 0.668148, which exceeds the +0.5 threshold for elevated stress. This indicates that while the composite measure remains neutral due to other factors, the underlying systemic stress as captured by STLFSI4 alone suggests a tightening environment. [Source: Methodology/Financial_Stress.html]

3. Credit Conditions Composite

The Credit Conditions Composite registered -0.337510 as of 2025-12-31, placing it in a "Neutral" regime. This reading is moderately negative, leaning towards easing conditions within the defined neutral band of ±0.75. The composite is derived from robust-normalised High Yield (HY_OAS), BBB Option-Adjusted Spreads (BBB_OAS), and the CBOE Volatility Index (VIXCLS). [Source: credit_conditions]

The latest observed values for HY_OAS (2.91), BBB_OAS (1.01), and VIXCLS (15.74) all contributed to a more easing trajectory in December, with their respective z-scores being negative. This signal generally aligns with the "NORMAL" credit spread regime, indicating a benign funding backdrop where spreads and volatility are near typical ranges. [Source: credit_conditions]

4. Private Credit Impulse

The Private Credit Impulse signal is currently in a "Stable" regime, with a composite value of 0.185323 as of 2025-12-31. This indicates a steady rate of credit creation, falling within the [-0.75, 0.75] threshold for stability. The signal has transitioned from a decelerating regime earlier in the year to a consistently stable range since March 2025. [Source: private_credit_impulse]

For December 2025, the signal was solely driven by the robust-standardised 6-month annualised growth of total bank credit (Z_TOTBKCR_ann6 = 0.185323), due to incomplete data for commercial & industrial loans and consumer loans. In prior months, consumer loans demonstrated negative contributions, suggesting varied dynamics across credit categories. The stable impulse generally supports the overall neutral assessment of credit conditions. [Source: private_credit_impulse]

5. Synthesised US Credit Conditions Assessment

Overall US credit conditions are assessed as predominantly Neutral to Moderately Supportive. This reflects historically tight credit spreads (HY & IG in Normal regime) and a "Neutral" classification for the Credit Conditions Composite and the Financial Stress Composite. However, a significant divergence exists with the "Bearish" signal from the underlying Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4), which indicates a measured increase in systemic stress when viewed relative to its historical robust z-score. Private credit growth, as measured by the Private Credit Impulse, remains "Stable," suggesting ongoing, albeit unaccelerated, credit creation in the private sector.

7. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

The current credit regime implies a generally risk-on sentiment environment, supported by low credit spreads and neutral credit conditions. This backdrop typically supports asset valuation and facilitates corporate refinancing activities. Default risk background appears contained given current spread levels. However, the "Bearish" reading from the Financial Stress Index (STLFSI4) introduces a note of caution, suggesting underlying vulnerabilities or a potential shift towards tighter systemic conditions. [Source: Methodology/Financial_Stress.html]

The observed positive momentum in spreads, despite low levels, also indicates that conditions may be transitioning from extremely easy to a more neutral or slightly tightening phase. Investors should note this nuanced environment where broad credit health signals stability, but specific stress indicators suggest the need for vigilance regarding potential future tightening in financial cycles. [Source: credit_spreads_methodology]
