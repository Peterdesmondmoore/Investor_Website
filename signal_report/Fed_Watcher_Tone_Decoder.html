Fed Tone Decoder – Communications Interpretation Module.

Speaker: Chair Powell | Date: December 10, 2025 | Source: Press Conference

  • Labour Market: ✓ Softening labor market with downside risks. Official employment data for Oct/Nov delayed, but available evidence suggests layoffs and hiring remain low (1). Unemployment rate edged up to 4.4% in September, job gains slowed significantly (1). Estimated actual job creation is negative, around -20,000/month (1). AI is contributing to job cuts but not yet a major driver of overall weakness (1).
  • Inflation Concern: ✗ Inflation remains somewhat elevated. Total PCE and core PCE prices rose 2.8% over 12 months ending in September (1). Tariff-related goods inflation is the primary source of excess inflation, expected to be a one-time price increase peaking in Q1 2026 (1). Non-tariff inflation is "not so far from 2 percent" (1). Longer-term inflation expectations remain consistent with the 2% goal (1).
  • Policy Bias: Policy rate lowered by 1/4 percentage point. This is the third consecutive cut (75 bps total since September), bringing the federal funds rate into a broad range of neutral estimates (1). The Committee initiated purchases of shorter-term Treasury securities to maintain an ample supply of reserves and support policy rate control, separate from monetary policy stance (1). Monetary policy is not on a preset course, decisions are meeting-by-meeting (1).
  • Risk Asymmetry: Balanced approach to dual mandate in a challenging situation where risks to inflation are tilted to the upside and risks to employment to the downside (1). The Committee is well positioned to wait and see how the economy evolves (1). Concerns about the sustainability of a K-shaped economy where higher-income households drive spending while lower-income struggle (1).
  • Internal Divergence: Significant division of views on the policy committee for future rate path, with some members favoring holds and others advocating for further cuts (1). The decision to cut today had "fairly broad support" with 9 out of 12 votes (1).
