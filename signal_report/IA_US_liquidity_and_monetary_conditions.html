US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant driver is the SOFR 5Y futures signal, which shows a strong speculative long bias (1). This implies market expectations for stable or lower future interest rates. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite indicates an expansionary regime, primarily due to reduced Overnight Reverse Repo usage (2).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized the SOFR 5Y signal due to its high aggregation weight, high conviction, and high interpretation confidence (1). This supported a non-restrictive outlook.
  • The US 10-Year Real Yield signal (3) was downweighted due to "Mixed Signals" confidence and an "Internal Conflict Flag" stemming from a data-methodology inconsistency. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (2) was also downweighted for "Mixed Signals" and "Internal Conflict," reflecting conflicting component inputs.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The strong speculative long bias in SOFR 5Y futures (1) aligns with the expansionary reading from the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (2). This suggests a market anticipating non-tightening or easing monetary conditions.
  • Tensions exist with the US 10-Year Real Yield, which indicates restrictive monetary conditions (3). Additionally, the M2 Money Supply, currently bullish, is projected to soften to a neutral regime (4). SOFR 3M shows a balanced short bias (5), implying stable short-term rate expectations.
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call. The high-weight SOFR 5Y signal provides a clear, coherent, cyclical view despite existing conflicts. The real yield's restrictive message is weakened by its internal inconsistencies and mixed confidence.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Liquidity remains broadly supportive, with market participants maintaining a speculative long bias in longer-term rates (1). This is conditioned on sustained reductions in ON RRP usage (2).
  • Primary upside risk: Broader liquidity expansion if M2 growth re-accelerates or real yields clearly shift to an accommodative trend (4)(3).
  • Primary downside risk: Liquidity contraction driven by a sharp reversal in ON RRP usage or persistent declines in reserve balances (2), alongside a confirmed tightening of the 10-year real yield trend (3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained and material increase in Overnight Reverse Repo usage, reversing the current easing liquidity interpretation (2).
  • The 10-year real yield consistently falling below its 6-month trend by more than 0.05 percentage points, signaling accommodative conditions (3).
  • A significant shift in 4-week speculative flow for SOFR 5Y from "Long_Build" to "Long_Reduction" or a strong "Short_Build" (1).
