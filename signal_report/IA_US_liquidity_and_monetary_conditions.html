US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Broad liquidity is accelerating as M2 momentum has reached 1.66% (1).
  • Reserve balances show a sharp 35.3% three-month expansion, lifting the liquidity composite (2).
  • However, the high-weight real interest rate signal remains restrictive (3).
  • Real yields are currently 15.5 basis points above their six-month trend (3).
  • Tactical signals were downweighted due to their shorter time horizons relative to structural rate trends (4)(5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • M2 and Federal Reserve liquidity signals both indicate improving system funding (1)(2).
  • Significant tension exists between expanding money supply and tightening real borrowing costs (3).
  • Institutional positioning maintains a short bias, reinforcing the restrictive policy narrative (4)(5).
  • The high weighting of real rates prevents a shift to a risk-positive label.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: A "liquidity-cushioned" restriction where reserve growth offsets high real rates.
  • Primary Upside Risk: Transition to expansionary liquidity if real yields mean-revert to trend (3).
  • Primary Downside Risk: A sudden spike in RRP usage draining system liquidity reserves (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The 10-year real yield falls within 0.05% of its six-month moving average (3).
  • M2 year-over-year growth accelerates beyond the 6.0% aggressive expansion threshold (1).
  • The Liquidity Composite moves below -0.75, signalling a return to contractionary conditions (2).

Methodology Applied:

  • Falling Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage is interpreted as incremental easing, indicating cash moving into the financial system (1).
  • Rising LC values indicate expansionary liquidity, providing risk-on and inflationary support (1).
  • Latest observation: 2026-04-30.

Key Dynamics:

  • The dominant driver is the sharp inflection in Reserve Balances (35.3% 3M change) and stabilized M2 growth (4.1% YoY) (1).
  • The signal transitioned from negative territory (-0.20 in late 2024) to a positive trajectory in early 2026.
  • Conditional Invalidation: A reversal of the Liquidity Composite below -0.75, indicating a return to contractionary drainage.
  • Recent history shows persistent improvement in the composite score over the last four months.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Sustained neutral-to-easing liquidity as RRP drainage continues to offset Fed asset reductions.
  • Upside risk: A transition into the "Expansion" regime (LC > 0.75) triggered by a halt in QT or accelerated M2 growth.
  • Downside risk: A sudden spike in RRP usage or a sharp drop in bank reserves draining system liquidity.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months), reflecting the lead-lag structure of monetary aggregates and balance sheet changes.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; the signal is nearing a regime boundary but currently remains in a neutral state.

  • This signal informs broad financial system liquidity and the availability of private sector funding (1).
  • Economic mechanism: Expansionary liquidity supports asset prices and eases short-term funding pressures through reserve replenishment.

  • Direction of change: Strengthening toward the Expansionary threshold (+0.75).

Model Limitations:

  • Equal weighting of components may not account for shifting importance between M2 and RRP dynamics.
