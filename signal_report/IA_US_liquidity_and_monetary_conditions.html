US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The primary anchor is the Real Interest Rate Trend (1), which remains highly restrictive.
  • Restrictive pressure is countered by expansionary momentum in the M2 Money Supply (2) and a stabilizing Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (3).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritizes the High-Weight, High-Conviction real-rate signal over Medium-Weight liquidity improvements.
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (3) was downweighted due to its Low Conviction band and proximity to the neutral threshold.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Tactical short-bias positioning in SOFR 3M (4) reinforces the restrictive real-rate signal (1).
  • Tensions exist between accelerating M2 momentum (2) and the restrictive impulse of real borrowing costs (1).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call because current liquidity levels remain below expansionary thresholds (3).
  • Strategic speculators in SOFR 5Y (5) are de-risking, supporting a range-biased baseline.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Range-biased price paths and normal volatility while liquidity remains neutral (1).
  • Upside Risk: M2 YoY growth accelerates above 6.0%, triggering an expansionary monetary regime (2).
  • Downside Risk: Accelerated rise in real yields further increases borrowing costs and pressures valuations (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • Real yields falling within 0.05% of the 6-month trend, shifting the signal from Bearish to Neutral (1).
  • The Liquidity Composite moving beyond 0.75, forcing a shift into an expansionary regime (3).

  • Easing Rule: Falling Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage is treated as an inverted signal, implying incremental liquidity easing. (1)
  • Expansion Rule: LC values ≥ 0.75 indicate an expansionary regime supportive of risk-on assets. (1)
  • Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite Signal: Latest observation 2026-04-30. (1)

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Liquidity remains a non-factor for price direction, forcing markets to rely on non-monetary drivers.
  • Upside risk: Rapid RRP drainage or renewed M2 acceleration pushes LC above 0.75, triggering an expansionary regime.
  • Downside risk: Accelerated Fed balance sheet contraction (QT) or reserve balance drainage pushes LC below -0.75.

Model Limitations:
