US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The regime is characterized by conflicting signals regarding interest rate expectations and a neutral, but internally conflicted, liquidity backdrop. The SOFR-3M signal, a high-weighted tactical indicator, suggests market expectations for lower rates (9). Conversely, the SOFR-5Y signal, a medium-weighted cyclical indicator, points to expectations for sustained or higher rates (12). This fundamental tension is a primary driver.
  • Broader liquidity measures reinforce a balanced, rather than expansive, state. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is Neutral, exhibiting conflicting forces from contracting Federal Reserve assets and easing overnight reverse repo usage (1). The M2 Money Supply signal is transitioning from a transient Bullish state to Neutral (6).
  • Signals were dynamically weighted. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1) and the US 10-year Real Yield signal (3) were downweighted due to mixed interpretation confidence and internal conflict. The M2 Money Supply signal (6), while exhibiting high confidence, was treated as a waning expansive force due to its projected shift to Neutral.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Tensions exist in the market's implied interest rate expectations. Tactical SOFR-3M positioning signals a bias towards lower rates (9), while cyclical SOFR-5Y positioning implies expectations for sustained or higher rates (12).
  • The overall Neutral state of the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1) and the M2 Money Supply's (6) shift to Neutral reinforce a stable, but not expansive, monetary backdrop. This aligns with the neutral real interest rate trend (3).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call. Instead, they define it as transitional and fragile, highlighting uncertainty in the monetary policy outlook. The lack of clear, broad-based expansive or contractive liquidity signals supports this.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Continued neutrality in liquidity and real rates, with unresolved tensions in interest rate expectations (1)(3).
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained re-acceleration of M2 growth beyond current projections (6), alongside a clear market consensus for lower rates across the curve.
  • Primary downside risk: Intensified Federal Reserve balance sheet reduction or a sharp decline in reserve balances (1), coupled with an unwinding of speculative long positions in SOFR markets (9)(12).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A clear and sustained reversal in the Federal Reserve's balance sheet policy or a significant acceleration in quantitative tightening (1).
  • A sustained drop in the 3-month M2 growth rate below 1.0%, or its year-over-year growth falling below 2.0% (6).
  • A deviation of the 10-year real yield by more than ±0.05% from its 6-month trend, shifting to a clear accommodative or restrictive stance (3).
