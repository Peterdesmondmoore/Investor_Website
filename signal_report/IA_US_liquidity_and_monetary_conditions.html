US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance.
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence.
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The headline Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is Neutral at 0.042 (1).
  • M2 money supply growth has shifted to Bullish with strong 1.66% momentum (2).
  • Bearish real interest rate trends create a restrictive cost-of-capital headwind (3).
  • Dynamic weighting balances the expansionary money supply against restrictive real yields.
  • Tactical SOFR positioning is downweighted as it reflects short-term flow rather than structural liquidity (4).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Stabilisation of Fed Assets and steady M2 growth reinforce the Neutral composite anchor (1).
  • Tactical SOFR "Long Build" flow aligns with rising M2 liquidity momentum (4)(2).
  • Tension exists between M2 expansion and the BEARISH real rate trend (3).
  • Tensions do not overturn the regime because the aggregate Liquidity Composite remains far from expansion thresholds (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant: Continued neutral liquidity as ON RRP drainage offsets balance sheet contraction.
  • Primary upside risk: M2 YoY growth accelerates above 6.0%, triggering an expansionary regime (2).
  • Primary downside risk: Systemic liquidity withdrawal via renewed aggressive QT or a TGA spike (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A move in the Composite Score (LC) above 0.75 or below -0.75 (1).
  • M2 Year-over-Year growth dropping below 2.0% (2).
  • 10-year real yields falling below 1.77%, removing the restrictive trend (3).
