US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Low Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • The M2 Money Supply signal, a high-weight cyclical driver, is Neutral, indicating stable but not exceptionally strong monetary expansion (1). This stability prevents a clear Expansionary classification.
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite points to Expansion, but with mixed signals and internal conflict, reducing its directional conviction (2).
  • Tactical SOFR positioning signals indicate balanced short-term expectations (3) and a reduction in speculative long positions for 5-year rates (4). This suggests market participants are not aggressively betting on falling rates, contributing to a fragile balance.
  • Signals were dynamically weighted. M2 Money Supply received high weight due to high confidence and aggregation hint (1). The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite and 5-Year SOFR Positioning received medium weight (2)(4).
  • The Real Interest Rate Trend signal was heavily downweighted due to low conviction, mixed interpretation confidence, and an internal conflict flag (5). The 3-Month SOFR positioning signal also received low weight as it is tactical and currently neutral (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Neutral M2 Money Supply (1) and the tactical SOFR signals (3)(4) reinforce a stable, non-extreme picture.
  • Tensions exist between the Expansionary Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (2) and the Neutral M2 signal (1). The Liquidity Composite itself has internal conflicts (2).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call, but rather define its fragility. The dominant M2 signal's neutrality combined with mixed liquidity and tactical positioning prevent a stronger conviction for either expansion or contraction.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A continued environment of stable liquidity with balanced, though not strongly bullish, interest rate expectations.
  • Primary upside risk: A sustained re-acceleration of M2 growth above 1.0% on a 3-month basis, potentially triggering a more robust liquidity expansion (1).
  • Primary downside risk: A more pronounced contraction in Federal Reserve assets or a reversal in ON RRP trends would swiftly dampen overall liquidity (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained decline in M2 Year-over-Year growth below 2.0% or 3-month momentum below -1.0% would shift the regime towards risk-negative conditions (1).
  • A clear and sustained shift to extreme speculative positioning in SOFR markets (e.g., Spec_zscore exceeding +/- 2.0) would signal a change in rate expectations and liquidity sentiment (3).
  • A significant and confirmed decline in reserve balances or a sustained positive growth in ON RRP would weaken the current expansionary liquidity signal (2).
