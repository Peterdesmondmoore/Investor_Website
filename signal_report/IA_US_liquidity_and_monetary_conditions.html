US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Status: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by a tension between restrictive real financial conditions and improving settlement liquidity.
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is currently Neutral (0.148) but shows improving cyclical momentum (1).
  • This is countered by the Real Interest Rate Trend, which remains in a Bearish, restrictive posture (2).
  • High-weight cyclical signals are in direct conflict, necessitating a conservative transitional classification.
  • Tactical SOFR signals were downweighted as they currently reflect normal, non-extreme positioning (3)(4).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • M2 Money Supply growth (4.88%) aligns with the expansionary impulse from RRP drainage (5)(1).
  • Significant tension exists as the 10-year real yield (2.02%) sits 15 basis points above its medium-term trend (2).
  • This tension prevents a shift to an Expansionary regime, as high real costs offset rising liquidity volumes.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Neutral liquidity expansion continues, supported by cash migration from the RRP facility (1).
  • Primary Upside Risk: M2 YoY growth accelerates above 6.0%, confirming aggressive monetary expansion (5).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Treasury replenishment drains bank reserves faster than RRP can offset, tightening settlement conditions (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • Real yields falling below 1.915% would signal an end to restrictive real-rate pressure (2).
  • The Liquidity Composite falling below -0.75 would indicate a transition to active systemic withdrawal (1).
  • A reversal in SOFR flow momentum toward "Short Build" would indicate a shift in policy expectations (3).
