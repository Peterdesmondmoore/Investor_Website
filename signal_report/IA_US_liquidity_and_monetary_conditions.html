US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Low Confidence
  • The regime is fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • M2 Money Supply (High weight) shows stable but slowing expansion within neutral thresholds (1).
  • The Real Interest Rate Trend (Medium weight) has shifted to a Bearish/restrictive state (2).
  • High interpretation confidence in neutral M2 and balanced SOFR positioning drives the baseline assessment (1)(3).
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite was downweighted due to its unclassified status and internal label conflicts (4).
  • SOFR-5Y was downweighted due to a conflict between net positioning and assigned regime labels (5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Tactical SOFR-3M and cyclical SOFR-5Y align on "Normal" and un-stretched market positioning (3)(5).
  • A primary tension exists between the bullish liquidity tailwind from RRP depletion and ongoing Fed asset contraction (4).
  • The Bearish real rate signal conflicts with the neutral/stable momentum observed in broader M2 supply (1)(2).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as they indicate a lack of directional consensus.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Liquidity stabilization as declining RRP usage offsets balance sheet normalization (4).
  • Primary upside risk: Resurgent M2 growth above 6.0% YoY triggering a broad liquidity expansion (1).
  • Primary downside risk: 10-year real yields rising significantly above the current 1.83% trend (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in M2 Year-over-Year growth below 2.0% would invalidate the neutral regime (1).
  • Real yields falling below 1.78% would signal a shift toward an accommodative/Bullish state (2).
  • A formal Liquidity Composite score exceeding ±0.75 would force a reclassification to Expansion or Contraction (4).
