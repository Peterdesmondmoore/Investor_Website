US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • Broad money supply (M2) acts as the primary structural anchor. It is currently in a stable Neutral state (1).
  • The Liquidity Composite has moved from negative to marginally positive territory, indicating a shift toward stability (2).
  • Real interest rates are downweighted due to "Low" conviction and internal threshold conflicts (3).
  • Tactical SOFR positioning signals are currently un-stretched, confirming a lack of immediate trend conviction (4)(5).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • M2 growth and SOFR positioning reinforce a range-bound, mid-cycle transition narrative (1)(5).
  • Tension exists within the Liquidity Composite. Bullish RRP drains are offset by Neutral reserves and Fed assets (2).
  • Real yields remain marginally restrictive, creating a valuation headwind against improving liquidity aggregates (3).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call as broad money aggregates remain within historical norms (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Neutral stability. Broad money aggregates maintain stable growth between 2% and 6% (1).
  • Primary Upside Risk: Expansion. Triggered if Federal Reserve Assets acceleration exceeds 0.8% on a 3-month basis (2).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Contraction. Triggered by a reversal in RRP trends or Reserve balances falling below 1% YoY (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Liquidity Composite score rising above 0.75 would signal a shift to an Expansionary regime (2).
  • M2 Year-over-Year growth falling below 2.0% would move the regime toward Defensive (1).
  • A 10-year real yield decline below 1.78% would signal a shift to accommodative conditions (3).
