US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • The regime is fragile due to diverging cyclical momentum between monetary aggregates and real cost of capital.

Why This Regime:

  • The assessment is driven by the offset between accelerating broad money supply (1) and persistent real interest rate restrictiveness (2).
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite acts as a primary cyclical anchor, currently holding a stable Neutral state (0.035) (3).
  • Tactical signals, such as the bullish build in 3-month SOFR flows (4), were downweighted as they lack the structural weight of the 10-year real yield trend (2).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Internal alignment exists between Fed Liquidity (3) and 5-year SOFR positioning (5), both indicating a "Normal" environment without extreme speculative crowding.
  • A primary tension exists between the Bullish transition in M2 momentum (1.66%) (1) and the Bearish stance of real yields remaining above their 6-month trend (2).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime because neither side has achieved a high-conviction breakout; the neutral liquidity baseline absorbs these opposing forces (3).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant Scenario: Range-bound liquidity conditions as balance sheet contraction is offset by falling reverse repo usage (3).
  • Primary Upside Risk: Aggressive expansion if M2 Year-over-Year growth exceeds 6.0% (1).
  • Primary Downside Risk: Monetary stagnation if real yields push significantly higher than 1.85%, increasing capital costs (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite breach of ±0.75, signalling systemic expansion or withdrawal (3).
  • A decline in the 10-year real yield below 1.77%, removing the restrictive Bearish bias (2).
  • A reversal in M2 3-month momentum to below -1.0% (1).

Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite Signal

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given the Neutral regime and a Composite Score of 0.035, this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Regime shift.
  • The signal remains in a persistent Neutral regime, having shifted from a negative bias in late 2024 to a stable, near-zero baseline.
  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Far; Revision Sensitivity: Moderate.

Methodology Applied:

  • A Composite Score (LC) between -0.75 and 0.75 defines a Neutral liquidity environment (1).
  • Falling Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage is interpreted as an incremental easing impulse, returning cash to the private banking system (1).
  • Rising Reserve Balances (WRESBAL) are viewed as supportive of lower short-term funding volatility (1).
  • Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite Signal, latest observation: 2026-03-31 (1).

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the stabilization of M2 Money Supply growth (YoY 4.48%) and the cessation of aggressive balance sheet contraction (1).
  • Incremental easing is provided by the massive 96% YoY decline in RRP usage, though this is offset by a 13.24% YoY decline in reserve balances (1).
  • The internal sub-components (M2, Fed Assets, Reserves) have converged toward Neutral status, reducing internal tension (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A Composite Score breach of ±0.75, indicating a shift to broad-based expansion or systemic withdrawal (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Continued range-bound liquidity as Fed balance sheet operations find a terminal floor.
  • Upside risk: Sudden M2 acceleration or total RRP exhaustion leading to a liquidity surge.
  • Downside risk: Renewed Treasury General Account (TGA) volatility or accelerated QT draining bank reserves.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months); the signal tracks the net expansion or contraction of the monetary base over multi-month windows.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; the Neutral regime acts as a "no-harm" filter for risk assets rather than a high-conviction driver.

Macro Relevance:

  • This signal informs the US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions dimension (1).
  • The economic mechanism is the transmission of Fed balance sheet operations into private sector credit availability (1).
  • Cycle position: Not determined.

Regime Context:

  • The current regime is persistent, having remained Neutral for over 24 months (1).
  • Direction of change: Stabilising; the composite has settled near the zero-line after recovering from negative territory in 2023 (1).

Model Limitations:

  • M2 reporting lags and sensitivity to Treasury General Account (TGA) volatility (1).

Data & References:

  • Conviction Band: Medium; Interpretation Confidence: High Confidence; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Near; Revision Sensitivity: Unknown.

Methodology Applied:

  • Real yield > 6-month moving average by > 0.05% implies tightening real-rate pressure at the margin (1).
  • Real yield within ± 0.05% of trend indicates stable conditions and a Neutral regime (1).
  • Observation date: 2026-03-01.

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the persistence of the 10-year TIPS yield at 1.85%, maintaining a restrictive gap above the moving average (1).
