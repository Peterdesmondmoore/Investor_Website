US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Expansionary / Risk-Positive
  • Regime Confidence Index: High Confidence
  • Regime Stability: Stable

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant drivers are expansionary liquidity and money supply conditions. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is in a strong expansionary regime, driven by significant declines in RRP usage (1). The M2 Money Supply signal is Bullish, indicating an expansion in the money supply due to strong short-term momentum (6). Long positioning in 5-year SOFR swaps also shows significant 'Long_Build' momentum, suggesting market anticipation of lower future rates (9).
  • These signals carry high weight due to their strong conviction, high interpretation confidence, and cyclical time horizons. The Real Interest Rate Trend Signal is Neutral, indicating stable monetary conditions rather than restrictive ones (4).
  • The 3-month SOFR futures signal was downweighted. It presents high tension and internal conflict between speculative long-building and hedger deep short positions (7). Its tactical time horizon and mixed interpretation confidence further reduce its influence on the overall regime call.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1), M2 Money Supply (6), and 5-Year SOFR Positioning (9) reinforce each other. They consistently point to increasing liquidity and market expectations for accommodative future monetary conditions, supporting a risk-positive environment for this theme (10).
  • Tension exists in the 3-month SOFR futures market, where speculative long-building confronts hedger deep short positions (7). This creates a 'High_Tension_Low_Flags' synthetic state, indicating divergence.
  • This tension does not overturn the regime call. The SOF 3M signal is tactical and displays internal conflict, limiting its weight. It also indicates "No_Clear_Reversal," suggesting the divergence has not yet reached a critical inflection point (7).

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is sustained expansionary liquidity, supporting financial conditions and risk assets.
  • Primary upside risk is a further acceleration in M2 or RRP drawdown, amplifying liquidity inflows.
  • Primary downside risk is an escalation of short-term rate tensions or a return to restrictive real rates.

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained contractionary shift in the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1).
  • A firm re-establishment of a Bearish/restrictive stance in the Real Interest Rate Trend Signal (4).
  • An unwind of speculative long positioning in 5-year SOFR swaps (9) or the triggering of a "Bearish Reversal" regime in 3-month SOFR futures (7).
