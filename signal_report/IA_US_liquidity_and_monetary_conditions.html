US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance.
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence.
  • Status: Fragile.

Why This Regime:

  • Headline liquidity composites for the Fed and M2 money supply are strictly neutral (1)(2).
  • Weighting prioritises the "Neutral" stance of medium-weight cyclical drivers over tactical positioning shifts.
  • The Fed Liquidity Composite was slightly downweighted due to internal conflict and "Mixed" interpretation confidence (1).
  • SOF 5Y signals were downweighted due to low aggregation weight and fading trend sponsorship (3).

Alignment & Tensions:

  • M2 growth (4.29% YoY) and the broad Fed liquidity composite (0.0179 z-score) reinforce a stable, non-directional liquidity environment (2)(1).
  • Tensions exist between neutral volume signals and restrictive price signals; real yields remain in a Bearish/Restrictive regime (4).
  • SOFR 3M flows indicate hawkish repricing and "Long Reduction," suggesting the market is anticipating tighter conditions than current liquidity data reflects (5).
  • Neutrality persists because the Fed's balance sheet contraction is currently sterilised by RRP depletion (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A "Liquidity Bypass" where settlement reserves remain stable despite ongoing quantitative tightening.
  • Primary upside risk: Acceleration in M2 growth beyond 6% or a shift to Bullish momentum triggers an Expansionary regime (2).
  • Primary downside risk: Exhaustion of the RRP facility leaves the banking system exposed to unsterilised balance sheet contraction (1).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite moves beyond the +/- 0.75 threshold, signaling a breakdown in the current offset mechanism (1).
  • The 10-year real yield falls below 1.77%, terminating the current Bearish trend and easing restrictive pressure (4).
