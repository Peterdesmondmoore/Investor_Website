US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • State: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The primary driver is the neutral liquidity quantity (z-score 0.027), where private sector money growth (M2) offsets central bank balance-sheet contraction (1).
  • M2 growth (4.29%) sits centrally within its historical neutral band, indicating stable capital availability (2).
  • High-confidence signals for money supply (2) and tactical SOFR positioning (3) define the base state. Real interest rate signals (4) were downweighted due to internal conflict and marginal threshold breaches.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • Quantity signals and speculative positioning reinforce a "Normal" state as prior long crowding has fully unwound (2)(3)(5).
  • Tension exists between restrictive real yields (1.85%) and neutral money stock (4).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime because positioning is no longer stretched, reducing the risk of a liquidity-driven squeeze (5).

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: Range-biased price paths as M2 growth remains stable (2).
  • Primary upside risk: Acceleration in M2 growth above 6.0% or a formal end to balance sheet contraction (1).
  • Primary downside risk: A re-acceleration of real yields significantly above their 6-month trend (4).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A decline in the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite below -0.75 (1).
  • M2 3-month momentum exceeding 1.0% or YoY growth falling below 2.0% (2).
  • The 10-year real yield dropping more than 0.05% below its 6-month moving average (4).

  • Internal Conflict: The composite remains near zero because private sector liquidity creation (M2) is largely offsetting public sector liquidity withdrawal (QT) (1).
  • Conditional Invalidation: A drop in the composite below -0.75 would signal a transition to a Contraction regime (1).

  • Most plausible downside risk: A re-acceleration of RRP take-up or deepening reserves (WRESBAL) contraction.

Regime Context:

  • Direction of change is stabilising; the composite has recovered from deep negative values in 2023 toward the zero-line (1).

Model Limitations:
