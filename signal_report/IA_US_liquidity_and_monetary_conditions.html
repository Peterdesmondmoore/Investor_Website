US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Medium Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • This regime is supported by high-weighted tactical signals indicating market tension and a struggle for rate direction. Speculative positioning in SOFR 3M futures is significantly net short, while hedgers are deeply net long (1). This creates high tension and suggests tactical short covering may lead to rates stabilizing or declining (1).
  • The SOFR 5Y signal reinforces a speculative long bias with sustained "Long_Build" momentum (2).
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is in an Expansion regime, indicating overall accommodative conditions (3). However, the M2 Money Supply signal, while currently Bullish, is projected to transition to Neutral (4).
  • Dynamic weighting prioritized the SOFR 3M signal due to its "High" Aggregation Weight Hint, "High" Conviction Band, and "High Confidence" Interpretation Confidence (1).
  • Signals downweighted include the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (3) and the US 10-year Real Yield (5) due to "Mixed Signals" Interpretation Confidence and "Yes" Internal Conflict Flags.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The Expansionary Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (3) and current Bullish M2 signal (4) align with the tactical SOFR signals suggesting stable or lower rates due to speculative short covering (1) and long bias (2), pointing towards generally easing monetary conditions.
  • Tensions exist with the M2 Money Supply's projected shift to Neutral (4). The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite contains internal conflicts with some contractionary components (3). The US 10-year Real Yield remains Neutral (5).
  • These tensions do not overturn the regime call, as the dominant weighted signals still imply stabilization or easing of market-implied rates. However, they highlight the delicate balance and potential for shifts.

Scenario Balance:

  • The dominant scenario is a continued fragile balance. Market-implied rates are expected to stabilize or see modest declines due to ongoing short covering pressures (1). Liquidity remains expansionary but with moderating M2 momentum (3)(4).
  • The primary upside risk is accelerated speculative short covering in SOFR, or a re-acceleration of M2 growth, driving rates lower and overall liquidity higher (1)(4).
  • The primary downside risk is renewed speculative short accumulation in SOFR futures or a significant tightening in Federal Reserve liquidity components, leading to higher rates and a reversal of liquidity expansion (1)(3).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite falling below 0.75 would shift it into a Neutral or Contraction regime (3).
  • A sustained drop in the 3-month M2 growth rate below 1.0% would invalidate the current Bullish signal (4).
  • A clear shift in SOFR 3M speculative flow back to strong short accumulation would reverse the current tactical rate bias (1).
