US Liquidity and Monetary Conditions
Synthesises Federal Reserve liquidity measures, money supply, real-rate signals and SOFR positioning to assess the current US monetary and liquidity regime.

Regime Assessment:

  • Regime: Transitional / Fragile Balance
  • Regime Confidence Index: Low Confidence
  • Stability: Fragile

Why This Regime:

  • The dominant drivers for US liquidity and monetary conditions exhibit significant internal tension (3). This supports a transitional phase rather than a clear directional regime.
  • The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1) indicates an "Expansion" regime. However, its Medium Aggregation Weight Hint, Mixed Signals confidence, and Internal Conflict Flag led to it being downweighted.
  • The M2 Money Supply signal (2), a high-confidence cyclical driver, is currently "Bullish" but explicitly projected to shift to "Neutral." This suggests moderating liquidity momentum.
  • Market positioning in SOFR 3M (4) and SOFR 5Y (5) futures is largely balanced. This indicates no strong market conviction for aggressive monetary policy shifts.
  • The Real Interest Rate Trend signal (6) was heavily downweighted due to its Low Aggregation Weight Hint, Mixed Signals confidence, and an Internal Conflict Flag regarding its 'Bearish' classification.

Alignment & Tensions:

  • The generally balanced SOFR futures positioning (4)(5) indicates market neutrality regarding immediate interest rate trajectory.
  • Significant tensions exist within the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite (1). While the composite signals "Expansion," Federal Reserve assets are contracting. M2 Money Supply (2) is also transitioning to "Neutral."
  • These tensions do not overturn the "Transitional / Fragile Balance" regime call. Instead, they reinforce the low confidence and fragility of the current state.

Scenario Balance:

  • Dominant scenario: A continued fragile balance in liquidity conditions. Monetary dynamics are likely to exhibit moderate fluctuations rather than strong directional shifts.
  • Primary upside risk and trigger: M2 Money Stock re-accelerates above its "Bullish" thresholds (2). Federal Reserve assets align with the overall composite's expansionary thrust (1).
  • Primary downside risk and trigger: Federal Reserve asset contraction deepens, or reserve balances decline significantly (1). M2 Money Supply transitions to a "Bearish" contraction regime (2).

What Would Change the Regime:

  • A sustained return to "Bearish" or "Contraction" in Reserve balances (1).
  • M2 Money Supply year-over-year growth falling below 2.0%, or its 3-month momentum dropping below -1.0% (2).
  • A significant rise in SOFR futures reversal scores or extreme positioning across the curve, indicating a market re-pricing of future monetary policy (5).
