Methodology Applied:

  • Divergence ($D_t$) is calculated by subtracting CPI YoY from PPI YoY to identify pipeline inflation pressure (1).

  • Regimes are defined by thresholds: HOT ($z_t \ge 1.0$), COOL ($z_t \le -1.0$), or NEUTRAL ($|z_t| < 0.35$ or intermediate values) (1).
  • CPI and PPI datasets used (latest observation: 2026-01-31) (1).

  • The z-score has stabilised within the $z_{band}$ (0.35), signifying no significant lead/lag pressure from upstream costs (1).

Scenario Balance:

  • Base case dominant: Continued balanced pass-through between production and consumption.
  • Upside risk: Rapid increase in commodity inputs (PPI) leading to positive divergence and pipeline pressure.
  • Downside risk: Sudden contraction in producer prices relative to sticky consumer prices, indicating margin compression.
