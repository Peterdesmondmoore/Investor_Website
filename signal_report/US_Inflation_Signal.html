Gemini Summary

The market is currently defined by significant disinflationary pressure, as indicated by the Fed Inflation Signal registering a "COOL" regime at -0.014734 (20.21 on a 0-100 scale) as of 2025-11-30. This marks a sustained decline from "NEUTRAL" in prior months and a dramatic shift from "HOT" levels observed in early 2022 (e.g., 4.638 on 2022-01-31). This environment could broadly support equities, especially growth stocks, due to potentially lower discount rates, though explicit "disinflation/deflation risk" suggests caution regarding demand. Bonds are generally favored, particularly longer-duration instruments, as falling inflation expectations typically lead to lower yields. Precious metals like gold may find support from potential central bank easing or safe-haven demand. Industrial metals, conversely, face headwinds from implied weakening economic activity.

Technical Summary: Technically, the Fed Inflation Signal demonstrates a clear and sustained downtrend since early 2022, culminating in the current "COOL" regime. This persistent decline signifies strong underlying disinflationary forces. The recent transition from "NEUTRAL" to "COOL" (October-November 2025) reinforces a shift towards lower inflation expectations, potentially signaling a more accommodative monetary policy outlook.
