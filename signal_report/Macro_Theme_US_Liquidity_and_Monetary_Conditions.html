Macro Theme : US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions

US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-11-30

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

Current US monetary conditions present a mixed but net accommodative picture, primarily driven by Federal Reserve liquidity dynamics. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is in an Expansion regime as of November 30, 2025, largely influenced by significant unwinding of the Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) facility. This expansion in broader liquidity occurs despite ongoing contraction in the Fed's balance sheet and reserve balances.

The M2 Money Supply is classified as Neutral as of November 30, 2025, indicating moderated growth impulse. Concurrently, the 10-year real interest rate maintains a Neutral stance as of November 01, 2025, with yields positioned near their medium-term trend.

  • Fed Liquidity Condition: Expansion
  • M2 Regime: Neutral
  • Real-Rate Regime: Neutral
  • Overall Monetary Tone: Accommodative due to strong liquidity impulse from ON RRP, partially offset by balance sheet runoff and neutral money supply and real rate signals.

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite indicates an Expansion regime, posting a value of 1.30 as of November 30, 2025. This marks the fifth consecutive month of expansion, initiated in July 2025, reflecting a persistent easing of broader liquidity conditions.

Individual components show divergent trends. ON RRP usage, when inverted to reflect easing, exhibits a strong bullish impulse with a year-over-year decline of -96.17% and a three-month change of -90.29%. Conversely, Fed Total Assets and Reserve Balances remain in a contractionary posture, registering year-over-year declines of -5.11% and -10.26%, respectively, and three-month changes of -0.77% and -10.18% respectively, categorised as Bearish signals.

2. Money Supply (M2)

The M2 Money Stock was classified as Bullish as of October 01, 2025, with a year-over-year growth of 4.65% and a three-month momentum of 1.22%. This signal indicated a positive growth impulse in money supply.

However, the M2 signal within the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is noted as Neutral for November 30, 2025. This indicates a moderation in the M2 growth impulse, suggesting that while prior momentum was strong, the latest assessment points to more balanced conditions.

3. Real Interest Rate Trend

The US 10-year real yield (DFII10) is currently in a Neutral regime, recorded at 1.77% as of November 01, 2025. The 6-month moving average trend for the real yield is 1.885%.

This classification implies that the real yield is positioned within a narrow band (±0.05%) of its medium-term trend, reflecting stable real interest rate conditions. This follows a period earlier in 2025 where real yields were predominantly in a bearish regime, indicating a shift towards a more balanced monetary stance from a real rate perspective.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

The overall monetary regime is characterised by a strong liquidity injection from the ON RRP facility, driving the composite into an Expansion phase, which counteracts the persistent tightening observed in the Fed's balance sheet and reserve balances. This creates a net accommodative liquidity environment despite the M2 money supply and real interest rates currently signalling a Neutral stance.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse from continued ON RRP unwinding.
  • Stabilising or falling real yields indicating reduced cost of capital.
  • Continuation or acceleration in M2 growth, supporting broader economic activity.

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields, signalling tighter financial conditions.
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics (e.g., increased RRP usage), reducing systemic liquidity.
  • Deceleration or contraction in money growth, potentially impacting credit availability.

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

Current funding conditions are influenced by the significant liquidity provided through the reduction in ON RRP usage, which partially offsets the structural contraction from the Federal Reserve's balance sheet runoff. This dynamic may support market functioning and provide a tailwind for valuation sensitivity across various asset classes.

Monetary transmission to risk assets under these conditions is complex; while ample system liquidity from the ON RRP mechanism is generally supportive, the underlying tightening in central bank assets and neutral real rate signals suggest a nuanced environment. Asset performance may be sensitive to the interplay between strong short-term liquidity injections and more persistent balance sheet normalisation efforts.
