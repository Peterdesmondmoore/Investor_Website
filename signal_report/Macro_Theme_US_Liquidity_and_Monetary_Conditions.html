US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-12-11

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply, SOFR positioning and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

The US monetary environment currently presents a mixed but predominantly easing liquidity picture. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite is in an Expansion regime, driven significantly by the unwinding of the Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) facility. Concurrently, the M2 Money Supply signal remains Bullish, indicating accelerating money impulse. However, the 10-year real interest rate trend signals a Neutral stance, reflecting a stabilization from previously restrictive conditions rather than an accommodative shift. SOFR futures positioning for both 3-month and 5-year tenors is balanced, indicating no strong consensus on the future path of policy rates among market participants. This suggests a period where liquidity is becoming more supportive, while real rates have settled to a neutral level, and policy expectations lack extreme bias.

  • Fed liquidity condition: Expansion
  • M2 regime: Bullish
  • Real-rate regime: Neutral
  • SOFR positioning tone: Balanced
  • Overall monetary tone: Predominantly easing with neutral real rates

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite has been classified in an Expansion regime since July 2025, reaching a value of 1.37 as of December 31, 2025. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Liquidity_Composite.html] This expansion is primarily driven by the significant unwinding of the ON RRP facility, which exhibited an extreme easing signal with a Year-over-Year change of -99.32% as of the same date. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Liquidity_Composite.html] The methodology interprets declines in ON RRP as an easing influence. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Liquidity_Composite.html] In contrast, components related to Fed Assets and Reserve Balances continue to indicate bearish (tightening) conditions with negative Year-over-Year changes. [Source: Federal_Reserve_Liquidity_Composite.html] Despite these countervailing forces from the balance sheet and reserves, the substantial liquidity injection from the ON RRP unwind dominates the composite, yielding a net injecting liquidity signal.

2. Money Supply (M2)

The M2 Money Supply signal is currently Bullish, with a Year-over-Year growth rate of 4.65% and a 3-month momentum of 1.22% as of October 1, 2025. [Source: m_2_money_supply.html] This Bullish regime has been consistent since April 2025, primarily influenced by the 3-month momentum exceeding the 1.0% acceleration threshold. [Source: m_2_money_supply.html] The Year-over-Year M2 growth remains within the defined high and low growth thresholds of 6.0% and 2.0% respectively. [Source: m_2_money_supply.html] These M2 dynamics, indicating an accelerating money impulse, reinforce the supportive liquidity stance suggested by the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite's Expansion regime.

3. Real Interest Rates & Policy Expectations

The US 10-year real yield (DFII10) is currently in a Neutral regime at 1.85% as of December 1, 2025, closely aligning with its 6-month moving average trend of 1.86%. [Source: Real_Interest_Rate_Trend_Signal.html] This classification signifies a stabilization of real rates, following a prolonged Bearish (restrictive) regime observed from January to March 2025, where the real yield consistently exceeded its trend by more than 0.05%. [Source: Real_Interest_Rate_Trend_Signal.html] The implied stance from the real-rate signal is one of monetary conditions shifting from restrictive towards a more balanced state.

In the SOFR-3M market, positioning is currently balanced as of November 4, 2025, with speculators net short by -4.80% of Open Interest and hedgers net long by 4.80%. [Source: SOF_3M_CoT_Signals.html] Neither speculative nor hedger positioning shows extreme 1-year z-scores, and the hedger pressure regime is neutral, indicating no significant tactical policy expectations. [Source: SOF_3M_CoT_Signals.html] For the SOFR-5Y market, speculative net positioning is 8.6% long, and hedgers are -8.5% net short as of November 4, 2025. [Source: SOF_5Y_CoT_Signals.html] Current speculative and hedger z-scores are near neutral at 0.3270 and -0.3252, respectively, indicating balanced positioning. [Source: SOF_5Y_CoT_Signals.html] While hedgers exhibited a "Deep Short" regime in early October 2025, [Source: SOF_5Y_CoT_Signals.html] the present state suggests no pronounced medium-term policy expectations. Both front-end and medium-term SOFR positioning are broadly consistent with the neutral real-rate regime, lacking strong directional conviction on future policy shifts.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

The overall monetary regime is characterized by supportive liquidity conditions concurrent with a neutral real interest rate stance and balanced policy expectations. The Federal Reserve's liquidity operations, largely influenced by the ON RRP unwind, are net injecting, alongside a bullish impulse from M2 money supply. This indicates an easing in broader monetary aggregates and system liquidity. However, real interest rates have stabilized to a neutral position, suggesting that while funding conditions are easing, the real cost of capital is not yet broadly accommodative. SOFR positioning data reflects an absence of extreme biases among speculators and hedgers, aligning with the neutral real-rate environment and indicating a stable, albeit not aggressively easing, outlook for future policy.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse (e.g., slower QT, stabilising reserves)
  • Stabilising or falling real yields from restrictive levels
  • Continuation or re-acceleration in M2 growth
  • SOFR positioning shifting towards a more accommodative policy path

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields above trend
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics or renewed liquidity drain
  • Deceleration or renewed contraction in money growth
  • SOFR positioning moving towards more persistent tightness or reduced cut expectations

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

The current monetary regime, marked by an Expansion in Fed liquidity and Bullish M2, typically suggests improving funding conditions, a reduction in liquidity premia, and enhanced market access. Such an environment generally supports credit expansion and can be constructive for risk assets. With real rates having transitioned to a neutral stance, the valuation sensitivity across rates, credit, and growth assets may find a more balanced footing, potentially reducing the drag from restrictive real rates previously observed. The balanced SOFR positioning implies that market expectations for policy rates are not a significant source of immediate directional pressure, contributing to overall market stability rather than prompting sharp repricing. This composite stance indicates a backdrop that could support a broad array of asset classes, particularly those sensitive to monetary conditions and liquidity availability, without explicitly signalling an aggressive easing cycle.
