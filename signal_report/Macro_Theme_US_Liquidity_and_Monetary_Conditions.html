Macro Theme : US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions

US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-11-30

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

The US monetary landscape currently presents a mixed but predominantly accommodative stance. The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite has been in an "Expansion" regime since July 2025, reaching 1.30 as of November 2025, driven significantly by declining ON RRP usage. Concurrently, the M2 money supply signal indicates "Bullish" conditions for October 2025, reflecting positive year-over-year growth and 3-month momentum. The 10-year real interest rate, while officially classified as "Neutral" in November 2025, shows its level below the prevailing trend, suggesting a underlying tilt towards accommodative real funding costs.

The latest data points to a sustained easing of broader liquidity conditions, despite ongoing contraction in the Federal Reserve's balance sheet and reserve balances. This divergence highlights the multifaceted nature of monetary transmission channels.

  • Fed Liquidity Condition: Expansion (as of 2025-11-30)
  • M2 Regime: Bullish (as of 2025-10-01, latest available standalone data)
  • Real-Rate Regime: Neutral (as of 2025-11-01)
  • Overall Monetary Tone: Accommodative with some underlying tightening pressures.

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

As of November 30, 2025, the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite registered 1.30, maintaining an "Expansion" regime since July 2025. This composite reading is influenced by varied signals from its underlying components.

  • Federal Reserve Total Assets (WALCL) and Reserve Balances (WRESBAL) continued to signal "Bearish" conditions, with year-over-year contractions of -5.11% and -10.26% respectively, for November 2025.
  • Conversely, Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage showed a strong "Bullish" signal due to a substantial year-over-year decline of -96.17%. The methodology interprets declines in ON RRP as an easing impulse.
  • M2 money supply inputs for the composite were not available for November 2025, resulting in a "Neutral" classification for M2 within the composite for this period.

2. Money Supply (M2)

The M2 Money Supply signal, based on standalone data, registered "Bullish" as of October 1, 2025. This classification is driven by continued positive momentum and growth. M2 data from the composite table for November 2025 was not explicitly available, therefore the M2-specific report data is utilized.

  • Year-over-year growth in M2 was 4.65% in October 2025, above the 2.0% low growth threshold.
  • The 3-month momentum for M2 was 1.22%, exceeding the 1.0% acceleration threshold.
  • The "Bullish" regime has been sustained since November 2024, indicating a period of expanding money supply following earlier contractionary phases.

3. Real Interest Rate Trend

The US 10-Year Real Yield (DFII10) signal was "Neutral" as of November 1, 2025. This assessment is based on the relationship between the real yield and its 6-month moving average trend.

  • The DFII10 stood at 1.77%, while its 6-month trend was 1.885%.
  • The difference between the real yield and its trend was -0.115%. According to the methodology, a difference below -0.05% typically indicates a "Bullish" (accommodative) signal; however, the provided signal for this date is explicitly "Neutral".
  • The real rate environment has largely been in a "Neutral" regime since July 2025, following a "Bearish" period earlier in the year, which suggested tightening conditions.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

Synthesising these components reveals a monetary regime characterized by an easing liquidity backdrop, supported by money supply expansion, yet with a real interest rate environment that remains centrally positioned or leaning towards accommodative, despite some underlying tightening forces from the Fed's balance sheet reduction.

Pillar Current Regime Interpretation
Fed Liquidity Expansion Broad liquidity conditions are expanding, primarily driven by the reduction in ON RRP usage.
M2 Money Supply Bullish Money supply growth and momentum indicate supportive broader liquidity for economic activity.
Real Rates Neutral Real funding costs are stable relative to their medium-term trend, though below trend suggests an accommodative tilt.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse from continued ON RRP run-off.
  • Stabilising or falling real yields, enhancing financial conditions.
  • Continuation in M2 growth, supporting broader economic liquidity.

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields above their trend, indicating tighter financial conditions.
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics, such as an increase in usage, implying liquidity absorption.
  • Deceleration in money growth (M2), signaling a contraction in systemic liquidity.

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

The current monetary configuration suggests a backdrop where funding conditions are generally easing, as evidenced by the overall liquidity composite and M2 trends. This environment may influence asset valuations and the transmission of monetary policy to risk assets. While some traditional tightening channels (balance sheet reduction) persist, the significant release of liquidity via ON RRP and positive M2 dynamics point to a supportive, rather than restrictive, monetary stance at present. The neutral real rate environment implies that inflation-adjusted borrowing costs are not a significant headwind, although vigilance for shifts in real yield trends remains pertinent for assessing future valuation sensitivity.
