Macro Theme : US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions

US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-12-06

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply, and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

The overall liquidity regime is currently categorised as Expansionary, primarily driven by a significant unwind in the Overnight Reverse Repurchase Agreement (ON RRP) facility. This expansive liquidity impulse is reinforced by a Bullish M2 money supply signal, indicating accelerating money growth momentum, albeit with M2 data reported with a lag compared to the liquidity composite.

Concurrently, real interest rates have transitioned to a Neutral regime after an extended period of restrictiveness, suggesting a stabilisation of underlying monetary conditions. This presents a nuanced monetary backdrop where systemic liquidity is broadly supportive, but real rates are no longer actively tightening.

  • Fed liquidity condition: Expansionary
  • M2 regime: Bullish (Re-expansion)
  • Real-rate regime: Neutral
  • SOFR positioning tone: N/A (data not provided)
  • Overall monetary tone: Net supportive with neutral real rates

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite currently registers an Expansion regime, with a composite score of 1.41 as of December 31, 2025. This classification follows a return to Expansion in November 2025 from a Neutral phase in October.

A primary driver of this expansionary signal is the substantial reduction in ON RRP usage, which showed a 99.68% year-over-year decline in December 2025, interpreted as an extreme easing impulse within the composite framework. In contrast, Federal Reserve assets and reserve balances continue to signal contractionary conditions within their respective components of the composite.

2. Money Supply (M2)

The M2 money supply signal is categorised as Bullish, based on data up to October 1, 2025. This reflects a year-over-year growth of 4.65% and a 3-month momentum of 1.22%, which exceeds the acceleration threshold of 1.0%.

The M2 dynamics, characterised by accelerating money growth momentum, reinforce the supportive liquidity stance indicated by the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite. This suggests a broadening of liquidity conditions, despite the data reporting lag for M2.

3. Real Interest Rates & Policy Expectations

The US 10-Year Real Yield (DFII10) stands at 1.85% as of December 1, 2025, with its 6-month moving average trend at 1.86%. This places the real yield within a Neutral regime, as it is observed within ±0.05% of its medium-term trend level.

This Neutral classification follows an extended period of Bearish signals from July 2022 through early 2025, during which real yields consistently remained above their trend, indicating restrictive monetary conditions. The current stabilisation to a neutral stance implies a reduction in this active tightening pressure from real rates.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

The current monetary regime is characterised by a transition towards net supportive conditions, driven by an expansionary liquidity impulse and re-accelerating money supply growth, alongside a stabilisation of real interest rates into a neutral posture after a period of restrictiveness.

Pillar Current Regime Interpretation
Fed Liquidity Expansion Broad liquidity injection, primarily from ON RRP unwind.

SOFR Policy Expectations N/A Data not provided.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse from continued ON RRP unwind or slower Quantitative Tightening.
  • Further stabilisation or a decline in real yields from current neutral levels.
  • Continuation or re-acceleration in M2 growth momentum.

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields, pushing them above their trend.
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics or a renewed net liquidity drain from the Federal Reserve balance sheet.
  • Deceleration or renewed contraction in M2 money growth.

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

A monetary regime characterised by expansionary liquidity and re-accelerating money supply, coupled with neutral real interest rates, generally implies more favourable funding conditions and a potential for reduced liquidity premia across markets. This environment may support broader market access and risk-taking sentiment.

Valuation sensitivities in rates, credit, and risk assets typically respond positively to such conditions. The combination of a supportive liquidity backdrop and real rates no longer actively restricting the economy can reduce the discount rate for future cash flows, potentially favouring growth-oriented assets and enhancing credit spreads. However, the divergence within the Fed Liquidity Composite (ON RRP vs. assets/reserves) suggests complexities that market participants may monitor for regime stability.
