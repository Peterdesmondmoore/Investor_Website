Macro Theme : US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions

US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-12-10

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply, SOFR positioning and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

The overall liquidity environment, as indicated by the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite, is in an Expansionary regime, primarily driven by the substantial unwind of the Overnight Reverse Repurchase Agreement (ON RRP) facility. Concurrently, M2 money supply growth signals a bullish trend, indicating broader money expansion. In contrast, real interest rates are currently in a neutral stance, suggesting neither overtly restrictive nor accommodative monetary conditions. SOFR futures positioning indicates a balanced but moderately stretched view, with hedgers maintaining a net short bias in both the front-end and medium-term contracts.

  • Fed liquidity condition: Expansionary
  • M2 regime: Bullish
  • Real-rate regime: Neutral
  • SOFR positioning tone: Balanced with hedger short bias
  • Overall monetary tone: Net Supportive (due to liquidity expansion and M2 growth, with tempered policy expectations)

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite currently registers an Expansionary regime, with a value of 1.37 as of 2025-12-31. This expansion is predominantly influenced by the significant decline in Overnight Reverse Repurchase Agreement (ON RRP) usage, which exhibits an extreme easing signal. The inverted ON RRP year-over-year change is +99.32%, classified as Bullish. Conversely, Federal Reserve Total Assets and Reserve Balances continue to contract, both registering Bearish signals, indicating ongoing quantitative tightening via these balance sheet channels. The substantial liquidity injection from the ON RRP unwind currently outweighs the draining effects from other components, driving the composite into an expansionary state.

2. Money Supply (M2)

The M2 money supply, as of 2025-10-01, is classified under a Bullish regime. This is supported by a year-over-year growth rate of 4.646% and strong 3-month momentum at 1.222%. Both metrics exceed their respective acceleration thresholds, indicating a re-acceleration in broad money growth. This dynamic suggests a supportive liquidity impulse within the broader monetary system. It is noted that the M2 data is lagged compared to the latest Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite data.

3. Real Interest Rates & Policy Expectations

The US 10-Year Real Yield (DFII10) is currently in a Neutral regime as of 2025-12-01. The real yield stands at 1.85%, closely aligning with its 6-month trend of 1.86%. This indicates that real rates are neither significantly above nor below their medium-term trend, reflecting a balanced stance of monetary conditions from a real yield perspective, without explicit restrictive or accommodative signals.

SOFR 3-month futures positioning, as of 2025-10-28, shows speculators are net short by -2.60% of open interest, while hedgers are net long by 2.60%. The latest 4-week flow indicates a minor long build of 0.20%, and hedgers are categorized in a "Hedgers_Deep_Short" pressure regime, implying underlying expectations for stable to higher near-term rates or hedging activity against such a scenario. SOFR 5-year futures positioning, also as of 2025-10-28, reveals speculators are net long by 17.60% of open interest and hedgers are net short by 17.60%. Both groups show moderately stretched positioning, with speculative z-score at 0.9598 and hedger z-score at -0.9653. A 2.40% long build is observed in 4-week flow, and hedger pressure is currently classified as "Hedgers_Neutral," though it was "Hedgers_Deep_Short" in prior weeks. Overall, SOFR positioning suggests a bias towards higher rates or hedging against rate cuts, presenting a tension with the broader liquidity expansion observed from the Federal Reserve Composite.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

The current monetary regime is characterized as Liquidity Expansion with Tempered Policy Expectations. System-wide liquidity is expanding due to the significant unwind of the ON RRP, which is further corroborated by a bullish impulse from M2 money supply growth. This broad liquidity injection provides a supportive backdrop. However, the neutral stance of real interest rates, coupled with a persistent net short positioning by hedgers in both front-end and medium-term SOFR futures, indicates a market segment anticipating stable to potentially higher policy rates or actively hedging against rate cuts. While the liquidity impulse is strong, expectations regarding policy rates remain balanced to cautious, suggesting that this expansion may not necessarily translate into an immediate acceleration of risk-taking if real rates and policy expectations remain firm.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse (e.g., slower QT, stabilising reserves)
  • Stabilising or falling real yields from restrictive levels
  • Continuation or re-acceleration in M2 growth
  • SOFR positioning shifting towards a more accommodative policy path

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields above trend
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics or renewed liquidity drain
  • Deceleration or renewed contraction in money growth
  • SOFR positioning moving towards more persistent tightness or reduced cut expectations

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

A monetary regime characterized by expanding liquidity and re-accelerating money supply typically correlates with improved funding conditions and reduced liquidity premia across financial markets. Such an environment generally supports market access and can be conducive to risk asset performance, as systemic liquidity acts as a tailwind. However, the concurrent neutral real-rate regime implies that the cost of capital in real terms is not overtly accommodative, and the persistent hedger short positioning in SOFR futures indicates an underlying market vigilance towards central bank policy, suggesting potential for interest rate sensitivity.

In this context, while broad liquidity may be supportive, valuation sensitivity in rates, credit, and equity markets may reflect a degree of caution. This is due to the absence of explicitly accommodative real rates and ongoing hedging activity against lower rates, which could constrain risk appetite or introduce volatility despite ample systemic liquidity.
