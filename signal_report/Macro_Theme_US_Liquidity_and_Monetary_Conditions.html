Macro Theme : US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions

US Liquidity & Monetary Conditions: synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

As of 2025-11-30

Gemini Summary

Synthesising Fed liquidity, money supply and real-rate signals.

Executive Summary

The overall US monetary conditions are characterised by an expansionary Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite, influenced significantly by declining ON RRP usage. This expansion occurs despite a continuous contractionary signal from the Federal Reserve's balance sheet assets and reserve balances. The M2 money supply, as of its latest available data, indicates bullish growth, while the 10-year real interest rate trend remains neutral.

This synthesis points to a nuanced environment where specific liquidity channels are easing, leading to an overall positive liquidity impulse, but underlying Fed asset contraction persists. Real rates, a key measure of monetary stance, are not actively driving either restrictive or accommodative conditions at present.

  • Fed Liquidity Condition: Expansion
  • M2 Regime: Bullish (as of 2025-10-01); Neutral (as of 2025-11-30 in composite due to data status)
  • Real-Rate Regime: Neutral
  • Overall Monetary Tone: Moderately Accommodative / Net Easing

1. Federal Reserve Liquidity Dynamics

The Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite currently registers at 1.30 as of 2025-11-30, categorising the system into an "Expansion" regime. This regime has been sustained since July 2025, indicating a period of increasing systemic liquidity from this composite perspective.

Individual components present a divergent picture: Federal Reserve Total Assets (WALCL) and Reserve Balances with Federal Reserve Banks (WRESBAL) consistently indicate "Bearish" conditions, with YoY declines of -5.108% and -10.258% respectively as of 2025-11-30. Conversely, Overnight Reverse Repo (ON RRP) usage shows substantial "Bullish" easing, with inverted YoY and 3-month changes of -96.174% and -90.294% respectively, reflecting significant withdrawals of liquidity from the facility and contributing strongly to the overall composite expansion.

2. Money Supply (M2)

As of 2025-10-01, the M2 money supply's year-over-year growth is 4.646%, with a 3-month momentum of 1.222%. These metrics classify the M2 money supply as "Bullish" under its dedicated signal methodology, driven by positive growth rates exceeding the acceleration thresholds. This indicates ongoing expansion in broad money.

It is observed that the M2 signal within the Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite for 2025-11-30 is reported as "Neutral," which is attributable to the absence of updated M2 underlying data for November in that specific composite calculation.

3. Real Interest Rate Trend

The US 10-Year Real Yield (DFII10) is 1.77% as of 2025-11-01. When compared to its 6-month moving average trend of 1.885%, the real yield is trading below the trend. However, the current signal for the 10-year real yield is classified as "Neutral."

This classification suggests that while real yields have softened relative to their recent trend, they have not crossed the threshold required to signal a definitively "Bullish" (accommodative) or "Bearish" (restrictive) real interest rate environment according to the specified methodology.

4. Synthesised Monetary Regime

The current monetary regime is characterised by a composite Fed liquidity signal indicating expansion, predominantly driven by the significant drawdown in the Overnight Reverse Repo facility. This easing occurs concurrently with ongoing contractionary dynamics within the Federal Reserve's balance sheet assets and reserve balances, presenting a mixed picture at the Fed-specific level. Broad money supply (M2) exhibits positive momentum (as of October data), while real interest rates maintain a neutral stance.

5. Forward Risks

Easing / Supportive Risks

  • Persistently positive liquidity impulse from continued ON RRP drain.
  • Stabilising or falling real yields, potentially shifting the real rate regime to 'Bullish'.
  • Continuation in M2 growth momentum, indicating broader monetary expansion.

Tightening / Reversal Risks

  • Renewed increase in real yields, pushing the real rate regime towards 'Bearish'.
  • Reversal in ON RRP dynamics, leading to an increase in facility usage and tightening liquidity.
  • Deceleration or contraction in money growth, signaling a reduction in broad liquidity.

6. Investment Interpretation (Informational)

Current funding conditions are influenced by a net supportive liquidity environment, indicated by the expansionary Federal Reserve Liquidity Composite. This suggests generally ample reserves and accessible short-term funding, despite the ongoing reduction in the Fed's balance sheet. Asset valuations may exhibit sensitivity to shifts in these liquidity dynamics, particularly if the ON RRP drain slows or reverses, or if M2 growth significantly changes course.

The neutral stance of real interest rates implies that while inflation-adjusted borrowing costs are not a primary driver of either significant expansion or contraction for risk assets, any pronounced move into a definitively 'Bullish' (lower real yield) or 'Bearish' (higher real yield) regime would likely alter monetary transmission channels to various asset classes.
