Executive Summary

The current US macro landscape is characterized by a late-cycle moderation, with disinflationary pressures taking hold amidst accommodating liquidity conditions. While core manufacturing shows resilience, the labour market exhibits underlying softening and elevated slack, and financial conditions remain generally neutral, though parts of the yield curve suggest ongoing policy restrictiveness. Cross-asset positioning points to normalised volatility but with concentrated exposures and a notable 'Supply Stress' theme.

  • Growth Regime: Late-cycle moderation with mixed real activity signals.
  • Labour Regime: Neutrality with underlying softening and elevated slack.
  • Credit Conditions: Neutral to Benign, normalizing from easing conditions.
  • Liquidity Stance: Expansionary, driven by ON RRP unwinding.
  • Inflation / FX Regime: COOL regime with ongoing disinflationary trend.
  • Positioning & Sentiment: Normal volatility, concentrated crowding, Supply Stress theme active.

Consolidated Thematic View

Growth & Business Cycle: The growth environment exhibits late-cycle moderation with mixed real activity signals. Core manufacturing shows bullish momentum, but business investment is normalizing, and industrial production remains neutral. This theme acts as a **neutral to slight headwind**, characterized by caution from financial conditions.

Labour Market: The labour market is in a state of neutrality with underlying softening and elevated slack. Rising unemployment, persistent underemployment, and weakening turnover dynamics contribute to an easing of tightness. This theme acts as a **headwind** to growth and inflationary pressures.

Credit Conditions: US credit conditions are generally benign and neutral, with historically tight credit spreads and stable private credit impulse. While systemic financial stress is within typical ranges, some normalisation from earlier easing is evident. This theme serves as a **neutral to supportive tailwind**.

Liquidity & Monetary Policy: Monetary conditions are net accommodative, driven by a strong liquidity impulse from the unwinding of the ON RRP facility. This expansionary force partially offsets ongoing Fed balance sheet contraction, while M2 growth and real rates remain neutral. This theme acts as a **tailwind** to asset markets.

Inflation & FX: The inflation regime is "COOL," reflecting broad disinflationary pressures. Policy-relevant PCE is neutral, and market expectations, while stable, show a short-term downside bias. Wage growth is also easing. This theme presents as a **supportive tailwind** for nominal assets and a reduced policy tightening constraint.

Positioning & Sentiment: Cross-asset positioning indicates normalizing market volatility after a stressed period, with broad systemic risk indicators subdued. However, specific sectors show concentrated speculative crowding and a dominant "Supply Stress" theme in commodities. This theme is **neutral**, with localized risks and opportunities.

Forward Balance of Risks

Upside / Supportive Conditions
  • Liquidity remains expansionary, continuing to flow into the system from ON RRP unwind.
  • Credit conditions persist in a benign state, maintaining historically tight spreads and stable credit impulse.
  • Core manufacturing momentum broadens, leading to more robust overall industrial activity and business investment.
  • Labour market indicators stabilise, alleviating hidden slack and supporting consumer sentiment.
  • Disinflationary trends allow for potential monetary policy easing, improving financial conditions.
Downside / Adverse Conditions
  • Liquidity impulse fades or reverses, potentially from increased ON RRP usage or faster balance sheet runoff.
  • Unwinding of speculative short FX positioning leads to dollar strength.
  • Labour market deterioration becomes broad-based, with underemployment signals turning "HOT" and job flows weakening.
  • Credit spreads move wider, particularly if HY momentum intensifies, signaling increased default risk or tighter funding.
  • A renewed surge in energy or commodity prices re-ignites inflation, potentially prompting a reversal in central bank policy expectations.
  • Growth signals continue to moderate, with Capex Intentions contracting and industrial production stagnating.

Investment Context (Informational)

A late-cycle moderation with disinflationary pressures typically implies a nuanced environment for asset allocation. Growth assets may benefit from easing real yields and ample liquidity, but their performance will be sensitive to the trajectory of labour market health and overall demand. Defensives could offer stability if growth deceleration becomes more pronounced. This regime is highly sensitive to shifts in liquidity impulse, especially any reversal in ON RRP dynamics, and further re-pricing of inflation expectations. Monitoring labour market dynamics and credit conditions is critical, as these are often leading indicators for late-cycle inflection points in corporate performance and broader economic health.
