Current View: The institutional outlook for silver has shifted significantly upward in early 2026, with recent forecasts establishing a new median expectation of $85.00 per ounce. This represents a substantial increase from late-2025 projections, which were centered closer to $55.00. Current sentiment is driven by a combination of persistent structural supply deficits and a strong historical beta to rising gold prices, leading several major banks to revise their base-case expectations toward the $75.00 to $100.00 range for the remainder of the year.

Recent Predictions: Forecasts issued in March 2026 underscore a bullish trend, though point targets remain somewhat dispersed. UBS recently set a mid-year target of $100.00, while ING updated its 2026 annual average to $83.00, up from an earlier estimate of $55.00. BMO Capital Markets and Fitch Solutions provide slightly more conservative but still elevated anchors at $75.00 and $93.00 respectively. This clustering suggests a solidified floor for the asset well above historical norms, even as individual analysts disagree on the exact peak.

Tactical Divergence: While annual averages are coalescing around the $80.00 mark, tactical and "bull case" targets show extreme dispersion, indicating high volatility and uncertainty. Bank of America and Citi have both highlighted the potential for price spikes reaching between $150.00 and $170.00 under specific tactical conditions, such as accelerated ETF inflows or acute liquidity tightness. In contrast, TD Securities has projected trading highs of $118.00 for the first half of 2026, illustrating that while the general direction is upward, the perceived ceiling varies wildly between institutions.

Shift in Distribution: The target distribution has moved decisively higher since the fourth quarter of 2025. Older benchmarks from institutions like HSBC ($44.50) and earlier Bank of America reports ($56.25) now serve as historical context rather than active market guidance. The upward revision of $30.00 in the median target over the last 90 days reflects a fundamental reassessment of silver's scarcity and its role in the "debasement trade," moving it from a recovery asset to a high-growth commodity in the eyes of institutional researchers.

Fundamental Drivers: The consensus view is underpinned by the Silver Institute’s report of a sixth consecutive annual market deficit and the CME Group’s observations on low supply elasticity. Analysts frequently cite the "gold-silver ratio" framing and the metal's industrial necessity in solar panels and AI infrastructure as primary justifications for these elevated targets. However, some institutions, including J.P. Morgan, note that the lack of central bank buying—a key driver for gold—may create a different floor structure for silver compared to its yellow counterpart.

Comparability and Conviction: It is important to distinguish between the various target types within this dataset to gauge conviction levels. The Reuters and LBMA survey averages, which both sit near $79.50, provide a reliable proxy for broad market consensus. These survey medians are notably more stable than the aggressive point forecasts from individual investment banks. While the overall distribution implies a strong upside bias, the wide gap between survey averages ($79.50) and tactical bull cases ($170.00) suggests that while institutions agree on the upward trajectory, they remain uncertain about the magnitude of potential price spikes.
