Gemini Summary

Signal Summary:

  • Configuration statement: Given a neutral composite of -0.17 and stable 5-year breakeven z-scores (0.00), this setup aligns with Range-biased price paths and Normal volatility, where the dominant risk is Mean reversion, not Regime shift.
  • The signal remains in a persistent Neutral regime, characterized by balanced inflation compensation and real yields.
  • Conviction Band: Low; Interpretation Confidence: Mixed Signals; Internal Conflict Flag: No. Signal Stability Assessment: Stable; Threshold Proximity: Far; Revision Sensitivity: Moderate.

Methodology Applied:

  • Regime thresholds are fixed at ±0.75; values above +0.75 indicate Reflation, while values below -0.75 indicate Disinflation (1).
  • The composite is a weighted blend: 40% 5-year breakevens (T5YIE), 30% 10-year breakevens (T10YIE), and 30% inverted 10-year real yields (DFII10) (1).
  • Robust standardisation uses a 60-month rolling median/MAD to dampen outliers and reflect relative historical extremes (1).
  • Latest data observation: 2026-02-28.

Key Dynamics:

  • The primary driver is the 10-year breakeven z-score (-0.55), which exerts a slight disinflationary drag offset by neutral 5-year expectations.
  • Momentum is stabilizing; the composite has stayed within the Neutral band for the entire 36-month provided history (1).
  • Internal tensions are low as all three components currently reside within +/- 0.6 standard deviations.
  • Conditional Invalidation: A move in the composite beyond ±0.75, likely triggered by a sharp divergence in real yields versus inflation compensation.
  • Signal Stability: High persistence; the regime has not shifted since March 2023.

Scenario Balance:

  • Base Case dominant: Continued Neutral regime as breakevens and real rates remain anchored to recent medians.
  • Upside risk: Transition to Reflation triggered by a sharp decline in real yields (DFII10) or spike in 5-year inflation compensation.
  • Downside risk: Transition to Disinflation triggered by a spike in real yields or collapsing long-term inflation expectations.

Time Horizon & Aggregation:

  • Time Horizon: Cyclical (months) because the methodology utilizes a rolling 5-year window for standardisation.
  • Aggregation Weight Hint: Medium; it provides a necessary anchor for inflation-sensitive assets but currently lacks directional extremity.

Macro Relevance:

  • Informs the Pricing and Inflation macro dimensions by blending market-implied expectations with real funding costs.

  • Cycle position: Not determined.
  • Typically interacts with liquidity signals; extreme disinflation signals often precede defensive cross-asset rotations.

Regime Context:

  • Persistent Neutral regime; the signal has occupied this state for 35+ months.
  • Direction of change: Stabilising near the lower bound of the neutral range.

Model Limitations:

  • Breakevens may reflect liquidity premia rather than pure expectations during market stress (1).
  • Monthly resampling may delay the recognition of rapid, high-frequency turning points.
