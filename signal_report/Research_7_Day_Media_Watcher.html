Executive Summary (Last 7 Days)

  • Geopolitical Risk Dominance: Escalating tensions in the Middle East, specifically threats to the Strait of Hormuz, have driven WTI Crude to long-term resistance levels while simultaneously triggering a "risk-off" environment for equities and base metals.
  • Yield and Dollar Headwinds: U.S. Treasury yields have increased in eight of the last nine sessions, with the 10-year yield reaching 4.23%. This, combined with a strengthening U.S. Dollar, has placed significant pressure on dollar-priced commodities and the precious metals complex.
  • Monetary Policy Recalibration: February CPI data and persistent shelter costs have led market participants to delay rate cut expectations, with the CME FedWatch Tool now reflecting increased sentiment for a July 2026 move.
  • Critical Minerals Shift: A bifurcated narrative has emerged in commodities; while industrial metals like Copper face short-term demand concerns from China, long-term institutional funding and offtake agreements (Lithium/Copper) are accelerating to support AI infrastructure and the "energy-nexus."
