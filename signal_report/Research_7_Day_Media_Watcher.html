Executive Summary (Last 7 Days)

  • Geopolitical Risk Supremacy: Market activity was dominated by escalating tensions in the Middle East, specifically threats to the Strait of Hormuz, which triggered a historic surge in WTI crude oil prices and heightened safe-haven demand for gold.
  • Volatility in Precious Metals: Gold and silver reached record weekly closes before experiencing sharp pullbacks driven by a strengthening U.S. Dollar and rising Treasury yields; however, dip-buying remains evident.
  • Equities Under Pressure: Broad equity indexes, led by the Nasdaq and Russell 2000, trended lower as rising energy costs and inflationary fears weighed on risk sentiment.
  • Strategic Mineral Reorientation: There is a distinct shift toward securing critical minerals for defense and national security, highlighted by U.S.-Venezuela diplomatic engagements and new domestic funding for strategic metals like antimony.
  • Mixed Economic Signals: U.S. data showed a dichotomy between stronger-than-expected private job growth (ADP) and a projected hiring cooldown in official non-farm payrolls, complicating Federal Reserve rate expectations.
